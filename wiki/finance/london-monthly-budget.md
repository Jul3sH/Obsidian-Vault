## The confusion to kill first

You keep half-remembering a "£2,000/mo UK rent" as part of the London picture. **It is not.**
- **In the London scenario you LIVE in Cecil Road** → £0 rent income, but £0 rent to pay (you own it; only the £213/mo interest-only mortgage).
- The £2,194/mo rent only exists in the **Malvern/stay** scenario, where you rent Cecil Road out and live elsewhere.
- You cannot both collect the rent and live in the house. So London's cost is **forgone rent, not a rent bill** - which is why you can never be "priced out" of London: you already own the home there.

## 3. The runway

Landing pot at departure (London-direct): **~£50,000 banked** (after pre-move HK burn ~£6,500 and moving one-offs ~£15,000; excludes unbanked £12,475 tax rebate). MPF (**£124,500**) unlocks as a lump sum on permanently leaving HK - but it is the only private pension, so spending it is spending retirement.

| Scenario | Pot | Burn | Runway |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| London lean, liquid only | £50,000 | £4,630/mo | **~11 months** |
| London lean, liquid + MPF | £174,500 | £4,630/mo | **~3.1 years** |
| London settled, liquid only | £50,000 | £5,450/mo | **~9 months** |
| London settled, liquid + MPF | £174,500 | £5,450/mo | **~2.7 years** |

A realistic search is 12-18 months. The liquid-only runway (~11 months lean) sits just below that - **London-direct forces early MPF drawdown** unless the £12,475 tax rebate lands before departure (which would push the pot to ~£62k and runway to ~13-14 months lean). Chase that rebate.

### Stress test
If living costs run **£1,500/mo over** the lean estimate → burn £6,130/mo → liquid lasts ~8 months; liquid + MPF ~24 months (~2 years). The decision remains survivable but the MPF gets eaten materially faster.

## Key Takeaways
- London lean burn (job-search, no paid help): **£4,630/mo**. Settled (employed, help + car): **£5,450/mo**.
- Itemised lean living costs: **£3,283/mo** with a real contingency line. Settled living costs: **£3,958/mo** (adds cleaner 8h/wk + babysitter 4h/wk).
- DB flat shortfall (**-£970/mo**) is already in the burn - it goes with you regardless of where you live.
- Liquid-only runway (landing pot ~£50k): **~11 months lean** - just below a normal search. London forces MPF drawdown unless the £12,475 tax rebate banks before departure.
- With MPF: **~3.1 years lean** - covers even a long search, but spends the only private pension.
- The "£2,000 UK rent" belongs to the Malvern scenario, not London - in London the cost is forgone rent, not a bill.
- The decision rests on **P(income)** - addressed by the job-market validation, not the budget.
