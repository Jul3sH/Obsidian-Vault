# UK Move - How Long Can I Survive? (three options)

> The one question: **with no income, how many months does my money last under each option?**
> Live spreadsheet (editable): [UK Move - Survival Runway (Google Sheet)](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1x9kREPSUNbrF3WXMYNoHJk4xzUno-IA7_fzrU_AHgq4/edit)
> Every number here traces to [[uk-move-financial-model]] §0, the single source of truth. Validated line-by-line by the 14 Jul [[uk-relocation-fable-consistency-review-2026-07-13|Fable review]].

## The answer

| | Stay Hong Kong | Move to London | Move to Malvern |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| **Monthly burn (no job)** | ~£7,100 | ~£4,630 | ~£2,575 |
| **Cash pot at start** | ~£79,400 | ~£50,000 | ~£55,000 |
| **Survive on cash** | **~11 months** | **~11 months** | **~21 months** |
| **Pension (MPF) available?** | **No - locked to 65** | +£124,500 | +£124,500 |
| **Total survival (cash + MPF)** | **~11 months, then nothing** | **~3.1 years** | **~5.8 years** |

## What this says in one breath

- **Stay in Hong Kong:** you last about **11 months** and then hit a wall. Your £124,500 MPF pension is **locked until 65**, so there is no fallback. Shortest survival, and no safety net.
- **Move to London:** about the same **11 months on cash**, BUT leaving HK **unlocks the £124,500 MPF**, giving ~**3.1 years** total. The catch: that is your only private pension, so the London path eats into retirement early.
- **Move to Malvern:** the cheapest to run (~£2,575/mo living at Mum's, Cecil rented out), so cash alone lasts ~**21 months**, and with the MPF behind it ~**5.8 years** - the pension is barely touched. Longest survival by far.

## Why the burns differ

- **Hong Kong is expensive to sit in** because you are still paying for the DB flat and Sophia's private school. Even after renting Cecil Road out (+£1,730/mo net), you burn ~£7,100/mo.
- **London is cheaper while job-hunting** than staying in HK: you live in Cecil Road (own home, no rent bill), Sophia is in free state school, so the two biggest HK costs vanish.
- **Malvern is cheapest** because you live at Mum's (no rent, no bills) and rent Cecil Road out for +£2,194/mo. Burn is only the honest ~£2,575 (board + a car + Sophia's interim schooling on top of the bare figure).

## Notes on the numbers (so you can trust them)

- Burns are the **search-phase** figures (no income). Once employed, depletion stops.
- London burn uses the **lean search tier (£4,630)**; the settled figure once life normalises is £5,450 (adds cleaner + babysitting + car).
- Malvern uses the **honest £2,575** (midpoint of £2,450-2,700), not the flattering £1,683 floor.
- Stay-HK pot uses **banked cash only (£79,400)**; the £12,475 tax rebate is shown as separate upside because it is not yet in the bank.
- Pots for the two moves are **landing pots** (~£50k London / ~£55k Malvern) after pre-move HK burn and moving costs; Stay-HK keeps the full liquid because there are no move costs.
- MPF is **retirement capital** - surviving "on MPF" means spending your pension, which is why London-vs-Malvern is really a question of how fast you eat it.
