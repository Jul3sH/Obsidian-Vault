# UK vs HK Cost Comparison - Codex Review 2026-07-09

## Verdict

**The current model is defensible as a decision input, but not clean enough to be trusted as a precise estimate.**

My central read, after applying the correct Band E Cecil Road council tax bill, is **London about £285-390/month cheaper before Clodagh contribution, car, and one-offs**. The honest range is roughly **London £300/month more expensive to £700/month cheaper**. If Clodagh actually pays her half of Sophia's HK school fee, the central case is roughly **cost-neutral to £20/month against London**. If Julian runs a car in London, London is probably **£300-700/month more expensive**.

So the document is **not still materially pro-move biased on the headline number**, and it is **not numerically over-corrected into pessimism**. The base arithmetic still says small London saving. But the conclusion has become rhetorically more pessimistic than the base case: "neutral-to-negative" is fair as a decision conclusion after contingencies and one-offs, but not as the base monthly delta.

The property identity problem is now resolved: the correct bill is for **48 Cecil Road, SW19 1JP, Band E**. The largest remaining weakness is house running cost: energy, maintenance, and any Wimbledon Common levy still need real bills or direct confirmation.

## Double-Counting Check

I did **not** find obvious double-counting of the DB shortfall and agent fee after the 2026-07-09 update. The problem is different: the correction bundle is not reconciled transparently to the final ~£325/month headline.

The old Fable bundle had: FX, DB shortfall, DB agent fee, DB void, council tax, energy, holidays, lumpy buffer, dental, and a Cecil-tax offset. The new model moved DB shortfall and agent fee onto the property line and reversed most of the council-tax dock. That is directionally right.

But the current document still leaves the reader unable to reproduce:

`old actuals-basis saving -> property update -> wrong Band C input -> corrected Band E input -> reduced bundle -> final ~£325`

That should be a single bridge table. Without it, the headline is audit-hostile.

## Discretionary Lines

The "hold flat unless structural reason" rule is the right anti-bias rule. It prevents the old pro-move fantasy that Julian will magically live cheaper in London.

But two lines remain weak:

- **Groceries:** London £750 versus HK £900 is plausible, but it needs receipts after arrival. It is not a decision-grade fact.
- **Holidays:** the +£50/month UK premium is not enough support for the weather-escape risk. A central +£100/month and stress +£350/month is more honest.

Dining held flat is defensible. Transport without car is defensible only if the "no car in SW19 with a teenager" assumption is explicitly chosen, not silently inherited.

## School Fee

HK$6,500/month equals £650/month at 10:1, so the line is arithmetically correct. At 10.3 it is about £631/month, not £650.

Base case should **not** assume Clodagh pays. The source docs say she has defaulted for about four years and is in crisis. A planned appeal is not cash. So the current base case, Julian pays full fee, is correct.

But the model's conclusion should separate:

- **Current cash reality:** UK school saves about £630-650/month.
- **Legal entitlement scenario:** if Clodagh pays half, the UK saving drops by about £305/month.
- **Unknown family-law effect:** moving to UK state school may change what is owed. The model flags this but cannot price it.

## The One Thing Least Supported

The model most wants to believe that the London house running cost is known.

The address and council-tax band are now known, but energy and maintenance are still not. A Band E council-tax bill does not prove the cost of heating and maintaining a house with recent repair history.

## What Survives

These pieces are solid enough:

- The both-properties method is correct. Julian owns both properties either way.
- Housing is not a big London saving. The Cecil rent forgone largely cancels the London owner-occupier advantage.
- The DB let-out shortfall is real cash drag, even though much of the outflow is principal/equity.
- Clodagh contribution should not be banked in the base case.
- The old "London is half price" story is dead.
- Cost is not a load-bearing pro-move argument. At best, London is modestly cheaper before car and one-offs.

## Reopen Condition

The pre-agreed condition is conjunctive:

1. London is not materially cheaper.
2. London job odds are not materially better.

**Leg 1 is met.** Test A no longer supports "London materially cheaper." A £285-390/month base saving on a £5-6k/month household cost base, exposed to £10-25k one-offs, Clodagh, car, and house running-cost uncertainty, is not material enough to carry the decision.

**Leg 2 is not met.** Test B does not prove an easy £150k UK outcome, but it does show a materially better UK floor: about £46-78k gross covers costs, sits below UK architect medians, and is high-probability subject mainly to over-qualified screening. For the £150k target, Test B says UK is hard and recruiter-validation-dependent. That is not the same as proving London job odds are not materially better.

Under the F-N-M-T test:

| Gate | Result |
|---|---|
| Fact | Test A cost correction is factual enough. |
| New | The full correction is new analysis, but most ingredients were knowable before lock. |
| Material | Cost leg is material, but the job-odds leg fails. |
| Table | Cost fear was already in the wobble table; this sharpens it but does not complete the reopen condition. |

**Formal reopen is not warranted.** The correct action is not to reverse the move decision. It is to close the property-identity gap and run recruiter validation on the UK floor and £150k lanes. If recruiter validation later shows the UK floor is not materially safer and the £150k route is a wash or worse, that would be a new reopen candidate. This review does not get there.

## Key Takeaways

- The current ~£325/month headline is plausible, but only as a rough midpoint, not a clean calculation.
- The model is not materially pro-move biased anymore; the rhetoric may now be mildly over-pessimistic.
- The old address discrepancy is resolved; house energy and maintenance are now the most serious remaining cost defects.
- One car, a Clodagh payment, or a normal maintenance reserve can erase the base-case London saving.
- Test A meets the cost leg of the reopen condition. Test B does not meet the job-odds leg. No formal reopen yet.
