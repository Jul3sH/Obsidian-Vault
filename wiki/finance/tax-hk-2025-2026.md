## Discrepancy: YTD Gross vs IR56F Total

The September 2025 STI payslip shows **YTD Gross from 1 April: 1,148,799.72**.
The IR56F total is **996,968**.
Difference: **151,831.72**.

This discrepancy is expected and fully explained. The YTD Gross is a payroll accounting figure that includes gross earnings before deductions and before tax exemptions. The IR56F correctly strips these out:

| Item | Amount (HKD) | Reason excluded from IR56F |
|---|---|---|
| Severance Payment (July payslip) | 75,450.00 | Employment Ordinance exempt - not taxable income |
| No Pay Leave deductions (Jun + Jul) | 76,379.01 | Reduces taxable salary (31,154.60 + 45,224.41) |
| Rounding | 2.71 | Cents truncated in IR56F |
| **Total difference** | **151,831.72** | |

**Conclusion:** The IR56F total of 996,968 is correct. The YTD Gross figure on the payslip is not the taxable income figure - it is a gross payroll running total before exemptions and deductions are applied.

## Notes

- **Severance (75,450)**: Excluded from IR56F as an Employment Ordinance Severance Payment (tax-exempt). If the EO calculation is ever questioned, confirm it is based on correct years of service and final monthly salary - any excess over the statutory EO entitlement would be taxable.
- **Prepayment Offsets**: April and May salaries were advanced as prepayments. These were recovered via deductions in the June and July payslips. They are internal payroll accounting entries and do not affect taxable income.
- **September STI in IR56F**: Paid after cessation but included by employer in the IR56F. Confirm no separate IR56G was also filed for this amount - if so, it would be double-counted.
- **EO Adjustments (Shanghai)**: Small amounts (May 109.29, Jul 59.68) for Shanghai public holiday adjustments. Included in the salary figure.
