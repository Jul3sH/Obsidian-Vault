# Malvern-Permanent Feasibility: the two new variants (14 Jul)

> **Purpose:** rule two newly-surfaced options in or out before they cloud the decision by feel rather than fact. Both put Julian's **permanent** base at Mum's in Malvern for the ~6 years until Sophia finishes school (age 55 → 61), and differ only in the income engine:
> - **Variant A - Semi-retired Malvern:** little or no earned income; live carefully off assets + Cecil Road rent.
> - **Variant B - Permanent London commute:** a London job worked 3 days/week from a Malvern base, for the full six years.
>
> **Why this exists (logged at Julian's request, 14 Jul):** Julian flagged that he is **tired right now**, and that a tired brain gravitates to whichever option *feels* easy. Variant A feels easy (semi-retirement at Mum's). Variant B is quietly forming in the back of his mind as the "responsible" compromise. **Both instincts need to be checked against numbers, not mood - that is what this file is for.** All figures derive from [[uk-move-financial-model]] §0 (the single source of truth) and [[financial-status-2026-07-07]]; **if any number here disagrees with §0, §0 wins.**

## Decision outcome (15 Jul): BOTH variants demoted to a trigger-gated fallback; primary is London-direct

> **Julian reached a decision on this the night before the TTI call.** Having pictured the permanent-Malvern-plus-commute future concretely, he ruled it out from the inside, and the low-income variant fails as a primary once its income is honestly discounted. **Neither permanent-Malvern variant is a primary option. London-direct becomes the primary UK-landing plan** (one move for Sophia, home nightly, keeps him earning, best for his social life). This sits *under* the standing TTI gate: a genuine perm HK role still trumps it; anything less → move, London-direct.
>
> - **Variant B - REJECTED on a lived-experience veto.** Not just dominated on the numbers: *"I'd be miserable travelling hours every Tuesday and Thursday and spending a couple of nights somewhere crappy for a hybrid role."* A valid, decisive reason to kill it.
> - **Variant A - NOT a primary.** Its income leg is not bankable (the AI-automation-agency / consulting / content routes are outside chances - high interest, low willingness-to-pay; see §1a). Stripped of speculative income, A is early-retirement-on-a-tight-budget that spends the pension buffer - a downshift Julian does not actually want, reached for out of tiredness + cost-fear.
> - **Both survive ONLY as a pre-registered, trigger-gated FALLBACK** - never a drift-default: *if, after landing, no London-viable income (a job or TTI-remote) has materialised by a set month AND liquid runway has fallen to a set floor → reassess permanent-Malvern as a deliberate downshift, via a fresh [[decision-journal]] entry + the reopen test, not by sliding into it.*
> - **The drift mechanism to guard (Julian's own words):** *"if I move to Mum's for a year and can't get a job in London, it could default to it."* That is the Fable-named drift trap. If London-direct is primary, the cleanest protection is to **land in London**, which forecloses the drift; if cost forces a Malvern-staged landing, the trigger + a hard move-by date must be set *before* departure.
> - **Sophia:** her enthusiasm is for *a nice school with the sports and the ski trip*, which is **transferable** to a Wimbledon school (see [[schooling-sw19-area]]) - so it argues for settling her **once** under London-direct, not for Malvern-then-Wimbledon.

## 1. Variant A - Semi-retired Malvern: IS the door open?

Model: live at Mum's, Cecil Road let (+£1,725/mo), DB flat held & let, Sophia at free state school. Draw the ~£179.5k spendable pot to cover the gap. **ISAs never touched.** Three income cases over the 6-year window:

| Case | Earned income (net) | Monthly pot drain | 6-yr drain | Pot left after 6 yrs (from £179.5k) | Verdict |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **A0** | £0 | ~£2,575 | ~£185,400 | **~ −£5,900** (exhausts ~month 70, then a small ISA nibble) | **Marginal** |
| **A1** | ~£1,000/mo (occasional interesting remote work, ~£15-20k/yr) | ~£1,575 | ~£113,400 | **~ +£66,100** | **Sustainable** |
| **A2** | ~£2,575/mo (covers burn, ~£40k/yr part-time) | ~£0 | ~£0 | **~£179,500 (intact)** | **Fully sustainable** |

**Verdict: the door is OPEN, but it is a real downshift, not a free lunch.**

- **At literally zero income (A0) it is *tight but not catastrophic*.** Six years consumes essentially the entire liquid + MPF pot and starts nibbling the ISA backstop right at the end. Even then Julian is not destitute: Cecil rent, the £326.6k ISAs, and the state pension from 67 catch him. But A0 leaves no margin for a shock (a market fall in the ISAs, a void period on Cecil, a health cost).
- **A modest ~£1,000/mo (A1) *would* make it comfortably safe** (pot lasts ~9-10 years, ISAs untouched, post-Sophia Cecil rent carries a solo life) - **BUT this income is NOT bankable** (§1a). It is an upside case, not the planning case.
- **Covering the burn (A2) preserves everything** - but that is part-time work, not "semi-retired," and rests on the same unreliable income.

### 1a. The income reality (corrected 15 Jul) - A0 is the honest base case

Julian's own read, which I back: the routes that would fund Variant A - a bit of consulting, online content, an AI-automation agency - are **outside chances, high effort, low pay**. The 2026 market truth he named is correct and structural, not his contact's bad luck: *lots of interest in AI, very little willingness to pay for operational-efficiency work* (bespoke delivery, education-heavy sales, price-sensitive buyers who can't evaluate it, poor retention). Cold-start content is even slower to monetise.

**So the planning assumption for Variant A must be ~£0 earned income (A0), with A1 as upside, not the reverse.** And A0 is the *marginal* case: it consumes essentially the entire ~£179.5k liquid + MPF pot over the six years and nibbles the ISA backstop at the end. Julian is not left destitute (Cecil equity, ISAs, and the state pension from 67 catch him), but **A0 spends his financial independence** - by his late 60s he is reliant on the state pension, one rental property, and a depleted ISA. The "easy life" then quietly becomes a **permanently money-vigilant life** - careful, can't-spend, no margin for a shock. Ease and constant financial vigilance are in tension, which is why A fails as a *primary* plan.

**The one income source that is NOT a hustle: TTI-remote.** If TTI lands as a remote engagement deliverable from a UK base (Julian's 4 Jul message already framed it as US-hours-friendly), *that* is the realistic income leg that turns a Malvern life from a slow drawdown into a funded one - Sophia present, no London commute, no speculative agency grind. So tonight's call is also relevant here: even a "six months, no perm" outcome could be the income that makes a settled UK life viable, not only a transition bridge. But it is unlanded and a 6-month engagement is not durable income, so it does not rescue A as a *primary* plan - it only strengthens the fallback.

**The honest costs the arithmetic hides:**
1. **It spends the MPF - his only private pension.** After six years of living on it, retirement rests entirely on ISAs + Cecil equity + state pension. Adequate for a modest life; **zero margin for error.**
2. **It is close to a one-way door on his career.** Stepping out at 55 and re-entering at 61 is very hard. Choosing A is largely choosing to be done with corporate earning.
3. **The DB flat leaks ~£970/mo cash.** Selling it (even at a loss, banking the recovery bet) would materially extend the runway - a lever to weigh if A is chosen.
4. **ISA sequencing risk** - they are invested; a crash in the year he finally needs them hurts most.

**So: A is affordable. That is not the same as costless.** It converts a ~£1.2M net worth into a careful, largely-fixed-income life and spends the pension buffer. **That is a legitimate choice for a 55-year-old who wants ease and time with his daughter - but it must be *chosen*, clear-eyed, not backed into because he is tired this week.** The pull toward A is real and the numbers permit it; the warning is that "the numbers permit it" is exactly what a tired mind wants to hear.

## 2. Variant B - Permanent London commute: the "worst of everything"

Model: live at Mum's, Cecil let (+£1,725/mo, now taxed higher as an earner), commute to a London job 3 days/week, crash-pad those nights, for six years. Burn = Malvern resident (~£2,575) + crash-pad (~£1,083, likely £1,200 at real fares) ≈ **~£3,750/mo**.

### 2b. What that ~£170-220k buys (the human cost)

- **Three nights a week away from Sophia for her *entire* adolescence, age 12 to 18.** Fable rated this the *primary kill-shot* for a **one-year** interim; Variant B commits to it for **six years**. It is her whole remaining childhood.
- **The 50/50 compounding:** if Sophia is half-time with her mother *and* Julian is away 3 nights in his half, his actual nights with her get thin fast. Map it concretely before trusting "4 days is plenty."
- **A 2.5-3 hour each-way commute, 3 days a week, for six years.** The literal opposite of the "easy life" pulling him toward Malvern.
- **The worst option for the social / love life** that was his *original* gut driver: rootless - no real home in London to build a relationship from, and absent from Malvern when he is there. Neither a city life nor a settled one.

### 2c. Verdict: DOMINATED. Log this plainly (Julian, 14 Jul).

**Variant B wins only the financial axis, by ~£170-220k over six years (corrected 17 Jul from the original ~£90k wiki-estimate figure; see §2a) - a sum that is meaningful but not decisive against a £1.2M net worth with £326.6k of ISAs - while losing or near-losing every human axis.** It is the false-economy middle:

- If the goal is **to earn and have a life** → **London-direct** gets ~80% of the financial benefit with a home, a city, and Sophia present nightly. B is strictly worse.
- If the goal is **ease and time with Sophia** → **Variant A / semi-retirement** delivers it. B is the opposite.

**B is the option a tired, money-guilty mind rationalises into ("I'll just commute, it's sensible") precisely because it *sounds* responsible. It is not. It pays a modest premium to see Sophia less, commute brutally, and forgo a love life.** Julian's own read - "it's the worst of everything" - is correct and is now on the record.

And a *real* TTI role is the one thing that squares the most circles at once - which is why the bar for calling it "real" tomorrow must be high.

## 4. Recommendation

1. **Variant B: rule OUT.** Dominated by London-direct (for earning) and by semi-retirement (for ease). Keep only as a theoretical fallback if a London job somehow required a Malvern base, which London-direct-with-Sophia-in-London already solves.
2. **Variant A: rule IN as feasible, but re-labelled honestly.** It is affordable *with even ~£1k/mo of income*; marginal at literally zero. It is a **permanent downshift that spends the pension buffer**, not a temporary safety net. Decide it as a life choice about the next chapter, not as a tired default. If chosen, two levers materially de-risk it: a modest income stream, and revisiting whether to hold the loss-making DB flat.
3. **Do not decide either tonight.** Tomorrow's TTI call splits the top of the tree: a genuine perm HK role changes everything; anything less returns the choice to **London-direct vs Variant A** - a clean either/or between a city-and-earning life and a settled-and-easy one.
