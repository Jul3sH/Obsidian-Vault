### Verdict

Mixed picture, with a meaningful structural gap.

The resume is a very strong match on the "how" of this role: solution architecture, presales, RFP leadership, stakeholder management, solution narratives/proposals, and multi-module solutioning across enterprise clients are all well-evidenced, and 20+ years comfortably exceeds the 3-7 year band.

But 3 of 7 Core requirements are missing, and they're not phrasing issues, they define the domain of the job: Psychometrics, SaaS, and HR Tech / Talent Tech. The entire evidenced domain is telecom/network/cybersecurity managed services for enterprise/BFSI clients. None of that experience touches psychometric assessments, talent lifecycle products, or HR systems (ATS/HCM/LMS).

**Missing Core requirements (3): Psychometric assessments, SaaS/multi-module platforms, HR Tech/Talent Tech**
- Psychometric assessments / Psychometrics: Genuinely missing. No reframe available, this is domain knowledge with no current evidence.
- SaaS / multi-module SaaS platforms: Partial reframe possible. The SASE blueprint work (SD-WAN + SSE + ZTA + NGFW as a "single vendor" multi-module security platform) is conceptually similar to "multi-module platform architecture", but it's managed network services, not SaaS, so an ATS keyword scan won't credit it.
- HR Tech / Talent Tech: Genuinely missing. "Talent management" in the resume refers to managing Julian's own teams (attract/develop/retain), not an HR-tech product domain, different context, won't read as a match.

**Missing High requirements worth noting:**
- Cross-functional collaboration, ROI/value proposition, Discovery engagements: all reframeable, strong underlying evidence exists (collaboration with Sales/Product/Engineering, extensive $ outcome quantification, full presales discovery-to-close lifecycle), just not phrased in the JD's exact language.
- Master's degree, Cantonese, Talent lifecycle, HR ecosystems: genuinely missing/not evidenced.

### Recommendation

Borderline, proceed only if there is a route into the domain gap (e.g. a related project, training, or a strong narrative for why the enterprise architecture methodology transfers to HR tech/psychometrics). On a pure ATS keyword pass, the 3 missing Core domain terms (Psychometrics, SaaS, HR Tech/Talent Tech) are likely to hold this resume back regardless of how well the High-tier language is tailored. A tailored resume would need to aggressively reframe the SASE "multi-module platform" angle and lean on the presales/RFP/stakeholder strengths, but the domain gap is real, not cosmetic.
