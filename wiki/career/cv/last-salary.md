## Note - voluntary MPF (the "10%" doesn't reconcile)

**Working annual figure: HK$131,542** (= payslip 10,961.84/month × 12). Use this until/unless the MPF statement gives an exact annual total.

Sanity check (16 Jun): the assumption that voluntary MPF is **10% of base does not hold**:
- 10,961.84/month is only **~7.0% of monthly base** (155,772.98), not 10%.
- A true 10% would be ~15,577/month (~186,927/year).
- 131,542 is **not** 10% of any comp figure (10% of base = 186,927; 10% of 1,989,275 = 198,928).

So `131,542` is the best **payslip-derived** estimate, **not** a rule-based figure. **For an exact annual total, pull it from the MPF contribution statement.** (Julian's earlier verbal total of 1,989,275 implied ~120k/yr and is superseded by the payslip-derived 131,542.)

## How to use this (disclosure note)

- The **voluntary MPF is optional to include** - it is additional employer pension contribution. Including it makes the headline number higher.
- **For CLSA specifically:** Julian's last salary already sits above HR's band, so he may *not* want to include voluntary MPF (it makes "too expensive" worse). Lead with the **base** figure there.
- Where a higher headline helps, include it.
