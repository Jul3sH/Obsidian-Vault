# Cleared Defence-Cyber Lane: Achievability Assessment

> **Question answered.** Is the UK **cleared (SC/DV) defence-cyber architecture** lane an achievable target for Julian on his current resume, and how much additional work would it take? Short answer: **the capability fit is strong, but the clearance gate is the binding constraint** - he is not near-term clearable for the premium roles because of a ~13-year overseas (Hong Kong) residency gap. It is a **medium-term build via a permanent, clearance-sponsored role**, not a landing spot on arrival. This weakens the "Malvern-first via cleared contract" hedge in the [[uk-job-market-remote-hybrid-split-2026-07-11|remote/hybrid report]].

## Key Takeaways

- **The bottleneck is clearance eligibility, not capability.** Julian's CCIE + CISSP + TOGAF + SASE/ZTA/NGFW + data-centre stack is a near-perfect match for the cleared architect titles. If he were already cleared he would be a strong, even premium, candidate.
- **You cannot self-apply for clearance.** It must be **sponsored** by an employer holding a contract that requires it.
- **Residency rule is the main gate.** SC normally wants **5 years' UK residency**, DV **10**. Julian left the UK ~2013 (~13 years in Hong Kong), so **DV is effectively out of reach** this horizon and **SC's clock effectively restarts on his return** - though residency is *"not necessarily a bar"* (GOV.UK): a waiver is possible but sponsor-dependent and slower given the HK years. The 13-year China-adjacent gap makes vetting slower and more scrutinised (foreign connections, financial ties).
- **The premium market wants people cleared *now*.** ~All high-paying titles state "DV Cleared" / "SC Cleared" as a *requirement at hire*. Contract roles cannot wait 6-18 months for vetting, so the £150k+ day-rate lane is closed to anyone not already cleared.
- **Realistic entry = a *permanent* role at a defence prime that sponsors SC** (£70-95k initially, below the £150k target but clearing the ~£77k floor and carrying benefits). Clearance then appreciates as an asset over ~5 years toward the premium lane.
- **Geography is tier-dependent (corrected 11 Jul per [[fable-review-jobdata-2026-07-11]]).** The premium DV *contracts* cluster at **Basingstoke/Milton Keynes** (London-side) - but that tier is ~10yr out of reach. The tier Julian can actually enter, **sponsored-SC permanent (£60-115k + benefits)**, clusters at **Cheltenham/Gloucester/Worcester - Malvern-commutable.** So the *accessible* tier leans **Malvern**, not London; only the *inaccessible* premium tier leans London. (Based on n≈37 cleared-in-title listings from a one-day snapshot - directional.)
- **"Clearable" needs a caveat wherever it appears:** clearable long-term via a permanent-sponsored path; not near-term for premium DV contract work.

Two structural facts: the **premium is in DV contract** (Basingstoke/MK, London-side), and the **Malvern/Cheltenham roles are the lower-paid permanent end**. So even a successful cleared pivot does not favour a Malvern base over London.

## The clearance gate, in detail

1. **Sponsorship-only.** No individual can buy or self-apply for SC/DV. An employer with a qualifying contract sponsors it. Permanent employers invest in clearing staff; contract clients hire pre-cleared.
2. **Residency thresholds:** SC ~5 years UK residency; DV ~10 years. Lack of residency is "not *necessarily* a bar" (GOV.UK), but it makes vetting slow, uncertain, and sponsor-dependent, and requires overseas checks for the years abroad.
3. **Levels, cheapest to hardest:**
   - **BPSS** (baseline): right-to-work + ID + basic checks, **no residency bar** - achievable immediately, but gates only low-sensitivity, lower-paid roles.
   - **SC:** the workhorse cleared level; gated on ~5yr residency; **restarts on return** for Julian.
   - **DV / eDV** (the £150k+ contracts and GCHQ Cheltenham roles): ~10yr residency; **effectively out of reach** this horizon.

## Julian's eligibility

| Clearance | Nominal residency | Julian's position |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **BPSS** | none | Achievable immediately (low-value roles only) |
| **SC** | ~5 years UK | Not near-term; clock restarts on return; waiver possible but uncertain; HK period needs overseas checks |
| **DV / eDV** | ~10 years UK | Effectively out of reach within the decision horizon |

**The Hong Kong factor.** ~13 years in a China-adjacent jurisdiction, plus HK property, MPF, and any HK-based relationship, sit squarely in the "foreign connections / financial vulnerability" areas vetting scrutinises hardest. Not disqualifying, but a real friction that slows and de-risks the sponsor's decision to back him.

## Resume fit for the work itself

**Strong.** Direct-match titles: Network Security Architect, Infrastructure Security Architect, Enterprise Security Architect, Technical Architect. His stack maps almost perfectly.

**Gaps (learnable, not structural):** no defence/government-sector experience (background is telecoms/BFSI); no NCSC / MOD framework exposure (JSP440, Defence Digital, gov-flavoured NIST/ISO). Weeks of familiarisation, not years.

## Verdict: achievable?

- **Near-term, as the high-day-rate DV contract lane: No.** Residency + sponsorship + already-cleared market lock him out for years.
- **Medium-term (3-6+ years), via a permanent clearance-sponsored path: Yes** - and that path fits his stated preference (permanent + benefits + management) better than contract does.

## The realistic path, and "how much work"

The work is mostly **time and one strategic acceptance**, not study or certification:

1. **On arrival, target a *permanent* role at a defence-adjacent prime** (CGI, Leidos, BAE, Capgemini, Methods, Fujitsu, IBM/gov) that **sponsors SC**. These are the £70-95k perm roles in the data.
2. **Accept a below-target salary as the on-ramp** (~£70-95k vs the £150k stretch). It clears the ~£77k cost-covering floor and carries the medical/life benefits he needs.
3. **Let residency + clearance mature** (~5 years). Live SC + a UK track record then opens the premium cleared work at £150k+.
4. **Marginal skill top-ups** (weeks): NCSC guidance, JSP440, MOD/Defence Digital patterns - interview polish, not the gate.

## Impact on the relocation decision

- **Tier-dependent, not a clean London win (corrected 11 Jul).** The premium DV *contract* tier (Basingstoke/MK, London-side) is ~10yr out of reach; the tier Julian can actually enter (sponsored-SC permanent) clusters **Malvern-commutable** (Cheltenham/Gloucester/Worcester). So the accessible cleared tier is a *Malvern* asset, not a London one - which, combined with the crash-pad interim, means the cleared lane does **not** reinforce London-direct as I first wrote. It is a possible Malvern-side long-game.
- **Corrects the "clearable" shorthand.** Where the [[uk-job-market-remote-hybrid-split-2026-07-11|report]] and [[target-role-profile]] say "clearable," read it as *clearable long-term via a permanent-sponsored path, not near-term for premium DV contract work.*
- **Offers a coherent long-game if wanted:** a permanent defence-prime role is a floor-clearing, benefit-bearing entry that builds a clearance asset over time - patient, not a quick win.
