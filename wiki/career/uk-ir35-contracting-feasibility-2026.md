# UK IR35 Contracting Feasibility 2026

## Key Takeaways

- Do not treat outside IR35 as the default. For medium and large private-sector clients, the client usually determines status.
- Inside IR35 means the contractor is taxed broadly like an employee for income tax and NI, but without normal employee benefits unless the umbrella or agency separately provides them.
- For Julian, inside IR35 at £700-775/day covers London costs but does not beat a £150k permanent package after benefits.
- Outside IR35 can be economically attractive, but only where the real working practices support self-employment: project deliverables, autonomy, substitution, financial risk, and low integration into client BAU.
- The likely outside-IR35 routes are short, specialist advisory or delivery projects. The likely inside-IR35 routes are bank or enterprise staff-augmentation architecture roles.
- The percentage of suitable roles that are outside IR35 is not reliably measurable from public desk research. Job-board sampling and recruiter calls are required.

## What IR35 changes

IR35 asks whether the worker would be an employee if engaged directly rather than through an intermediary such as a personal service company.

If the engagement is inside IR35, the fee payer must usually deduct income tax and employee NI, and employer NI sits in the labour-cost stack. The result is intended to be broadly similar to employee taxation.

If the engagement is outside IR35, the contractor can operate through a company and take profits through a mix of salary, dividends, pension contributions, and expenses, subject to normal company tax rules.

## Why this matters for the £150k question

The headline annualised turnover can mislead:

| Day rate | Days | Assignment value |
|---:|---:|---:|
| £700 | 220 | £154k |
| £775 | 220 | £170.5k |
| £775 | 240 | £186k |

Those numbers look like they clear the £150k target. Inside IR35, the net cash is much less compelling:

| Inside-IR35 umbrella assumption | Estimated taxable pay | Estimated net cash |
|---|---:|---:|
| £700/day x 220 | ~£132.9k | ~£82.2k |
| £775/day x 220 | ~£147.1k | ~£89.8k |
| £775/day x 240 | ~£160.6k | ~£96.9k |
| £850/day x 220 | ~£161.4k | ~£97.3k |

Comparison point: £150k permanent salary is about £91.3k net cash before employee pension contributions, using 2026/27 PAYE and NI rates.

So £775/day inside IR35 at 220 days is roughly cash-equivalent to £150k permanent, but worse after pension, medical, life cover, sick pay, income protection, and renewal risk.

## Calculation assumptions

The table above assumes:

- England PAYE tax, 2026/27.
- Employee NI category A.
- Inside-IR35 advertised day rate is an umbrella assignment rate.
- Umbrella margin modelled at £1,300/yr.
- Employer NI at 15 percent and apprenticeship levy at 0.5 percent reduce the assignment-rate pot before taxable pay.
- No employee pension salary sacrifice.

Important caveat: some adverts quote "PAYE rate" differently from umbrella assignment rate. Julian must ask every recruiter: "Is this the assignment rate before employer NI and umbrella costs, or the taxable PAYE rate?"

## What makes outside IR35 plausible?

Outside IR35 is more plausible when the engagement looks like a genuine business-to-business service:

| Factor | Outside-leaning pattern |
|---|---|
| Control | Julian decides how the work is done, within agreed outcomes. |
| Substitution | Contract allows a genuine substitute, and the client would accept one in practice. |
| Mutuality | No expectation of ongoing work after the project deliverable. |
| Financial risk | Fixed deliverables, rework risk, own tools, own insurance, multiple clients. |
| Integration | Not treated like staff, not in org chart, no employee benefits, no BAU line role. |
| Deliverable | Specific architecture assessment, migration plan, SASE design, assurance review, or recovery programme. |

Outside IR35 is less plausible when the role is full-time BAU staff augmentation:

- "Join the architecture team for 12 months."
- "Report to the Head of Architecture."
- "Own the roadmap as one of our internal architects."
- "Hybrid 3 days in office, same controls as staff."
- "Backfill a permanent vacancy."

## Julian-specific realism

Likely better outside-IR35 candidates:

- Short SASE / Zero Trust architecture assessment.
- Network-security transformation blueprint.
- Vendor-selection and assurance engagement.
- Interim architecture recovery for a defined programme.
- Independent consulting via a specialist SI where outcomes are deliverable-based.

Likely inside-IR35 candidates:

- Bank in-house architecture role.
- Long-running transformation programme staffed like an internal employee.
- Enterprise architecture seat embedded in a governance function.
- Contract-to-perm role.
- Any role where the client has a blanket cautious policy.

This matters because Julian's highest-paying contract-looking opportunities may cluster in the large-enterprise and BFSI world, exactly where clients are more cautious.

## Trend and percentage of inside versus outside

I would not trust a precise percentage from desk research.

What can be said safely:

- GOV.UK confirms the client is responsible for status determinations in most medium and large private-sector cases.
- FT reporting on IPSE's 2024 survey described significant contractor rejection of inside-IR35 work and concern that IR35 reduced labour-market participation.
- Secondary summaries of IPSE's 2025 IR35 Spotlight suggest outside-IR35 engagement share fell versus 2023-2024, but I did not verify the primary report directly.

Decision implication: treat outside IR35 as an opportunity filter, not a planning base case. The plan should work on inside-IR35 economics. Outside IR35 is upside.

## Recruiter questions

Ask every contract recruiter:

1. Is the advertised rate inside or outside IR35?
2. If inside, is the rate the umbrella assignment rate or taxable PAYE rate?
3. Who made the status determination?
4. Is there a status determination statement?
5. Is this staff augmentation or a defined deliverable?
6. Is substitution contractually allowed and practically accepted?
7. Is the client using blanket determinations?
8. Are there current contractors in similar roles outside IR35?
9. What is the expected contract length and renewal pattern?
10. Does the client permit PSC engagement at all?

## Bottom Line

For relocation modelling, use inside-IR35 economics as the conservative contract case. Outside IR35 can improve the contract route, but it is not reliable enough to carry the £150k thesis without live validation.

For Julian specifically, permanent remains the cleaner economic route because the benefits package is not generic. It protects against medical, life-cover, and daughter-dependency risks that contractor cash only weakly replaces.
