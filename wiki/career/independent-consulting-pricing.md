# Independent Consulting - Pricing & Rates (HK)

> How to price independent consulting work as a Hong Kong-based solo operator. Reusable across any engagement, not TTI-specific. Live benchmark figures come from [[last-salary]]; the current live application is the [[tti-engagement-strategy|TTI engagement]].

## The benchmark: employee-equivalent total cash

- Anchor the premium on **total cash compensation** (base + target bonus), not base alone and not the benefits-inclusive package.
- Current benchmark (last role, Telstra): **HK$2,082,631/year** (HK$173,553/month) = base HK$1,869,275 + 12.5% bonus HK$213,356. See [[last-salary]].
- Apply the premium to total cash because the premium is what *replaces* lost benefits; applying it to a benefits-inclusive figure double-counts them.

## The conversational principle (what you say out loud)

Lead the conversation with a principle, not a number pulled from the air. The cleanest, hardest-to-argue version:

> *"I take my last base salary, including the pension contribution my employer made, and add around 25% to cover the bonus I no longer earn, health and life insurance, and the MPF I now fund myself."*

- Base (final run-rate) HK$1,869,275 + employer voluntary MPF HK$131,542 = HK$2,000,817. Add 25% = **~HK$2.5M/year (~HK$208k/month)**. See [[last-salary]].
- **This is your floor logic, not your opening.** It is deliberately conservative (it starts from base, not total cash, and adds only 25%), which is exactly what makes it sound reasonable and unarguable in conversation.
- **Do not let HK$2.5M become the anchor.** State the principle so the floor sounds fair, then anchor and settle higher (see bands below). The principle justifies the floor; it does not set the price.

## What the premium pays for (the justification stack)

Two layers. Lead a negotiation with the hard costs - they are documentable.

**Hard replacement costs (annual, must now self-fund):**

| Item | Why it's lost | Est. HK$/yr |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Employer MPF (mandatory HK$18k + any voluntary) | No employer pension contribution | ~150,000 |
| Medical insurance (family, private) | No employer plan | ~40,000* |
| Life insurance | No employer cover | ~20,000* |
| Business overhead (accounting, company admin, professional indemnity) | Cost of operating through own company | ~40,000* |
| **Subtotal** | | **~250,000 (~+12% on total cash)** |

*Estimates - replace with real quotes when priced. The MPF line is the largest and most documentable.

**Risk and profit (justifies the rest):**
- No severance, long-service payment, or statutory employment protection.
- No job security beyond term; utilisation risk and business-development time between engagements.
- Profit margin - it is a business, not a salary.

## The premium bands

| Position | Multiple on total cash | Use when |
|----------|------------------------|----------|
| **Floor** (don't go below) | 1.25x (+25%) | Quasi-employment minimum; below this you're worse off than a job |
| **Target** | 1.35x (+35%) | Near-full-time, low-risk fixed contract |
| **Anchor** (open here) | 1.5x | Specialist consulting rate |
| Stretch | 1.75-2.0x | Heavy scope, or true independent advisory with gaps |

Rule of thumb: 25-35% premium for a quasi-full-time contract that replaces employment; 1.5-2.0x for true specialist consulting with downtime, admin, BD, and commercial risk.

## Two billing models (and the holiday logic)

**Monthly retainer (preferred for embedded, near-full-time):**
- Paid flat each month regardless of days worked - take normal leave and stay paid.
- Do **not** add a separate "no holidays" premium; you're paid through leave, so it would double-count.
- Premium justified by MPF + insurance + severance/security + overhead + profit.
- More senior framing, predictable income, no timesheet scrutiny, judged on outcomes.
- Neutralise scope-creep risk by stating expected commitment ("broadly full-time, normal leave") plus defined scope in the SOW.

**Day rate (fallback - staff-aug style, or if procurement insists):**
- Bill only days worked; leave is unpaid.
- "No holidays" is handled by the **divisor, not the multiplier**: divide the annual target by ~**220 billable days** (≈260 working days − ~20 annual leave − ~15 HK statutory holidays − sick buffer), not 260. That gross-up *is* the holiday premium.
- Then apply the same benefit/risk multiplier on top.
- Downside: caps upside at days billed, invites micromanagement, junior framing. Don't volunteer it.

The two models reconcile at ~220 billable days (same annual outcome).

## HK classification (the real rule)

- The risk is not your quote, it is worker classification. If the reality looks like employment (fixed hours, close supervision, integrated role, no business independence), the "consultant" label does not by itself prevent employment-related issues, and you still miss statutory benefits.
- Protect with a clear SOW: scope, deliverables, payment basis, notice, IP/confidentiality, expenses, and control over *how* the work is performed.
- Quote by retainer, day rate, or project fee - not a mirror of a payroll salary.

## Pricing posture

- **Say the principle, anchor above it.** Lead with the base+vMPF+25% principle (~HK$2.5M) as floor *justification* - it sounds fair and is unarguable. Then open the negotiation at the anchor (1.5x on total cash, ~HK$3.12M) and expect to settle at target (~HK$2.81M).
- **Floor: ~HK$2.5M** (the principle). Don't go below the number your own stated logic produces.
- The principle is what you *say*; the bands are where you *aim*. Never let the conservative principle become the opening figure.
- Keep the fee **off** any leave-behind or briefing that travels to procurement or third parties - it anchors low and invites scrutiny. Disclose verbally or in the SOW.
- "Nominal to the client" and "excellent for you" coexist at enterprise scale: a fee that is trivial on a large P&L can still be strong personal income.

## Key Takeaways

- **Conversational principle (what you say): "last base + employer pension, plus 25%" = ~HK$2.5M/yr.** This is the floor logic, deliberately conservative so it's unarguable. Say it; don't let it become the anchor.
- Anchor on total cash comp, not base and not the benefits-inclusive package.
- Hard replacement costs (~HK$250k/yr, MPF-dominated) justify ~+12%; risk and profit take it to +25-35% (retainer) or 1.5-2.0x (specialist).
- "No holidays" applies only to day-rate billing, via the /220 divisor - never double-count it on a retainer.
- Prefer a monthly retainer with defined scope; keep the day rate as a fallback and don't volunteer it.
- The real legal risk is HK worker classification - defend it with a proper SOW.
