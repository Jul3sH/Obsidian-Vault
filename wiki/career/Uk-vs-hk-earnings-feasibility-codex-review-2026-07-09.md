## Source-of-Truth Conflicts

1. **HK PR is now confirmed, but [[uk-vs-hk-earning-comparison]] still treats it as open.** Lines 66-72 and line 88 should be updated. Correct wording: "HK PR confirmed. Localisation remains a hiring preference, not a work-permit hurdle."
2. **Comparison line 62 says "certain right-to-work" as a UK win.** That remains true for the UK, but it is no longer an asymmetry against HK if HK PR is confirmed. It should be reframed as "UK right-to-work certainty plus relocated UK address."
3. **Cost comparison dependency is too compressed.** The comparison correctly says costs are roughly a wash, but "cost-covering floor easier in UK" could be misread as a cost-side win. Test A says the cost saving is only about £300-390/mo central, fragile, and mostly school-fee contingent.
4. **Robert Half exact salary figures are not preserved as primary evidence in the wiki.** The report cites them, but the raw working files are outside the wiki. For a real relocation decision, retain screenshots or PDF exports of the Robert Half calculator outputs.

## Decision Safety

**Safe to rely on for the relocation decision, with corrections.**

Use these two reports for the strategic conclusion:

- The UK is safer for a cost-covering income floor.
- HK is economically better if a large role lands.
- The £150k target is not a UK base case.
- Contract should not be the primary UK strategy because benefits matter unusually heavily here.
- Income alone does not justify the move or the stay.

Do not use them as a precise probability model. The following still needs primary validation:

1. Recruiter validation of UK £150k principal presales OTE and top-of-band EA/Head-of routes.
2. Recruiter validation of rare-stack supply depth: CCIE + CISSP + SASE + presales + leadership.
3. A live scrape of commutable London/West-Corridor roles by lane.
4. A 15-20 JD sample for BFSI "within financial services" vs "employed by a bank" gates.
5. Preserved Robert Half salary calculator outputs for UK EA London and HK EA.
6. Exact monetary value of the unbuyable medical/life cover gap.
