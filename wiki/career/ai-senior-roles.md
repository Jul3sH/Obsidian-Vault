# AI Senior Roles - Market Analysis and Career Positioning

> Reference article capturing the role landscape considered during the June 2026 career positioning review. Records the decision rationale for targeting **GenAI Solution Architect** and the pivot away from Forward Deployed Engineer. Use this before applying, rewriting the CV, or reframing in interviews.

---

## Roles Considered

### 1. Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) / Forward Deployed AI Engineer

**What it is:** Customer-embedded technical delivery role. The engineer owns the full cycle - discovery, scoping, system design, coding, integration, rollout, and adoption - inside the customer's environment. Sits between product engineering, solutions architecture, and implementation consulting, but with direct responsibility for shipping working systems.

**Representative employers:** OpenAI, Palantir. Market signal is highest at frontier-model and enterprise AI deployment companies.

**Typical profile:** Strong software or systems engineer (5+ years production engineering) with customer-facing delivery experience. Palantir explicitly requires prior LLM solution-building and strong engineering foundation. OpenAI asks for production-grade coding across frontend and backend.

**Compensation signal:** US$135k–$200k base (Palantir public postings), excluding equity. High, but directly tied to engineering depth.

**Key skills:** Production coding (Python, JavaScript, Java, C++), LLM/GenAI implementation, systems integration, APIs, data pipelines, customer communication and discovery.

**Why Julian does not target this:**
- **FDE is builder-first.** The coding requirement is not peripheral - it is the baseline, not a differentiator. Competing without a production engineering pedigree means entering at a disadvantage on the core selection criterion.
- **Architecture capital is undervalued.** 20+ years of enterprise architecture, presales, and delivery leadership counts for little when the screen is "can you own production code paths under high ambiguity."
- **Better roles exist.** The adjacent cluster (below) monetises Julian's actual capital more directly.

**Note:** The FDE framing remains valid for the TTI bespoke consulting engagement, where Stephan is not screening against a JD and the "embedded practitioner delivering outcomes" description is exactly right. See [[fde-conversation-piece]].

### 2. Agentic AI Engineer

**What it is:** Core-systems builder role for orchestration, memory, RAG pipelines, evals, multi-agent coordination, and runtime reliability. Skews closest to a principal software engineer or platform architect specialising in agent infrastructure.

**Typical profile:** Deep individual-contributor systems craft. Strong coding pedigree is assumed, with AI/ML specialisation on top.

**Why Julian does not target this:**
- Even more engineering-heavy than FDE.
- 20+ years of leadership, presales, and delivery capital counts for almost nothing at entry.
- This is the "agentic engineer" path the June 2026 positioning review explicitly rejected. See [[genai-sa-pivot-strategy]] for the full rationale.

---

### 3. GenAI Solution Architect ← **CHOSEN TARGET**

**What it is:** Enterprise architecture role centred on identifying use cases, shaping the GenAI roadmap, designing secure and scalable solutions, integrating with enterprise systems, and aligning business, data, engineering, and governance stakeholders. The mainstream enterprise title in this cluster.

**Representative employers:** Capgemini, Wipro, Accenture, IBM, and most large enterprise AI consultancies.

**Typical profile:** Senior architect background (8–15+ years in enterprise solutioning) with GenAI depth layered in. Coding bar is **fluency** - comfortable with Python, APIs, cloud patterns, RAG/LLM architecture, vendor evaluation - not production engineering ownership.

**Key skills (from current postings):**
- Enterprise solution architecture across cloud, APIs, data, and business applications
- Hands-on understanding of LLMs and GenAI application patterns (RAG, intelligent assistants, summarisation, search)
- Stakeholder engagement and translation of business problems into architecture and delivery plans
- Governance, security, risk, and responsible AI controls
- Python or adjacent technical fluency

**Why this is the right target:**

| Julian's capital | GenAI SA requirement |
|---|---|
| 20+ years enterprise solution architecture (TOGAF) | Senior architect background - direct fit |
| US$300M+ transformation delivered | "Has turned enterprise use cases into operational solutions" - the key differentiator |
| CISSP/GRC | Governance, security, risk, responsible AI controls |
| CTBME/FinOps | Cost management and architectural governance |
| AI OS built during sabbatical (agentic workflows, tool-using agents) | Hands-on LLM/agentic implementation - proof of pattern |
| 15+ years BFSI vertical | Enterprise domain credibility |
| Presales (US$170M+ generated) | Stakeholder engagement, business translation |

The role is "architecture-and-value-first" - exactly the profile Julian has. The only targeted upskilling needed is deepening LLM/RAG patterns and building publishable proof of work.

### 4. Agentic AI Solution Architect (Evolution Path)

**What it is:** Emerging variant of the GenAI SA role with more explicit build-and-implement orientation around agents, LangChain-style orchestration, LLM integration into business processes, and end-to-end delivery of agentic or GenAI solutions.

**How it differs from GenAI SA:** Narrower, more delivery-focused. Asks for practical experience with orchestration frameworks (LangChain, LangGraph, Semantic Kernel, AutoGen, CrewAI), agent memory, evaluation, observability, and multi-agent coordination.

**Why this is the evolution target:** As proof-of-work builds (real client engagements, published agentic workflow projects), the credential base naturally moves toward this label. Julian's existing AI OS work is already partially relevant here. The Agentic AI SA title carries stronger differentiation and a faster-growing niche.

**Promotion condition:** Can credibly demonstrate practical examples of orchestrated workflows, tool-using agents, evaluation frameworks, and implementation patterns beyond prompting.

---

### 5. AI Consultant / AI Transformation Consultant / AI Implementation Lead (Adjacent)

**What these are:** Consulting-oriented roles emphasising assessment, strategy, use-case prioritisation, integration planning, metrics, training, and adoption support. Wider than the architect cluster but with less ownership of the architecture itself.

**Why not the primary target:** These titles carry less technical prestige signal and weaker compensation benchmarks than the architect cluster. The GenAI SA label captures the same market opportunity with stronger positioning.

**When relevant:** If a specific opportunity is framed as a consulting engagement (e.g. TTI) or if a firm's levelling structure uses "consultant" at the senior level, these titles may apply. Treat as equivalent positioning, not a step down.

## The Pivot Decision (June 2026)

The June 2026 career positioning review started from the hypothesis that **Forward Deployed Engineer** was the right target, given the surface similarity to Julian's embedded-practitioner career pattern. Research quickly revealed the flaw:

- FDE is "builder-first": production coding is the baseline, not a differentiator
- Julian's 20+ years of capital is "architecture-and-value-first": enterprise design, stakeholder trust, operating-model thinking, AI-into-delivery
- The gap to close for FDE (production engineering depth) is large and would require 12–24 months before Julian was competitive
- The gap to close for GenAI SA (LLM fluency and published proof of work) is manageable and can be closed while the job search runs

The better question, which the research confirmed: **"Which AI role best monetises my current architecture and consulting capital with only targeted upskilling in code?"** The answer is GenAI Solution Architect.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **GenAI Solution Architect** is the primary market target. Architecture-and-value-first; coding bar is fluency, not production depth.
- **Agentic AI Solution Architect** is the evolution as proof of work builds. Start building toward it in parallel.
- **FDE/Agentic Engineer** require a production coding foundation Julian does not have. Not the right targets for Q3 2026 or near-term.
- The strongest differentiator across all these roles is not "knows GenAI" but **"has turned enterprise use cases into secure, integrated, operational solutions."** That is Julian's actual credential.
- The TTI engagement (FDE framing, Director EA JD) remains valid as a bespoke consulting conversation. The market positioning uses GenAI SA; the TTI pitch uses the embedded-practitioner / FDE narrative. These are not in conflict.
