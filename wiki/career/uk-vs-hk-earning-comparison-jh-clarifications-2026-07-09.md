# UK vs HK Earning Comparison - JH Clarifications

## Key Takeaways

- "HK has no cheap floor" was too strong. The better reading is that HK$100k/mo gross is a **weak recovery floor**: survivable once Cecil Road rent is offset, but not a strong savings, pension, or DB-flat-repair path.
- "Shrinking market" means the HK senior-job market is cooling, not that there are literally no HK jobs. The report cites weak 2026 HK headcount-growth intent and the loss of Julian's best-fit telco/SI employer type.
- The four-year London runway still stands with the latest London figures, but only when the MPF is included. Non-ISA liquid alone gives about 20 months. Non-ISA liquid plus MPF gives about 47 months, about 3.9 years.
- The "~10x role volume" claim is directional, not a hard measured fact. It comes from UK versus HK role-count snapshots and the UK feasibility work. It should be used as "order-of-magnitude larger UK market," not as a precise statistic.
- HK PR is confirmed. That removes work-permit uncertainty from the HK case. Localisation remains a hiring preference, not a visa hurdle.

## 1. What does the HK floor really mean?

After the income-floor reconciliation, it should not be read as "HK$100k/month is unlivable."

It means there is no low-stress, lower-paid HK job that clearly rebuilds safety.

In the UK, the cost-covering floor is low relative to the target market:

| UK floor | Approx gross required | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Net-worth break-even | ~£49-50k | Covers consumption and non-equity costs. |
| Cash-flow break-even | ~£77-78k | Covers full monthly cash burn. |
| London architect median | ~£82.5-95k | The floor sits at or below the median. |

So in the UK, a median or below-median architecture role can cover the household.

In HK, the household burn is much higher:

| HK figure | Amount |
|---|---:|
| 12-month HK actual mean | ~HK$92,196/mo |
| Recent 6-month trend | ~HK$83,940/mo |
| Lower-paid HK role checked by Julian | ~HK$100,000/mo gross |
| Estimated net from that lower-paid role | ~HK$85,000/mo |
| Cecil Road net rent offset if staying in HK | ~HK$17,360/mo |

That lower-paid HK role is survivable if the Cecil Road rent offset is counted properly. Against recent actuals, the first-pass spreadsheet shows a surplus of roughly HK$18k/month; against 12-month mean actuals, roughly HK$10k/month.

So the corrected wording is:

> HK$100k/month is a weak recovery floor, not an unlivable floor.

It may stop or reduce drawdown, but it does not obviously rebuild pension, repair the DB-flat position, or create much safety. The UK floor remains easier because the required UK job sits below the local architect median, while HK$100k/month is still a serious senior-role requirement in a cooling market.

## 2. What does "shrinking market" mean?

The wording should be softened from "shrinking" to "cooling" unless live role-count trend data is collected.

The evidence is:

- The comparison report cites KPMG HK 2026: only 19 percent of employers expected headcount growth, the weakest since 2020.
- The finance model already treated the stay-HK option as requiring roughly a 45-50 percent chance of landing a large HK role.
- Julian's best-fit HK employer type has weakened: the report cites Telstra's enterprise / managed-services exit and cuts as a hit to the telco/SI lane he came from.
- HK role-volume data is thinner than UK data; there is no ITJobsWatch equivalent and no public per-posting applicant data.

Clean wording:

> HK is not a zero-job market. It is a cooling, local-first senior market where the viable salary floor is itself a senior role.

## 3. Does the four-year runway still stand with the latest London figures?

Yes, but with a specific caveat.

The latest London burn in [[uk-move-financial-model]] is about £4,550/mo after the corrected Band E council tax and honest DB-flat shortfall. The DB letting agency fee is now treated as a one-off HK$11,000 (~£1,100), not a recurring monthly allowance.

Runway:

| Pot | Amount | Burn | Runway |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Non-ISA liquid only | ~£91,869 | ~£4,550/mo | ~20 months |
| Non-ISA liquid plus MPF | ~£216,339 | ~£4,550/mo | ~48 months, about 4.0 years |

So "four years" still stands if the MPF is successfully withdrawn and included in the spendable transition pot.

It should not be stated as "cash in bank gives four years." The cleaner wording is:

> London has about 20 months on non-ISA liquid alone, and about four years if the MPF withdrawal lands and is treated as transition capital. ISAs and property sales remain outside the runway floor.

Important caveats:

- MPF withdrawal has timing and UK-tax-treatment risk.
- The £12,475 HK tax rebate is anticipated, not banked.
- One-off moving costs of £10-25k reduce effective runway.
- The DB-flat shortfall is a cash drag, although much of the mortgage payment is equity.

Even after those caveats, the runway still covers a 12-18 month search with margin.

## 4. What is the evidence for "far more absolute well-paid seats" in the UK?

The evidence is directional, not precise enough for the phrase to stand unqualified.

What supports it:

- The UK is a national market, plus London and the M3/M4 West Corridor, plus fully remote UK roles. HK is one city of about 7.5M people.
- The UK feasibility work used UK job-board and salary sources with much better coverage than the HK side.
- The UK report found materially more roles across the target families, especially when counting solution architecture, enterprise architecture, security architecture, network-security, presales, and consulting.
- HK sources were thinner and more snapshot-based.

What weakens it:

- "Well-paid seats" is not the same as "all seats."
- Public role counts do not tell us applicant count, true salary, remote eligibility, age-screen risk, or whether Julian is a top candidate.
- The rare-stack advantage is still a hypothesis until live recruiter or job-board validation confirms the actual competitor pool.

Corrected wording:

> The UK appears to offer materially more absolute seats across Julian's target families, and likely more well-paid seats in absolute terms, but the exact multiple is directional and needs live validation.

## 5. What is the evidence for "~10x role volume"?

This should be treated as an order-of-magnitude shorthand, not a measured constant.

The report's source base is:

| Evidence type | What it supports | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| UK feasibility report | UK has many more relevant role postings across architecture families. | Medium-high for direction |
| LinkedIn / Glassdoor snapshots | UK role-counts materially exceed HK role-counts. | Low-medium |
| ITJobsWatch | UK salary, vacancy, and day-rate trend coverage. | High for UK, no HK equivalent |
| HK job-board snapshots | HK market is much thinner. | Low-medium |
| Population / market-size logic | UK national market plus London corridor should exceed a single-city HK market. | Directional |

Use this as:

> UK volume is several times larger, probably around an order of magnitude across broad target families.

Do not use it as:

> UK has exactly 10x the number of suitable, £150k, commute-valid, age-neutral roles for Julian.

That exact claim has not been proven.

## 6. HK PR confirmed

This is resolved.

Corrected interpretation:

- HK PR removes work-permit risk.
- Localisation remains a preference: Cantonese, local network, local employer familiarity, and "local first" hiring can still matter.
- Therefore the UK no longer wins on "right to work versus HK work permit."
- The UK still wins on "relocated UK address from day one" once Julian is physically in London.

The comparison report has already been corrected to say:

> HK PR confirmed. Localisation remains a hiring preference, not a work-permit hurdle.

## Decision Implication

The comparison's headline mostly survives, but the language needs discipline:

| Claim | Safe version |
|---|---|
| UK clearly wins the floor | Safe, because the UK floor salary is low and runway is strong. |
| HK has no cheap floor | Too strong. Replace with: HK$100k/month is a weak recovery floor, survivable with Cecil Road rent offset but not a strong rebuilding path. |
| HK market is shrinking | Better as "cooling" unless live trend data is gathered. |
| UK has 10x roles | Directional, not exact. Use "several times larger, roughly order-of-magnitude." |
| HK visa uncertainty | Removed. HK PR confirmed. |

The final strategic position:

> Income does not justify the move by itself. The UK is safer for the cost-covering floor. HK is financially better if a large role lands. The well-paid target is still a wash because HK pays more conditional on success, while the UK likely offers a broader, safer landing surface.
