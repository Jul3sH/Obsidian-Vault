## What Peter is for in this signal layer

Peter is the **peer-level credibility voucher**, not Horst's deputized enforcer. His role:

- Confirm Julian is doing serious enterprise AI work that warrants Stephan's time
- Lightly indicate the topic is on Horst's radar without weaponizing it
- Validate the friendship-and-trust quality of Julian as a connection

**What Peter is NOT for:**
- Selling Julian's specific pitch (Julian does that direct, in writing and in the coffee meeting)

## Specific Don'ts

- **Don't say "Horst said you should…"** — that weaponizes the family channel. Stephan reads it as dad sending an enforcer. Peter's own instinct flagged this risk and was right.
- **Don't recount Sophia / custody / ex-wife specifics.** The ambient personal-inflection signal *is* valuable (per the corrected empathy-lever reading — see [[tti-stephan-relationship-analysis#Material Correction]]) but the specifics belong elsewhere. "Interesting inflection point" — yes. "Julian's getting divorced and might lose custody" — no.
- **Don't sell the briefs Julian has just sent.** Julian's documents speak for themselves. Peter shouldn't try to explain them.
- **Don't sound rehearsed.** This is a peer dropping a credible signal, not delivering a speech.
- **Don't ask Stephan to do anything specific.** No "introduce him to X" or "give him an interview." That's Julian's ask to make.

## Why this calibration works — the three-channel triangulation

Each channel plays the role it's best suited to:

| Channel | Role | When |
|---|---|---|
| **Julian → Stephan (direct)** | The business case. Substance, specificity, ask. | Day 0 (this week) |
| **Peter → Stephan (peer-level)** | Validates substance and trust. Confirms Julian is worth time. | Day +2–4 |
| **Horst → Stephan (family-level, probable)** | Sanctions follow-up. Closes the loop. | Day +7–10 (after Horst returns from Germany) |

If Peter overreaches into Horst's lane, the family-signal layer becomes less credible when it arrives. If Peter underreaches, Stephan may not register the peer-level endorsement. The middle line: warm, brief, credible, non-directive.

By the time Horst asks Stephan, Stephan has:
- Briefs in hand (Day 0)
- Peer-level Peter signal (Day 2–4)
- A natural ready-answer for Horst: *"Julian's sent me a couple of things; we're setting up coffee."*

That's the optimal converging-signals shape.
