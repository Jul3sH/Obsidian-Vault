## The Role-Conversion Mechanic (how an artefact becomes a role)

Only relevant *after* a name + conversation is secured. Preserved here as the reference for that moment:

1. Relevant two-pager → Stephan forwards to the named exec (or asks Julian to send directly).
2. Exec engages → discovery conversation about one candidate workflow.
3. In that conversation the natural exec question is *"how would we actually do this?"*
4. Julian's answer: *"Two ways. A 90-day consulting engagement to prove the model on one workflow, or — more efficient for TTI — bring me in as the EA Director in region with this as the first 90-day deliverable. You already have the JD from Stephan."*
5. Exec says yes to one, or escalates internally. Either way Julian is *inside* the decision conversation.

**Key principle:** every artefact closes on the small concrete ask (the meeting), never the big abstract ask (the role).

## Standing Risks

- **Singular Stephan dependency.** All channels route through Stephan. Mitigation: Peter (parallel channel to Stephan and potentially Horst); Horst's sanction; cold-outreach Plan B.
- **The Horst window.** Horst is 82–83; the protective canopy has finite duration. Monitor via Peter; compress if a health signal appears.
- **Consumed-but-not-converted.** The recurring failure mode (Cipriani brief, the CV, the JD). The diagnose-first gate is the direct response: stop sending into a channel of unproven function.
- **Generosity leak.** An excellent strategy doc can be implemented without Julian. Mitigation: artefacts describe *what/why*, not the full *how*; implementation detail is the consulting deliverable.
