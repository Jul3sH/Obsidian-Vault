# PART THAT GOES OUT (strip everything above; ≤ half a page)

**For Ty — sizing the cost of fragmented regional technology, and deciding the fix without betting on it**

**The problem, in money.** Our technology runs on three ERP tiers — SAP group-wide, Oracle Cloud in North America finance, Oracle EBS legacy in Hong Kong — with no group layer aligning them. Every cross-region technology decision therefore escalates to a standing committee. The cost lands twice: senior time in the rooms, and, larger, decision latency — projects and spend stalled while a call bounces between regions. Finance meets the same thing as reconciliation effort at every close.

**I won't hand you a number I haven't measured.** The cost is real but unquantified — and it's cheaply quantifiable: the committee cadence, attendee lists and durations are all in the calendar. Someone in finance ops can pull them in an afternoon.

**So the first step isn't a hire — it's a two-week audit.** Pull the standing cross-region committee data, convert the senior-hours and the reconciliation effort to money, and put a real number on the coordination cost. Cost of the audit: effectively zero. Output: the number this whole question turns on.

**Then a pre-agreed gate decides the role, not faith.** Set the threshold now: if the measured coordination cost exceeds **[X]×** the fully-loaded cost of a director-level coordinating role (~**[$Y]**), the role is chartered; if it doesn't, it dies. The role earns its existence from the measured number before anyone commits a dollar to it.

**Why director-level (one line).** The role coordinates the existing regional technology directors; below their level it has no standing to make a cross-region call stick, so the cheaper version simply doesn't function.

**What it is NOT.** Not a turf move: regional directors keep their teams, budgets and line authority. The forum decides only the cross-region questions no single region can settle alone — the ones already going to committee today.

**The ask.** Approve the two-week audit. It's costless, reversible, and it replaces an argument with a number. If the number isn't there, we both walk away clean.

## Appendix — Fable red-team of v1 (skeptical CFO, 19 Jul)

Full findings that drove the v2 rewrite, preserved for the record.

**Frank's 60-second reaction:** "You walked a page into a CFO's office with square brackets where the numbers should be. The role you want me to fund is the thing that's supposed to find out whether the role was justified. If the number is a calendar audit away, do the audit first. And why is this on my desk and not Ty's?"

**Ranked problems:**
1. **FATAL — self-funding test has numbers on neither side.** Benefit bracketed, role cost absent entirely; "covers its cost several times over" is an equation with no terms. The missing number is an afternoon in Outlook, so its absence reads as "nobody bothered," not honesty. Hours aren't a CFO's currency — dollarise them.
2. **FATAL — circular / hire-first-justify-later.** Asks Frank to spend to discover whether spending was right.
3. **SERIOUS — Ty's ownership asserted, not evidenced; the anonymous "I" betrays it.** Frank's first question: "Has Ty seen this?"
4. **SERIOUS — Tony landmine armed:** "decisions once / committees retired" retires Tony's rooms; "Oracle EBS HK" names his estate as problem child; "pools authority rather than removing it" begs the question.
5. **SERIOUS — no reason to act now, no easy action.** The ask is a question, not an executable decision; "park it for my successor" is the rational zero-mess move.
6. **MINOR — "defined stop point" undefined; "why director-level" reads as grade-lobbying; bracket/tilde typography shouts unfinished draft.**

**Fatal omission:** the role's own cost and which budget carries it. A CFO won't sign a multiple of an unstated number.

**Strategic verdict:** Frank is the wrong primary recipient — a departing CFO structurally can't stand up a new role; his residual value is a cheap blessing that survives his exit. Target Ty (owns it) + the incoming CFO (inherits funding). **The one change:** replace the ask — not "fund a director role" but "approve a two-week, zero-cost coordination audit with a pre-agreed threshold: above X the role is chartered, below it dies." Converts "build structure in my final quarter" into "let someone count meetings." → *All actioned in v2 above.*
