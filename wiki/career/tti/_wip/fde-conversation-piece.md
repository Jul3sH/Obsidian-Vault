## 1. Verbal opener (high-level, for the meeting)

> "There's a role that's blown up across enterprise AI this past year, the forward deployed engineer. The idea is simple. Instead of a consultant who advises and leaves, or a data scientist building models in a lab, you embed one senior person inside the business. They find the workflows that actually cost time and money, and they build the AI that fixes them, in production, working alongside your teams. The whole point is measurable business outcomes, not technology projects.
>
> What's interesting for TTI is that it's almost exactly the role we already sketched out: hands-on, practitioner, bridging APAC and the US, turning the AI promise into real EBIT. The forward-deployed model just puts a name on it. I'd see it as a twelve-month engagement, the first six are embedding and getting traction, the back half is where the value compounds, and we prove it on one or two real workflows before anyone commits to anything permanent.
>
> Where it starts depends on what discovery surfaces, wherever the biggest, most provable win is. Cybersecurity is often one of those, given how much there always is to do there, but it could equally be a cost or supply-chain process. That's the point of starting with discovery."

## 2. The two-page leave-behind

**Forward Deployed Engineering at TTI**
*One concrete way Julian could add value, mapped to the Director, Enterprise Architecture role*

**What a Forward Deployed Engineer is**
- A senior practitioner embedded *inside* the business, not an outside adviser.
- Finds the highest-value workflows, then builds the AI that runs them in production, alongside the teams who use them.
- Measured on business outcomes (cost, efficiency, risk), not technology activity.
- The fastest-growing, scarcest senior role in enterprise AI. The labs and enterprise leaders created it because the hard part of AI is not the model, it is fitting it into how an enterprise actually works.

**Why it matters now**
- Every enterprise has the same gap: AI promises much, little reaches the P&L. The blocker is rarely the technology, it is embedding it into governed, real-world workflows.
- That is a service-design and architecture problem, not a data-science one, which is where deep enterprise experience wins.

**It is the role TTI already has on the table**
The Director, Enterprise Architecture profile (shared 16 April) already describes a forward deployed engineer:

| The Director EA profile says | A forward deployed engineer is |
|---|---|
| "Leads by doing," gets things done personally, no large team | An embedded senior builder, not an adviser |
| "Design AI-native workflows... restructure work, not merely accelerate it" | Someone who builds AI into the workflows that matter |
| "Measurable business outcomes: cost, efficacy, risk," not activity | Outcome-measured by design |
| Bridges APAC and US, influence without authority | Embeds across the org and ships |

The label simply adds external validation to the role TTI already defined.

**How Julian aligns**
- 25+ years hands-on enterprise and solution architecture across global MNCs. TOGAF, CISSP, CTBME/FinOps: the JD's exact credential set.
- US$300M+ of transformation delivered. "Influence without authority" is the career, not a stretch.
- The past year spent hands-on building AI agents and agentic workflows (a personal AI operating system): the *build* half of FDE, on top of the architecture half.
- Service design as the differentiator: 10+ years turning new capabilities into governed processes, exactly what "AI into workflows" demands.

**What TTI gets, and the low-risk on-ramp**
- Outcome-led and discovery-first: the engagement opens by mapping TTI's highest-value AI opportunities, then builds. The value can land wherever the benefit is greatest, cybersecurity governance and compliance (a frequent high-value area across a globally distributed supply chain, mapping directly to Julian's CISSP/GRC depth), cost and EBIT, or a core supply-chain or EA workflow.
- A scarce, in-demand capability, proven on one or two of TTI's own workflows first.
- A bounded ~12-month engagement through Julian's HK company, with a six-month checkpoint: no headcount friction, take stock and continue or stop.
- Additive to the existing AI platform work, not competing with it. The platform builds capability; this converts it to behaviour and outcomes inside real workflows.
- One of several ways Julian could contribute (cost optimisation, security architecture, EA governance); the forward-deployed model is simply the most concrete and fastest to prove.
