# AI Leverage: Working Notes — sizing the method against a live initiative

**Held for:** Ty Staviski, Group Deputy CFO · on request · June/July 2026

> **In brief.** A senior enterprise-technology operator (cybersecurity, enterprise architecture, US$300M+ delivery), now working AI-native. **The lever:** redesigning work around AI, rather than bolting it onto existing roles, takes out structural admin and G&A cost, illustratively ~US$55k per absorbed role per year, recurring. **The frame:** size where it lands against an initiative you already run, in the 9.3% to 10% EBIT build.

You told the market that 2025's margin improvement came from taking out "structural corporate admin and G&A cost" and from "leveraging synergies, AI and our assets," building from a 9.3% normalized base toward a 10% EBIT target: taking bad cost out of the system so it can be reinvested in Milwaukee and RYOBI. That is precisely the work I do, now AI-native. These notes are how I approach it and the value it creates.

## 1. What I bring

- **A cost operator first.** Restructured a function to a 35% sustainable saving in one year, then 6%+ YoY efficiencies, mapping technology spend to EBIT in FinOps and TBM terms (CTBME, FinOps).
- **Three disciplines that usually need three hires, in one person:**
  - **Enterprise architecture** (TOGAF): governed US$200M+ of complex designs to cost, capability and quality.
  - **Cybersecurity** (CISSP): built a US$25M+ managed-security portfolio (SASE, SSE, ZTA, SIEM/SOAR, EDR).
  - **Programme delivery:** governed and assured US$300M+ of transformation delivered without business disruption, to the standard your North America ERP conversion set.
- **25+ years** across APAC, EMEA and the Americas, under US technical leadership from a Hong Kong base: the US to Asia bridge, in region.

## 2. What is different now, and why TTI is positioned to win here

I have rebuilt how I work around AI: agentic workflows and a personal operating system that let me produce, solo, at a level that used to take a team. That is not a tools claim; it is a working method, and I am a live example of the thing itself, one experienced operator, AI-leveraged, absorbing the work of several.

Most enterprises are not getting this. McKinsey's 2025 agentic-AI research found more than 80% of companies still report no material contribution to earnings from generative AI, because they bolt it onto existing roles instead of redesigning the work. TTI is already in the minority that has moved the number: you attributed 2025's margin gains to AI and asset leverage. The opportunity is not to start, it is to push the same lever further and faster than a bolt-on approach ever reaches.

## 3. How I approach it: resource design, not data science

This is not model-building. It is resource design: deciding what genuinely needs human judgment, what AI can now carry, and how the two fit together. That has been my discipline for a career, designing end-to-end operational workflows across complex, multi-outsourced environments for 12 years. AI only moves where the line sits: it is a new kind of resource in the design, an entity in the workflow rather than a person.

So I do not arrive with a fixed AI plan to sell you. I look at the work you actually do and find where the balance has shifted.

## 4. A worked example: ~US$55k per absorbed role, recurring

Open roles are one concrete place this shows up. Before adding a head, the resource-design question is how much of the role's task content AI can now absorb, and whether augmenting the existing team beats hiring.

| Step | Figure | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Fully-loaded cost of a senior technical / finance-systems role | ~US$165k / year | ~US$115k US wage (BLS, 2024) plus the ~30% employer benefit burden (BLS ECEC, 2026) |
| Automatable share of task time | ~one third | A 2024 study of 100,000+ workers in GenAI-connected roles found AI cuts working time by 50% for a third of tasks |
| Recurring value per role absorbed | **~US$55k / year** | Loaded cost × automatable third |
| Across a 15 to 25 role requisition pipeline | **~US$0.8M to 1.4M / year, recurring** | Plus the full loaded cost wherever a hire is avoided outright |

This one narrow lever is illustrative, not the prize; it is a checkable proof of the method. The larger opportunity is the same discipline applied across the whole technology cost estate, starting with the non-strategic admin base, where you run admin at ~9.8% of sales with "further efficiencies" already flagged: that is where basis points, not thousands, are in play. The honest figure is discovery-dependent, so I will give you the underlying, recurring number after a look, not a headline number now. What I stand behind is that the method is sound and the numbers compound.

## 5. Where it fits

Complementary, not competing. The systems and asset leverage you have credited for 2025's margin, and the drive to one standard way the business reads its P&L and margin, are exactly this operating layer: I extend work already underway rather than start something new. The product and data-platform AI mandates sit elsewhere; disruption, as Steve put it, "is not just product alone," it is AI in the supply chain, in quality, and in the back office, which is my lane. It is one-team work: I augment existing people rather than replace them, which is also how the saving is realized. The same discipline covers security and governance: AI now carries much of the routine evidence and compliance work under NIST CSF while risk ownership stays human, exactly the same line-drawing, and the AI threat surface is evolving as fast as the efficiency side, which is its own reason to hold this capability in-house.

## 6. The frame for the conversation

Not a role, and not a fixed proposal: one short "test and learn." Pick an initiative you or a leader already care about, and let me show you where the leverage is and what it is worth. I would rather earn the right to more scope by proving it on one workflow than argue for it on paper.

The structure is prudent but proactive: a time-boxed consulting engagement through my Hong Kong company, so no headcount, simple to start and simple to stop. If discovery does not surface a pipeline worth a multiple of the engagement cost, I will tell you, and we stop. The first ~90 days: a few weeks watching how the work actually gets done and sizing the pipeline (that is where the stop decision sits), then one workflow redesigned and proven live with the team that runs it, with the saving measured by a method your own finance team signs off.
