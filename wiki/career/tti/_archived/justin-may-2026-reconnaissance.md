# Justin Reconnaissance Brief — 29 May 2026

> *Briefing for Julian's conversation with Justin Gilbert ahead of Justin's catch-up with Stephan today. Justin's role today is empathy-priming and expectation-setting, NOT advocacy. The substantive material (the AI Enablement Strategy artefact) is in production and will land in Stephan's hands ~9–10 June. Today is about ensuring Stephan is primed to receive it warmly.*

---

## What we want Stephan to feel after Justin's conversation

1. **Anticipation.** "Julian's working on something — proper, focused. It's coming."
2. **Empathy.** Refreshed personal context, ambient, no specifics.
3. **No pressure to do anything.** Justin is not asking Stephan for anything on Julian's behalf today.
4. **No specific content to react to.** Justin must not describe what Julian is working on in any detail — he'll mangle it (documented risk in [[tti-executive-stakeholders#Justin Gilbert]]).

---

## What Justin should say (ambient, conversational, not scripted)

Three lines Justin can drop naturally into the conversation. Use them light, use them once, don't repeat.

### Line 1 — Anticipation
> *"Julian's been heads-down on something — I think he's going to send you something properly thought through soon."*

**Why it works:** Stephan starts expecting content. When the AI Enablement Strategy lands in 10 days, it arrives at the moment Stephan is primed to read it rather than at a cold moment.

### Line 2 — Empathy
> *"It's been a difficult few months for him personally — he's not making a big thing of it but it's there."*

**Why it works:** Re-engages Stephan's empathy (his primary action-engine per [[tti-stephan-relationship-analysis]]). Vague enough that Stephan doesn't feel pressured to act, specific enough that the personal context is alive in his mind when the artefact arrives.

**No Sophia specifics.** Justin doesn't need to name what's difficult. The ambient signal is the signal.

### Line 3 — Posture
> *"I think this is the most focused I've seen him on something in a while — he's not winging this one."*

**Why it works:** Pre-validates the seriousness of the upcoming artefact. When Stephan opens a 30-page NPV-grade strategy document, he's already been told to expect substance. Reduces the friction to taking it seriously.

## What Justin MUST NOT do

- **Don't name the artefacts.** Don't say "AI Enablement Strategy" or "Cybersecurity" or "Enterprise Architecture." Don't even say "documents" or "briefs" (Stephan was expecting briefs and they're not coming). Stick with "something" / "something properly thought through."
- **Don't ask Stephan to do anything for Julian.** No "you should help him" / "you should set him up with X" / "you should introduce him to Y." The personal-friendship endorsement only works when there's no transaction attached. The moment Justin asks for something, his voice becomes self-interested in Stephan's ear.
- **Don't reference the old briefs.** The consulting brief and AI leadership brief are dead. If Stephan asks "wasn't Julian putting some stuff together?", Justin's response is: *"I think he's pulled it all into one bigger thing — I'd let him tell you."*
- **Don't share opinions on what Julian is producing.** Justin doesn't know the content; he should not speculate. If pressed, deflect: *"I haven't seen it — I think it's the kind of thing he wants to land directly with you."*
- **Don't drink before this conversation if avoidable.** Realistic check: if it's an evening drinks context, that's the context. In that case lean even harder on the *impression* framing because precision will degrade. Daytime / in-office contact is reliable per [[tti-executive-stakeholders#Justin Gilbert]].
- **Don't argue with Stephan if Stephan has reservations.** If Stephan says *"yeah but he hasn't sent me anything for weeks"* — Justin agrees, doesn't defend: *"Yeah, I think he's deliberately gone away to do something serious — I'd give it a week or two."*

## Risks specific to today

### Justin volunteers more than briefed
Justin has a track record of mangling business detail and ego-defending if challenged. If he gets enthusiastic and starts trying to sell the artefact (which he hasn't seen and doesn't understand), Stephan's expectations get set wrong.

**Mitigation:** Frame the brief to Justin as *"keep it light — I don't want to pre-empt what I'm sending him, I just want him to know it's coming and that I'm taking it seriously."* This satisfies Justin's ego (he's being trusted with sensitive context) AND constrains him to the right tone.

### The Jamie pattern
~2 years ago, Justin was upset when Julian went to Stephan after Justin had blocked his Milwaukee attempt — Julian had told Justin in advance but Justin (drunk) complained to mutual friend Jamie that "Julian went behind his back." This codifies *"Justin likes to be involved."*

Today's brief satisfies this by giving Justin a defined role (reconnaissance + empathy-priming + observation report) — he is involved, just not as advocate.

### Drinking pattern
After-work / evening drinking only; never during the day (per [[tti-executive-stakeholders#Justin Gilbert]]). If today's catch-up is daytime / in-office, reliability is normal. If it's an evening drink, expect Justin to embellish — the empathy line will probably land naturally, the anticipation line may get exaggerated, and the posture line may turn into something more specific than briefed.

**Suggested opening:**
> *"Mate — quick favour. I'm putting something pretty substantial together for Stephan over the next couple of weeks. I don't want to walk him through it yet, I just want him to know it's coming and that I'm being serious about it.
