# Stephan Lunch - Game Plan (Tue 23 Jun)

> Glanceable prep. Stephan invited you and is arranging it - warm, engaged. Likely warmed by Justin's lunch with him today (22 Jun). Catch Justin first if you can; adapt to whatever he says Stephan is already thinking.

## The one goal
Not to close. Stephan cannot decide - he opens doors and advocates. **Win = a concrete next step that survives his Thursday departure.** Either he routes you UP/around Tony (Frank Chan / Horst / a US owner like Ty) before he goes, or he green-lights a small discovery phase. Keep it alive over the summer; don't let it die on his holiday.

## Tone
Relationship first. He moves on the relationship, not the deck. It's a friendly lunch he called - let it breathe (Fred, family, Hannah banter). Don't pitch. The structure below is so YOU sound clear when it turns to business, not a speech to deliver.

## Opener (light, only when it turns to "so what are you thinking?")
> "There's a role that's blown up in enterprise AI this past year - the forward deployed engineer. Instead of a consultant who advises and leaves, you embed one senior person inside the business: they find the workflows that actually cost time and money and build the AI that fixes them, in production, alongside your teams. Measured on business outcomes, not tech projects. It's almost exactly the role we already sketched - hands-on, bridging APAC and the US, turning AI into real EBIT."

## The structure (have it ready; offer, don't lecture)
- **Bounded ~12-month engagement through my HK company. Six-month checkpoint** - first half embeds and gets traction, back half compounds. Take stock and continue or stop.
- **Discovery-first on-ramp** - opens by mapping TTI's highest-value AI opportunities, then builds. Prove it on one or two real workflows before anyone commits to anything permanent. *This is the easiest thing for him to say yes to.*
- **No headcount friction** (runs through my company). **Additive to Brian Pivar's platform**, not competing.
- Value can land wherever discovery says: cyber/GRC, cost/EBIT, supply chain, or EA governance.

**Commercial flex (only if he probes "how would it work"):**
1. Initiative-led advisory - 2-3 days/week vs a named priority, a quarter at a time.
2. Fixed-scope sprint - 4-8 weeks, one deliverable. Procurement-friendly SOW.
3. Fractional retainer - by week/month, drawn against work as it emerges. Lowest commitment, easiest start.
