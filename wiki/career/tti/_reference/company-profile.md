# TTI — Company Profile

> *Reusable reference on TTI as an organisation — useful whether pursuing a role or working there.*
> 
tags: [career, tti]
## Overview
- **Full name:** Techtronic Industries (TTI)
- **HQ:** Hong Kong
- **Listed:** SEHK: 0669
- **Core business:** Power tools, outdoor power equipment, floorcare
- **Key brands:** Milwaukee Tool, Ryobi, AEG, Empire Level, Stiletto
- **Scale signal from 2025 materials:** c.48k employees, US$15.3B revenue, US$1.2B net profit, US$1.4B free cash flow, and US$757M R&D investment

## Brand Portfolio
- **Professional tools:** Milwaukee is the flagship professional brand and the main growth engine.
- **Consumer tools:** RYOBI is the core consumer / DIY brand.
- **Other power-tool and accessory brands:** AEG, HART, Stiletto, Kango, Empire, Imperial Blades.
- **Floorcare:** Hoover, Oreck, VAX, Dirt Devil.
- **Portfolio concentration:** 2025 materials frame Milwaukee and RYOBI as the dominant focus, together representing c.91% of group revenue.

## Culture and Beliefs
- Doing the right thing.
- Treating each other with integrity and respect.
- Building strong, trusted relationships.
- Giving back.
- Encouraging innovative spirit.
- Exceeding customer expectations.

## Technology Landscape
- ERP: Oracle (factories), SAP (other business units)
- CRM: in use (specific platform TBC)
- The IT function historically focused on systems administration across ERP/CRM

## Organisational Notes
- Chairman and co-founder: Horst Pudwill
- Vice-Chairman: Stephan Pudwill (Horst's son)
- Regional structure — APAC operations include factories and commercial offices
- Hiring for IT roles has historically gone through both internal referral and external agencies
- Operating model appears federated: TTI Group sets strategy, capital allocation, performance standards, and governance; major brands such as Milwaukee and RYOBI retain substantial operational autonomy.
- Practical implication for pitches: group-level propositions should emphasise cross-brand efficiency, governance, risk, and measurable group performance; brand-level propositions should emphasise speed, local ROI, customer advantage, and execution within the brand's own operating model.

## Milwaukee Snapshot
- Milwaukee is the professional / trade brand inside TTI.
- Product breadth includes power tools, hand tools, accessories, lighting, safety solutions, storage, apparel, and jobsite solutions.
- Cordless systems noted in the source material: M12, M18, and MX FUEL.
- ONE-KEY is the connected-tool platform for inventory, tool tracking, tool management, and workforce / jobsite management.
- Source caveat: the raw Google Doc says Milwaukee was acquired by TTI "15 years ago"; that looks stale and should not be repeated without independent verification.

## Strategic Direction (per 2025 Annual Results)

- **10% EBIT margin target by 2027** — frames the cost-discipline lens across all initiatives
- **R&D investment: US$757M (5.0% of turnover)** — significant tech and innovation commitment
- **AI mandates declared at executive level:**
  - Steve Richman (CEO): AI in supply chain, manufacturing, quality
  - Ty Staviski (Deputy CFO): AI + asset leverage to remove structural G&A cost
  - Shane Mall (President, Milwaukee): Digital ecosystems and ML in products (AutoStop, One Key)
- **Automation capex** is a 2025 priority — operational excellence as a core pillar
- **Floorcare reorganised into a single global business** — recent structural change
- **North American ERP implementation** cited by Steve as the benchmark for "flawless execution"
- **Growth segments cited:** data centres and high-tech manufacturing (AI infrastructure tailwind)
