## Key Takeaways

1. **No CAIO and no strategic AI governance seat exists at TTI Group or Milwaukee Tool** — the most senior AI title found is VP, Enterprise Data & AI (Brian Pivar), which is technical/infrastructure in scope.
2. **The EBIT-linked AI transformation mandate (Ty Staviski's explicit agenda) has no identified owner** — this is the sharpest evidence for the gap Julian's proposition addresses.
3. **No APAC-based AI leadership role with strategic scope exists** — APAC digital technology is handled by execution-layer directors (Benjamin Lee, Maximilien Lefeuvre) with no strategic AI mandate.
4. **The CIO layer (Alex Dye / Jason Simpson) is where AI strategy sits by default** in the absence of a dedicated strategic AI seat — making CIO the most natural reporting line for a new Director-AI role.
5. **Brian Pivar (VP Enterprise Data & AI) is the closest US-side counterpart** — his scope is technical (MLOps, Azure, Databricks infrastructure), not governance or transformation strategy. Julian's role partners with him, not against him.
6. **Benjamin Lee (Director, Technology — Milwaukee Asia, Hong Kong) is the most politically sensitive boundary** — geographically co-located in APAC, "digital transformation" framing, but scope is digital systems and marketing tech execution, not AI strategy.
7. **Dan Endean (Director, Machine Learning) and Logan Romine (Solutions Architect, Agentic AI) are low-conflict** — product-embedded ML and IT automation respectively; no enterprise-wide or strategic mandate.
8. **TTI Inc. (Berkshire Hathaway, Fort Worth TX) is a different company entirely** — Atul Thatte (VP Global Data Science & AI, TTI Inc.) and Jon Cowley are at the electronic components distributor. All TTI Inc. profiles are irrelevant to this engagement.

## Political Risk: External AI Leadership Positioning

*Based on cross-validated research (LinkedIn deep-dive + Gemini analysis, May 2026).*

The US-based AI team is substantial, well-integrated, and would present organised (if quiet) resistance to any external appointment framed as "AI leadership from APAC":

- **Brian Pivar's mandate explicitly covers "Enterprise AI"** — any senior AI appointment that doesn't report to or through him creates a competing track he has no incentive to sponsor.
- **The data science, ML, and AI architecture community (Gardner, Endean, Romine) is cohesive** — they would align with Pivar's framing, not with an external APAC voice.
- **The American faction is already asserting dominance** within TTI (see [[tti-internal-politics]]) — an APAC-sourced AI strategy appointment runs directly against the political current.
- **Julian's AI background, while genuine, is practitioner-level, not research-lab-level** — in a political contest with Pivar's team, framing it as equivalent seniority invites a credentials fight Julian doesn't need to have.

**Implication for Julian's proposition:** The AI platform/governance/strategy layer is occupied and politically defended. The unoccupied space — and therefore the durable positioning — is the **employee AI enablement and AI-enabled workflows layer**. None of the 9 identified roles has any mandate covering this. See [[role-context]] for the updated strategic direction.

1. **US-side partner — Brian Pivar.** Pivar owns the data/AI platform infrastructure. Julian's mandate sits above: governance, business-case translation, EBIT linkage, cross-functional programme delivery. These are complementary functions. The pitch is "the strategic layer that makes your platform spend pay off."

2. **APAC boundary — Benjamin Lee.** Lee's team handles digital delivery; Julian's role handles AI strategy. The framing that works: Lee's team *implements* AI initiatives; Julian's role *defines which ones, governs how, and measures the business outcome*. No territory conflict if framed correctly from the start.

3. **Reporting line — CIO layer.** With no CAIO, the CIO (Alex Dye / Jason Simpson / Tom Uva depending on scope) is the natural senior technology counterpart. This is consistent with the modal enterprise AI governance pattern (CIO absorbs AI mandate before CAIO is appointed — see [[enterprise-ai-leadership]]).

4. **The gap that proves the case — Ty Staviski's mandate.** Staviski owns the AI-to-EBIT conversion agenda as Deputy CFO. No role in the identified structure is scoped to own this. This is a C-suite mandate with no delivery owner — the single strongest evidence that a Director-level AI role (Julian's proposition) is solving a real structural gap, not creating a new cost centre.
