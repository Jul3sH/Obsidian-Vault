## Key Dynamics

### US vs Hong Kong stakeholder tension
- Broad pattern: US-based stakeholders (Milwaukee leadership, US-based execs, American board members) vs old-school Hong Kong stakeholders (long-tenured APAC employees, HK-based exec layer)
- Everybody is feeling slightly threatened — politics is active, not dormant
- The American faction is perceived (by HK-side) as ruthless in jockeying for power and positions

### The APAC senior executive layer
- **David Butts — President Asia Pacific & Group EVP** ([[tti-executive-stakeholders#David Butts]]) — sits at the top of the APAC structure; inside the Pudwill family Christmas party circle; senior to Tony Chung. Direct existing relationship with Julian (Christmas party touchpoints).
- **Tony Chung — SVP Finance APAC** ([[tti-executive-stakeholders#Tony Chung]]) — finance with technology oversight; reports up through the APAC structure (likely under David Butts); probable detractor in Julian's engagement
- **Justin Gilbert — SVP Communications** ([[tti-executive-stakeholders#Justin Gilbert]]) — peer SVP of Tony; territorial dynamic between their teams
- **Strategic implication for Julian:** the cleanest routing path for Door 2 (Director-AI-APAC) is **Stephan → David Butts**, not Stephan → Tony Chung. David Butts is senior to Tony, family-circle trusted, and has direct existing rapport with Julian. This bypasses Tony deliberately without making the bypass overt — Tony is below David in the structure, so going to David above him is normal organisational practice, not a political slight.

### Horst as the protective canopy
- Horst's continued chairmanship is the protective cover for HK-side long-tenured employees
- He treats certain individuals like family — e.g., Justin Gilbert (sees him like another son)
- While Horst is active, these individuals are effectively untouchable
- **The fragility:** Horst won't be around forever. Stephan (per HK-side perception) is unlikely to provide equivalent protection against US-side political pressure.

### The "patronage SVP" pattern — and why it matters around Stephan
- Several senior figures hold their positions primarily on the strength of long Pudwill relationships rather than independent commercial achievement (Justin Gilbert is the clearest example — see [[tti-executive-stakeholders#Justin Gilbert]])
- These figures are **untouchable while Horst is active, but not respected as subject-matter experts** by senior leadership
- **Why "they" keep figures like Justin close to Stephan:** Stephan is risk-averse and easily influenced. Trusted personal-network voices like Justin provide a familiar, low-risk consultation pool — safer than Stephan being influenced by commercial outsiders. The lack of independent commercial weight makes them *safe* advisers (not competing power centres).
- **Strategic implication:** when third-party voices around Stephan carry weight (the "what do you think?" pattern — see [[engagement-history#Mid-April 2026]]), those voices are calibrated by the family/board to be loyal-but-non-competing. Their personal-friendship endorsement is high-leverage; their business judgement is not.

### Horst's intelligence-extraction pattern via Justin
- Horst uses Justin Gilbert as a **gossip-extraction node** — allows Justin to rant about what's happening internally so Horst can hear the unfiltered view that wouldn't reach him through formal channels
- Julian's observation: Horst is "very, very clever at getting information from people" — this is a deliberate pattern, not accidental
- **Strategic implication:** anything Justin says about Julian — positive or negative — propagates to Horst over time, even when Horst is overseas (Justin–Horst contact channels remain open)
- Counter-implication: light positive Justin-mentions about Julian are durable signals, not one-off

### July 2024 layoffs
- Major round of layoffs ~July 2024
- Many long-tenured TTI employees lost their roles
- Pattern of who was cut amplifies the current insecurity among the HK-side surviving group
- Context: aligns with the broader cost-takeout / margin-improvement agenda visible in AR25 ([[tti-2025-ar-key-initiatives]])

### Justin Gilbert specifically — current vulnerability
- An American non-executive board member is positioning to take over his executive communication responsibility
- Justin is not busy at work — visibility is low, which amplifies the insecurity
- Currently protected by Horst (family-style relationship) — see [[tti-executive-stakeholders#Justin Gilbert]]
- Worried about life after Horst — concerned Stephan won't be a sufficient protective figure

### Territorial dynamic: Justin's team vs Tony Chung's team
- Justin has IT/web-design people in his own team (small, separate function from the main IT under Tony Chung)
- Tony Chung runs APAC technology more broadly
- Territorial / co-ordination tension between the two
- **Implication for Julian:** don't expect Justin to advocate in any way that pits him against Tony Chung's structure — Justin's own standing is too fragile right now to take that political position

### Justin's perception of APAC IT
- Justin's view: IT in Hong Kong is "terrible"
- **Specifically names Tony Chung as terrible** (Tony Chung = Tony Cheung, romanisation variant of the same name — see [[tti-executive-stakeholders#Tony Chung]])
- General sentiment: "they just do stuff" — reactive, not strategic
- **Justin and Tony Chung are peer SVPs** — the criticism is from a peer, not a subordinate, which gives it different weight in the internal political picture
- **Strategic implication:** internal frustration with Tony's IT leadership is broader than Horst's view; the Director EA pitch ([[role-context]] Door 2) is solving a real perceived problem at multiple levels of the organisation, not just landing as Julian's opinion — AND validates routing around Tony rather than through him

## Implications for Julian's engagement

1. **Position Julian as solving a real problem, not creating a new one.** Internal frustration with current APAC IT competence is broad — the Door 2 case is more durable than it might seem from outside.
2. **Don't ask any HK-side player (Justin, others) to take political risk on Julian's behalf.** Their positions are fragile; asking them to advocate against Tony or against US-side hires is asking too much.
3. **The strategic routing is correct: through Stephan to US executives** (see [[role-context]]). This avoids the HK political mess entirely — and aligns with where decision-making is migrating anyway as the US faction asserts itself.
4. **Time horizon matters:** the protective canopy Horst provides has a defined duration (age 82–83 in 2026). The window for routing through the family channel is finite. The current sanctioned-follow-up window (May 2026) operates inside this; the broader Julian-at-TTI window is also bounded by Horst's presence.
