## About TTI

TTI (Techtronic Industries) is a world-class leader in the design, manufacturing and marketing of power tools, outdoor power equipment, floorcare and cleaning products for professional, industrial, DIY, and consumer markets across the home improvement, infrastructure and construction industries. Listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange and a constituent of the Hang Seng Index, TTI's brand portfolio includes Milwaukee, Ryobi, AEG, Hoover, and Oreck, among others. With 48,000+ employees globally and R&D leadership centred in Brookfield, Wisconsin, TTI operates manufacturing facilities across China, Vietnam, Mexico, and the US. The company's long-term strategic plan focuses on four drivers: Powerful Brands, Innovative Products, Operational Excellence, and Exceptional People, and is targeting a 10% EBIT margin by 2027.

## Role Purpose

TTI is seeking a hands-on Director, Enterprise Architecture to bridge its APAC and US technology organisations. This is a practitioner role. The successful candidate will be as comfortable producing a detailed architecture blueprint or security design as they are presenting strategy to C-suite stakeholders in Hong Kong or the US. They will not arrive and build a team; they will arrive and get things done personally while managing the complex stakeholder landscape required to achieve cross-enterprise alignment.

The role spans enterprise architecture, cybersecurity, and AI enablement. Across all three, the measure of success is the same: measurable business outcomes, not technology activity. Cost reduction, improved efficacy of the business and risk reduction are the metrics that matter, and with TTI targeting a 10% EBIT margin by 2027, the commercial stakes for technology leadership are real.

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Enterprise Architecture

- Drive alignment and execution of the technology vision and strategy set by TTI and Milwaukee technology leadership.
    
- Produce hands-on deliverables, including architecture designs, solution blueprints, technology standards and governance frameworks, without a large supporting team.
    
- Drive cross-enterprise alignment on technology strategy, working across APAC and US stakeholders to secure agreement on direction, priorities, and standards.
    
- Manage the full stakeholder engagement required to land architecture decisions across functions and geographies, from technical teams to business and finance leadership.
    
- Translate group objectives set in Milwaukee into executable plans for TTI, and surface TTI operational realities back to US leadership in ways that inform Milwaukee strategy.
    
- Work with the IT senior leadership team and other business areas on the internal service portfolio, developing capability roadmaps aligned to value maps and business outcomes.
    
- Assist with the governance of controls established for the processes and functions performed by architecture and design teams.
    
- Champion capability maturity to improve cost reduction, business efficacy and risk reduction.
    

### 2. Cybersecurity

- Apply the NIST Cybersecurity Framework across TTI's enterprise security posture, from strategic architecture design through to operational and surface-level guidance.
    
- Provide security architecture and engineering across key domains including SASE/SSE, network security, cloud security, application security, data security, security awareness, IAM, EDR/XDR, TDIR, CTEM, CNAPP, IoT/OT security, email security, and SIEM/SOAR.
    
- Contribute to the formulation and execution of GRC (Governance, Risk and Compliance) frameworks, policies, and practices across TTI's enterprise technology environment.
    
- Protect a globally distributed manufacturing and supply chain environment spanning Asia, the Americas, and Europe, with particular focus on OT/IT convergence risks.
    
- Evaluate and advise on the role of AI in cybersecurity, both as a defensive capability and as a threat vector requiring structured governance.
    

### 3. AI Strategy, Ways of Working and Internal Capability Building

- Design and lead a structured AI capability-building program that converts individual AI productivity into measurable firm-level EBIT contribution.
    
- Re-baseline productivity expectations, design AI-native workflows, and establish governance frameworks aligned to TTI's stated AI and EBIT objectives.
    
- Define evaluation standards, quality frameworks, and cost and ROI disciplines for AI deployment across enterprise functions.
    
- Work hands-on with AI tools and platforms, demonstrating practical deployment quality and identifying where AI can restructure work rather than merely accelerate it.
    
- Build organisational AI capability progressively, seeding skills and standards that scale beyond a single practitioner.
    
- Advance the use of emerging AI capabilities to support TTI's technology strategy, and champion involvement in TTI's AI innovation efforts.
    

### 4. Technology Delivery and Business Outcomes

- Contribute to the building of services to support both internal users and external customers.
    
- Deliver technology initiatives with clearly measurable business outcomes aligned to cost reduction, business efficacy and risk reduction.
    
- Optimise costs to ensure that more value can be achieved within budget guardrails set by the strategic objectives of IT and the enterprise.
    
- Lead cross-functional delivery without requiring a dedicated reporting structure, rallying stakeholders, vendors, and functional teams to execute against shared objectives.
    
- Hold initiatives accountable to outcomes rather than activities, establishing clear KPIs and re-baselined performance expectations throughout delivery.
    
- Drive ongoing business continuous improvements through process and technology suggestions.
    
- Be an advocate for change that delivers true business value and enables operations to scale with growth.
    

### 5. APAC and US Stakeholder Management

- Build successful stakeholder relationships with other IT and business leaders by developing a clear understanding of business needs, acting as a trusted advisor, and ensuring fast, reliable, cost-effective delivery of IT services to meet those needs.
    
- Navigate the organisational and cultural dynamics between APAC operations and US corporate leadership, ensuring both sides are aligned, informed, and able to execute together.
    
- Communicate complex technology strategy in business terms to non-technical stakeholders at executive level.
    
- Act as a bridge between APAC and US teams, facilitating alignment on priorities, resolving cross-regional tensions, and accelerating shared decision-making.
