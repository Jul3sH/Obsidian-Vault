## Regional Split — APAC vs Americas

TTI's technology function is split along regional lines with meaningful autonomy between them:

- **APAC technology** is focused on manufacturing and operations — the factory floor, OT (Operational Technology), systems administration, and the technology stack that supports physical production
- **Americas technology** owns ERP, CRM, sales systems, and commercial platforms — the Oracle Cloud ERP migration and Salesforce Marketing Cloud footprint sit in this remit
- Regions coordinate but operate with significant autonomy — what makes sense for the Americas does not necessarily translate to APAC, and vice versa
- Source: Tony Chung (SVP Finance + Technology APAC), Sep 2025

---

## APAC Technology Focus Areas

- **OT (Operational Technology) security** — flagged by Tony Chung as a relevant area; manufacturing environments running factory systems create an OT attack surface
- **Systems administration** — core infrastructure management across APAC manufacturing and operations sites
- **Manufacturing technology stack** — on-prem and hybrid infrastructure supporting production (VMware, Windows Server, RHEL, Active Directory — see [[tti-technology-stack]] for full stack)

## Governance & Oversight

- Tony Chung (SVP Finance Asia Pacific) holds both finance and technology oversight for APAC — an unusual dual remit that puts a finance executive in control of technology decisions
- Finance-first governance: technology spend decisions are filtered through a cost-control lens; Tony is not a technology advocate and will not support investment he does not see as operationally necessary
- Local team composition — Tony runs a predominantly local team; this shapes hiring preferences and internal culture

## Group and Brand Technology Model

- **Group CIO / enterprise technology owner:** Tom Uva is the closest visible group-level enterprise technology leader for IT platforms, ERP, infrastructure, applications, and business optimisation.
- **Product and engineering technology:** appears to sit mainly inside brand / business P&L structures rather than under a single group CTO.
- **Milwaukee product technology:** Shane Mall is the practical product-and-platform owner at Milwaukee level, with engineering and product specialists beneath him.
- **Milwaukee manufacturing technology / data:** the source notes Brandon Hoffman as VP Global Advanced Manufacturing Technology & Data, positioned as the operations-technology and Industry 4.0 counterpart.
- **Cybersecurity:** Eduardo Ortiz owns TTI group-wide cybersecurity; Milwaukee has its own combined security and infrastructure leadership via Ray Farrell and Mike Donaldson. See [[tti-cybersecurity]].
- **Caveat:** the raw source also names Denis Fauteux as "Chief Technical Officer", focused on battery and rechargeable technologies. Treat this as product-engineering / battery technology, not as the group enterprise IT owner.

## Strategic Technology Priorities From 2025 Materials

- **ERP modernisation and execution discipline:** North America ERP implementation is presented internally as a major "flawless execution" proof point.
- **AI and machine learning:** cited across supply chain optimisation, manufacturing quality, product innovation, and Milwaukee connected products.
- **Connected products and IoT:** ONE-KEY is the main visible digital platform, positioned as a connected-tool and jobsite productivity ecosystem.
- **Automation and manufacturing technology:** automation capex and global manufacturing optimisation are core to productivity and quality.
- **Best-cost systems:** Ty Staviski's finance remit includes finding where TTI can leverage best-cost systems and reduce global technology / back-office overhead.
- **Global standardisation:** source material points to a push toward common systems for P&Ls, gross margin analysis, and finance reporting.
- **Expansion enablement:** Asia and Latin America growth create demand for scalable systems, e-commerce, CRM, infrastructure, and operating discipline.

## Milwaukee IT Role Architecture

The source block sketches Milwaukee's technology organisation from public roles and job descriptions:

| Layer | Roles / examples | What it signals |
|-------|------------------|-----------------|
| CIO | Mark Molitor, CIO Milwaukee Tool | Mature brand-level IT leadership under Milwaukee |
| VP layer | VP Information Technology; VP IT Infrastructure and Operations | Enterprise services, I&O strategy, stakeholder management, budget, standards, ITSM, sourcing, talent |
| Director layer | Director IT Architecture, Infrastructure and Security; Director Digital and Information Security; Director Information Technology; Director Software Development | Formal architecture, infrastructure, security, software delivery, and digital security leadership |
| Senior manager / specialist layer | Supply Chain and ERP, Lead Systems Analyst, Product Information Manager | Strong operational systems base: ERP, WMS, PIM, DAM, Salesforce, Magento, Oracle EBS |
| Product / platform layer | ONE-KEY product managers, product owners, and software development leadership | Milwaukee is not just a tool maker; it has a digital product operating model |

### Milwaukee IT Operating Themes
- Support Milwaukee's continued growth.
- Partner with business functions to align IT strategy and solutions.
- Automate business processes.
- Develop custom software solutions.
- Govern access to data.
- Evaluate and implement purchased software.
- Enable AI and machine learning.
- Manage infrastructure and security across the enterprise.
- Solve business challenges and drive efficiency through technology.

### VP / Director Capability Themes
- Business acumen and manufacturing / product domain knowledge.
- 24x7 global operations.
- Strategic planning, budget control, sourcing, ITSM, and governance.
- DevOps, cloud, IoT, edge computing, big data, AI / ML, and target operating model evolution.
- Security partnership across SOC 2, PCI, ISO, GDPR, and enterprise infrastructure controls.
- Stakeholder translation: technical concepts for business leaders, business concepts for technical teams.

## Strategic Notes

- The APAC/Americas split creates a natural gap in technology coordination — an enterprise architecture or CAIO-type role with cross-regional mandate could address this, but would need a business case framed in operational terms, not technology terms
- TBM (Technology Business Management) — raised with Tony Chung in Sep 2025 but he appeared unfamiliar; this framework directly bridges finance and technology governance and could be a strong future angle if introduced through the right channel (see [[tti-executive-stakeholders]] — Tony watch-outs)
- The source strengthens the case that Milwaukee's technology maturity is the internal benchmark. The opportunity is not to criticise APAC, but to translate mature brand-level practices into group-wide and APAC-relevant operating improvements.
- The cleanest Julian fit remains cross-functional architecture, AI-to-EBIT translation, governance, and process optimisation, not a narrow systems-administrator role.
