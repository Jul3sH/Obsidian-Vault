## The opportunity

**NIST CSF is a perpetual backlog machine.** The framework is comprehensive and detailed by design. In practice this means:
- Control implementation, audit evidence collection, and framework alignment work is never fully caught up.
- Multi-framework compliance (NIST CSF + overlapping regulatory regimes) requires continuous maintenance, not one-off cycles.
- Third-party and vendor risk assessment is manual and evidence-heavy even where the SOC is automated.

**AI-assisted GRC process controls convert directly to coverage and cycle time** - without touching the SOC. Four high-value processes:
1. Control mapping and framework alignment across NIST CSF and overlapping regimes - maintained continuously rather than reconstructed each audit cycle.
2. Audit evidence collection - automated workflows replacing manual evidence-gathering.
3. Third-party / vendor risk - structured assessment pipelines run at scale.
4. Policy and board reporting - AI-drafted with human review, consistent and auditable.

**Why Eduardo is the right routing target (not Tony):**
- Separate budget lane from Asia headcount.
- NIST CSF creates structural demand that won't go away.
- Julian's CISSP + NIST CSF + GRC credentials are the qualifying fit - not agentic AI, which is contestable territory (Brian Pivar, Ryan Rahim).
- Additive to the SOC/platform teams, not competitive.
