## What an Engagement Would Look Like

Three engagement shapes, picked to fit different levels of stakeholder appetite and procurement preference:

**1. Initiative-led advisory.** Embedded two-to-three days a week against a named priority, for a quarter at a time. Best fit for work like the AI-to-EBIT translation Ty has scoped, or the supply chain AI mandate. Outcome: a working artefact (governance framework, target-state architecture, baseline-and-roadmap) plus stakeholder alignment carried through delivery.

**2. Fixed-scope sprint.** Four to eight weeks, defined deliverable. Best fit for an IT cost baseline review (CTBME-led), an enterprise AI governance framework, or an ERP integration architecture review. Procurement-friendly SOW shape.

**3. Fractional capacity.** Retained by the week or month, drawn against initiatives as work emerges. Best fit where TTI knows the capability gap but the specific scope is still forming. Lowest commitment, easiest start.

Happy to flex on commercial structure under any of these shapes.

## Why a Consulting Model Specifically

**APAC base, US-hours available.** Hong Kong-based — I cover US working hours from APAC, in real time. Useful for any of the mandates running out of Milwaukee or the US.

**Single accountable practitioner, AI-multiplied.** What you see is what you get — no slim partner wrapped around a deck of juniors. AI tools sit at the core of how I deliver, not as a Copilot licence sprinkled on top, which materially changes throughput on architecture and stakeholder-facing work. Cost structure runs at a fraction of a Big 4 SOW for equivalent senior time.
