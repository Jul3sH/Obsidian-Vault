## The Trend

Across large enterprises, AI leadership is being formalised at pace. IBM Institute for Business Value reports that the share of organisations with a dedicated Chief AI Officer rose from **11% in 2023 to 26% in 2025** — and **66% of sitting CAIOs expect a majority of organisations to have one within two years**.

The CAIO seat is the most visible signal, but it is not the modal form factor. Empirical analysis of corporate AI governance structures shows that only ~6% of organisations have a dedicated C-suite AI role; the most common instantiation is at **VP / Director level (27%)**, with the responsibility frequently absorbed into existing CIO, CTO, CDO, or Head-of-AI mandates.

In industrial and manufacturing peers specifically, the pattern is clearer:

- **Flex** is currently recruiting a *Director, AI Product Management — Enterprise Forecasting* (live job advert). Director-level, supply chain AI.
- **Mattel** has a Senior Director, Head of AI — global consumer products manufacturer.
- **Jindal Steel** appointed a Chief AI Officer in late 2025 — heavy industrial precedent.
- **51% of US manufacturers already use AI in operations; 61% expect AI investment to increase by 2027** (National Association of Manufacturers).

The geographic distribution is also relevant. Forrester finds 33% of APAC respondents identify the CEO as the primary owner of AI strategy — compared to 18% in North America and 8% in Europe. For a global company with substantial APAC operations, AI leadership at the executive level is more advanced regionally than a US-led view often assumes.

## What This Looks Like at TTI

TTI's AR25 announcements set out a clear AI agenda at the executive level:

- **Steve Richman:** *"leverage AI in supply chain, manufacturing, and quality"*
- **Ty Staviski:** *"leverage AI and synergies to drive EBIT margin improvement"* — targeting structural G&A reduction toward the 10% EBIT 2027 goal
- **Shane Mall:** *"machine learning and AI is reshaping how we bring new solutions to market"*

These are strategic intents, not operating designs. The work between intent and outcome — workflow re-baselining, governance, evaluation design, role redesign, and the economic discipline to convert AI usage into measurable EBIT contribution — is precisely the gap the productivity-gap brief described. That work doesn't get done by adding AI tools to existing roles; it requires:

1. **Role-class redesign, not role acceleration.** When organisations layer AI tools onto existing roles, individual employees use them to reduce personal effort and stress. Productivity does not change at the organisational level. Closing the gap requires identifying which role-classes can be structurally redesigned around AI capability — not just made faster.

    *Worked example.* Take infrastructure operations — IT, systems administration, and similar role-classes. The conventional "AI-enabled" approach gives the role-holder a Copilot licence and looks for time savings. The role-class-redesign approach asks instead: what does this function look like if AI is the default operating mode, with human judgement reserved for exceptions and stakeholder communication? Typically the answer is a smaller team covering more environments, with a different skill mix, lower-cost escalation paths, and a measurable reduction in run cost. The same logic applies to financial reporting, supply chain analysis, and IT cost optimisation.

2. **Governance and evaluation infrastructure.** Specification precision, evaluation design, failure-pattern recognition, and the economics of AI deployment at scale. This is enterprise architecture work, not pure data science.

3. **Cross-functional translation.** AI capability has to be coordinated across technology, finance, operations, and HR. The modal AI leadership role today carries a mixed mandate spanning strategy, governance, data compliance, and workforce education — senior-practitioner work, not point-tool work.

A Director-level AI leadership role is the form factor industrial peers are already adopting to do this. In each case, the role partners with global CEO, CIO, or CTO leadership and operates with a regional or functional mandate.

## The Proposition

A Director-level AI leadership role, APAC-based, partnering with whichever executive owns the global AI agenda from the US.

This is deliberately positioned below the global strategic seat, not in competition with it. The role is the regional executor: the senior practitioner who turns US-set AI strategy into operational delivery across APAC manufacturing, sourcing, and supply chain — and who feeds operational intelligence back to global leadership.

Why this shape works for TTI specifically:

- **TTI's operational centre of gravity for AI work is APAC**, but the strategic centre is Milwaukee. A senior AI practitioner sitting in the US cannot realistically run governance, role redesign, and evaluation across the APAC manufacturing footprint without a trusted regional counterpart.
- **The modal industrial pattern matches this.** Flex's Director-AI role partners with global business leadership; Mattel's Head of AI partners with the corporate AI strategy office; Dell's APAC commentary explicitly describes CIOs working with regional AI practitioners as the maturity model for enterprise AI.
- **It works regardless of TTI's eventual decision on a global CAIO.** Today, the role partners with whoever owns the AI agenda (CEO / Deputy CFO / divisional CIO). If TTI later appoints a global CAIO, the role becomes that seat's regional lieutenant rather than competing for it.

### What I bring

- US$300M+ in enterprise transformation delivered, consistently with zero-disruption outcomes
- The cross-functional certification stack the work requires — enterprise architecture (TOGAF), AI strategy and governance (Azure AI, UPenn AI Strategy & Governance), IT cost management (CTBME / FinOps), security architecture (CISSP), programme delivery (SAFe, Prince2)
- 30+ years operating under US technical leadership while delivering across APAC — the bridge work is something I have done at scale, not in theory
- Hands-on practitioner with AI tools as core delivery infrastructure — not theory, not a Copilot licence on the side

### Engagement models

- **Permanent.** Director, AI (APAC) — reporting to global CIO / CAIO / equivalent US-based executive owner of the AI agenda.
- **Interim.** Same scope on a fixed-term basis — demonstrate the value before committing to permanent headcount.
- **Hybrid.** Start in the consulting model (see companion brief) and convert to permanent or interim when the case is proven.
