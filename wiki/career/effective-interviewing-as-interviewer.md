> *Your job as interviewer is to reveal how candidates perform under pressure — not to make them comfortable.*

tags: [career]

## The Two Failure Modes

1. **Over-helping** — giving hints, steering answers, rescuing a candidate who is struggling. You're not testing them; you're doing the interview for them
2. **Being over-awed** — giving experienced candidates an easy ride because you feel embarrassed asking "basic" questions. Experience doesn't exempt anyone from fundamentals

## Running a Good Interview

- **Test under pressure deliberately** — silence, follow-up challenges, and probing questions reveal how someone actually thinks. Discomfort is data
- **Ask the fundamental questions regardless of seniority** — use a framing line to take the awkwardness away: *"Some of these may seem straightforward given your experience, but we run through them as a formality with everyone."* This is professional, not embarrassing
- **Don't let impressive CVs distort your standards** — a senior candidate who can't explain basics is a red flag, not an exception to overlook
- **People talk fluently about what they've rehearsed** — put them on the spot; that's where you find out what they actually know versus what they've memorised
- **Remember the asymmetry** — a PM can articulate network architecture in a meeting; that doesn't mean you'd let them design one. Subject matter expertise must be verified directly

## Preparation

- Define what a strong answer looks like before the interview, not during it
- Agree scoring criteria with co-interviewers beforehand to avoid post-hoc rationalisation
- See [[working-with-others/always-prepare-for-engagements]] for general preparation principles

## My Pattern

Over-helping instinct leads to steering answers and softening follow-ups. Being over-awed by seniority creates resistance to fundamental questions. The framing line is the practical tool that removes awkwardness.

## Key Takeaways

- Helping candidates in real-time invalidates the assessment
- Senior experience doesn't justify skipping technical verification
- Use a disclaimer line to ask fundamental questions without awkwardness
- Discomfort in an interview is a feature, not a bug — it reveals the real candidate
