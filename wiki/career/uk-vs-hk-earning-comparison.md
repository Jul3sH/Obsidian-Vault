# UK vs Hong Kong: Where Is It Easier for Julian to Earn? (July 2026)

> **The decision question, finally head-to-head.** The [[uk-150k-feasibility-report|UK feasibility report]] answered the UK side; a parallel HK research pass answered Hong Kong. **Both sides were Fable adversarially reviewed** (the HK pass carried two *stay-leaning* magnitude inflations, corrected here — so this comparison, honestly done, does **not** flatter the move). Candidate + roles per [[target-role-profile|Target-Role Profile]]. Costs are roughly a wash between the two cities ([[uk-vs-hk-cost-comparison|Test A]]), so the question reduces cleanly to **income-probability + tax**, which is what this covers.
>
> Two bars, as established: the **cost-covering floor** (a job that stops the drawdown) and the **well-paid target** (£150k+ gross / ~HK$1.5M+). Currency ~HK$10 = £1 (approximate).

Clarification pass: [[uk-vs-hk-earning-comparison-jh-clarifications-2026-07-09]] answers Julian's follow-up questions on the HK floor, runway, role-volume evidence, and confirmed HK PR.

Income-floor reconciliation: [[uk-vs-hk-income-floor-reconciliation-2026-07-09]] found the earlier "HK has no cheap floor" wording was too strong. HK$100k/month gross looks survivable once Cecil Road rent is offset; it is better described as a **weak recovery floor**, not an unlivable floor. This does not make HK clearly superior, but it narrows the UK floor advantage and needs adversarial review before the headline verdict is hardened.

## Verdict up front

| Bar | Easier in… | Why (one line) |
|---|---|---|
| **Cost-covering floor** | **UK, but narrower than first stated** | UK floor sits below an abundant median; HK$100k/month can be survivable with Cecil Road rent offset, but is a weak recovery floor in a cooling market. |
| **Well-paid target (£150k+)** | **Wash** | HK pays *better if landed* (~one quartile higher band position + ~1.4x net after tax); the UK is *safer to land* (more seats, age law, right-to-work certainty). |

*Wash = neither option dominates; the advantages cancel. HK pays more if you land a role; UK makes landing more probable. Expected value is roughly equal.*

**Bottom line:** income is **not** a decisive reason to move. On the floor the UK is safer; on the well-paid target it is roughly even, with HK holding a real economic edge *conditional on landing a role in a cooling, local-first market*. The move has to be justified on its non-income pillars and on risk appetite, not on "I'll earn more/more-easily in the UK."

## Bar 1 — Cost-covering floor: UK is easier

| | UK (London/West-Corridor + remote) | Hong Kong |
|---|---|---|
| Role volume (his families) | High; ~5-18x HK | ~10x fewer seats (single 7.5M city); est. ~150-190 live per family (soft) |
| Market direction | Volume up, pay flat-to-down | **Cooling** - KPMG HK: only 19% of employers expect 2026 headcount growth (weakest since 2020) |
| Where the floor sits | ~£48-75k gross = **below** the architect median, abundant, he's over-qualified | HK$100k/month gross can be survivable once Cecil Road rent is offset; stronger recovery still needs a senior role |
| Cost-covering probability | **High** | Medium (directional; cooling + local-first drag) |

**Verdict: UK easier, but not because HK$100k/month is unlivable.** The corrected read is narrower: UK floor income is easier to find because it sits below the London architect median, while HK$100k/month gross is survivable only after offsetting Cecil Road rent and still leaves a thin recovery margin. The real UK floor risk is "over-qualified" screening, not earning capacity. Runway is about 20 months on non-ISA liquid alone, or about 47 months (~3.9 years) if MPF is successfully withdrawn and treated as transition capital.

## Bar 2 — Well-paid target (£150k+ / ~HK$1.5M): a genuine wash

This is the nuanced one, and it is where the move does **not** win.

### HK is the better-paid outcome *if landed* (two real, verified advantages)
- **Band position (like-for-like, Robert Half both sides):** UK EA London P25/P50/P75 = **£111.5k / £129.5k / £153.75k**; HK EA P25/P50/P75 = **HK$1.0M / 1.5M / 2.16M ≈ £100k / £150k / £216k**. So £150k is **HK P50 vs UK P75** — in HK the well-paid target is a *median* outcome; in the UK it's an *upper-quartile* one. HK is ~one quartile better-positioned. *(Note: on UK job-board data (ITJobsWatch) £150k reads as ~P90 — recruiter bands skew higher; the like-for-like recruiter-to-recruiter comparison above is the fair cross-market one.)*
- **Tax:** HK effective ~14.3% on HK$1.5M vs UK ~39.6% on £150k. On a **like-for-like £150k gross both sides**, HK net take-home is **~1.4x** the UK's (≈ £128.5k net vs ≈ £90.6k net). Real and decisive on economics — but it is **1.4x, not the 1.6-1.8x** the raw research claimed (that figure secretly compared his old HK$1.87M gross against £150k). He has already held this HK band (HK$1.87M ≈ P70).

### The UK is safer to *land* it (why HK's edge is only "if landed")
HK's landing probability for a well-paid role is dragged down by: the cooling market, **local-first hiring** (Cantonese/localisation preference), **no age-discrimination law** at 55, and his **best-fit employer type shrinking** (Telstra exited managed services - the exact telco/SI line he came from - with ~2,800 enterprise cuts). The UK appears to offer materially more absolute well-paid seats, but the exact multiple is directional and needs live validation. The UK also has an age-discrimination law, less-bad market direction, and a local address from day one after relocation. Landing a *well-paid* role is hard in **both** (UK top-decile-ish; HK a cooling-market coin-flip) - this is the same **~45-50% P(large HK role)** the [[uk-move-financial-model|finance model]] assumed.

**Net: wash.** HK = better-paid-if-landed; UK = more seats + safer-to-land. Neither dominates.

## HK right-to-work: confirmed

**HK PR confirmed.** Localisation remains a hiring preference, not a work-permit hurdle. The UK advantage on this point is right-to-work certainty plus a UK address from day one; not an asymmetry on work-permit access.

## What this means for the decision

- **Income is not the reason to move.** On the floor the UK is safer; on the well-paid target it's a wash, and HK actually holds an economic edge *if* he can land a role in a shrinking market.
- **It is also not a strong reason to stay.** HK's better-paid-if-landed advantage is gated behind a ~45-50% landing coin-flip made worse by age, localisation, and his employer-type exiting — the very risks the finance model already priced.
- **So the decision rests where it always did:** the non-income pillars (Sophia's schooling, family, lifestyle), the runway (which funds either path for years), and risk appetite (a safer UK floor vs a higher-variance HK jackpot). This comparison removes "I'll earn better/more-easily in the UK" as a load-bearing argument — it isn't true on the well-paid question, and it's only true on the *floor*.

## Confidence and gaps

- **High confidence:** salary bands both sides (Robert Half UK + HK), the tax differential, HK market cooling, no HK age law, the cost-wash (Test A).
- **Directional only (not model inputs):** all landing probabilities — treat as judgement, not numbers. HK role-volume counts (Glassdoor snapshots; JobsDB unretrieved). HK data is genuinely thinner than the UK's — there is no ITJobsWatch equivalent and no public per-posting applicant counts.
- **Open:** HK network-security-specific volume and presales OTE (unquantified).
- **Tilt check (Fable):** the HK research's only material inflations were *stay-leaning* (band-percentile mismatch + the tax multiple), both corrected above; no move-leaning inflation of comparable size was found. This comparison, corrected, does not flatter either choice.

## Key Takeaways

- **Cost-covering floor: the UK is easier, but the previous wording was too strong.** HK$100k/month gross is not obviously unlivable once Cecil Road rent is offset; it is a weak recovery floor. UK still wins the floor because the required role is below median and the landing surface is broader.
- **Well-paid target: a wash.** HK pays better *if landed* (£150k is HK-median vs UK-upper-quartile, and ~1.4x net after tax); the UK is *safer to land* (more seats, age law, local UK address after relocation).
- **Income is not a decisive reason to move** — nor a strong reason to stay (HK's edge is gated behind a ~45-50% landing coin-flip worsened by age + localisation + his employer-type exiting).
- **The HK edge is honestly ~1.4x net and ~one quartile** - not the inflated figures the raw research first gave; the inflations were stay-leaning and are corrected.
- **The decision now rests on the non-income pillars + runway + risk appetite,** not on earnings.
