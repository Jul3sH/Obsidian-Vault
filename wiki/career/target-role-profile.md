**Single-domain-expert distinction (matters for contract bids):** the resume positions Julian as a *single-domain expert* only in **networking and network-security** (years of titled, hands-on evidence). In **cloud** and **general security** he holds the skills and certifications (AWS SA, CISSP) but the resume does not read as a single-domain specialist. Implication: pure single-domain contract bids are strongest in network/network-security; cloud and security convert better inside a cross-domain or leadership pitch than as standalone specialist bids.

## Employment mode

**Decision rule: maximise total economic value (cash + benefits), not title or career progression.** At this career stage earnings are the priority; title and climbing are not. Whether perm or contract wins is decided on money, not on which is perceived as more attainable.

**"Well paid" threshold: £150k+ gross (before tax)** is the floor that defines a role as worth taking; below it a role does not meet the economic goal regardless of title. **Feasibility reality (per [[uk-150k-feasibility-report|UK £150k Feasibility]], Jul 2026):** £150k is a **top-decile UK outcome, not the base case** (expected £85-130k perm / £120-145k contract salary-equivalent). It is reachable only via specific lanes - top-of-band Enterprise Architect / Head-of perm, consultancy senior EA, BFSI lead, or principal presales OTE - and **contract does not clear it net-of-benefits** (a headline day-rate loses to a perm package once the post-cancer insurance gap is netted off). Treat £150k as a **stretch target requiring lane discipline**, not an expected outcome; whether to hold it as a hard floor or accept it as a longer-horizon target is Julian's call.

- **Benefits are a first-class factor, and they favour permanent.** Medical and life insurance matter heavily because they are effectively **unobtainable independently due to previous cancer history** - insurance companies will not write independent policies post-cancer. A perm package including them carries real hidden value a headline contract day-rate does not. Always compare **net of benefits**, never gross day-rate vs gross salary.
- **Managing and leading technical teams is a genuine strength and a wanted feature** (90%+ team satisfaction, Team of the Year, talent management at scale) - this points to permanent leadership roles (Head of Architecture / practice lead), not solo contracting.
- **Ideal job: a high-paid permanent role with medical + life insurance + management.**
- **Contract is still researched co-equally** because it may in some cases earn more or be more attainable at 55 - but the bar it must clear is *total economic value including the benefits it does not provide*. The single-domain-vs-cross-domain competition risk (above) applies mainly to contract.

Net: preference tilts **permanent**, driven by money-plus-benefits and the management strength, with contract kept live as a money-maximising alternative to be tested, not a default.

## Location and commutability

Base: **Wimbledon, SW London.** Only two categories of role are viable:

- **Fully remote (UK-wide)** - no commute.
- **Commutable on-site** - office in **London or the West Corridor** (M3/M4: Reading, Basingstoke, Slough and nearby tech hubs), daily-commutable from Wimbledon. Typical hybrid = ~3 days/week at that office.

**Excluded entirely:** any role requiring on-site presence not daily-commutable from Wimbledon (e.g. Manchester, Edinburgh, Bristol). Not counted as available, even if otherwise a strong fit, unless it is offered fully remote. Within the viable set, full-office, hybrid, or fully remote are all acceptable.

## Positioning rules (apply to every application and the resume)

1. **AI reinforces, never leads.** Julian is not AI-certified and is not selling himself as an AI engineer. Frame the sabbatical as capability built *for network/security/operations automation*, differentiating him from peers, not as a career pivot.
2. **Credential order: lead with CCIE + CISSP + TOGAF.** CCIE is the master-level networking credential, directly relevant to SASE (network + network security), so it leads the technical stack; CISSP carries security breadth, TOGAF architecture governance. Nuance: CCIE is a premium signal at SIs/telcos and for network-security/SASE roles but can read as "legacy" at cloud-first enterprises - lead hard with it for Primary B / network-security roles, and pair it with CISSP + TOGAF for cross-domain ESA and general-security applications so it does not keyword-route into a lower pure-network band.
3. **Presales revenue is the commercial hook.** "Architects who close revenue" is rare and valued provider-side.
4. **BFSI depth is stated as client/sector experience, and its sufficiency is flagged, not assumed** (see below).
5. **Age is a documented factor in UK hiring discrimination.** Do not hide it, but mitigate its salience by leading with currency: Global Head title (recent, not legacy), AI sabbatical work (modern capability), and sector depth (continuously relevant). These are signals of ongoing capability and relevance, not obsolescence.
6. **Lead with team leadership where the role has a management dimension.** Managing and leading technical architecture teams (90%+ satisfaction, Team of the Year, talent management at scale) is a differentiator that junior specialists and pure-IC contractors lack - foreground it for Head-of / practice-lead / permanent leadership roles.
7. **Compete in the niche lane, never as a generic architect.** The UK's market-size advantage over HK is *likely* real **only** in the rare-stack niche (CCIE + CISSP + hands-on SASE + presales + leadership) and is **contingent on that niche talent pool being genuinely scarce** (asserted, not yet evidenced - recruiter to confirm); presenting as a generic Solution Architect or pure-cloud specialist drops him into the oversupplied median glut where any advantage evaporates (per [[uk-150k-feasibility-report|feasibility report]]). Lane discipline makes the UK income case *possible*, not guaranteed.

## Load-bearing assumptions - feasibility-research verdicts (+ residual validation)

1. **BFSI third-party vs direct — VERDICT: MIXED, leans HOLDS for perm.** Most JDs ask for experience *within* financial services (domain/compliance), which the bank-embedded BT/AT&T/Telstra work satisfies; hard "must have worked *for* a bank" is the exception. Banks curbing PSC contractors post-IR35 shrinks the BFSI *contract* premium and favours the *perm* route. Fix: frame as "delivered under [bank] banking compliance," not "employed by." Residual: sample live BFSI JDs to quantify how often the hard gate appears.
2. **Age discrimination delta: UK vs HK — VERDICT: legal delta HOLDS, hiring-outcome delta UNPROVEN; NOT the core mover.** Verified: HK has no age-discrimination law (and no plan to legislate); the UK has the Equality Act 2010. But that legal gap does *not* translate into a measured hiring-probability gain at 55, and UK tech ageism is endemic (61% of IT workers see prejudice; "too old" ~55). So age is a **legitimate secondary argument, not "the core mover"** it was previously called. The real pro-UK anchor is **market size in the niche lane + right-to-work**, not age - do not lead the relocation rationale on age. (Full detail + sources: [[uk-150k-feasibility-report|feasibility report]].)
3. **Contract single-domain competition at 55 — VERDICT: MIXED.** Breadth reads as over-qualified/over-priced on commoditised single-domain contracts (no breadth premium); it wins only where scarcity is genuine (network-security / SASE). Take breadth to **perm Head-of**; take contract only into the scarce niche lane. Note: contract also fails the £150k net-of-benefits test (see Employment mode).
4. **HK location filter — VERDICT: HOLDS while HK-based, MOOT once relocated.** An HK address is a binary pre-skills screen; it disappears with a UK (Wimbledon) address + right-to-work, except cleared/DV roles. During the transition: "relocating to London, available [date]," right-to-work confirmed.
