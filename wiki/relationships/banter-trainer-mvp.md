# Banter Trainer (for Sophia) - MVP Scoping

Working scope for the parked funnel idea: give [[sophia|Sophia]] a usable way to
practise responding to UK-style banter, coaching her toward a small set of
rehearsed "stay cool, don't take the bait" moves ahead of the
[[uk-relocation-decision|UK move]]. Parked in [[funnel|the project funnel]] under
Relationships.

## Objective and scope guardrail

**The objective is a usable thing for Sophia. Full stop.** Not a lab experiment,
not a build project, not an AI-engineering exercise. The simplest path that gets
her practising wins - and that may well be **just using or lightly enhancing an
existing app** (Tough Tongue AI), not building anything.

Guard against drift (a known over-complication tendency): if a step doesn't make
Sophia's practice more usable *sooner*, it is out of scope for the MVP.

Two use cases, kept apart on purpose:

1. **MVP - usable for Sophia (this doc, Relationships).** Simplest path.
   Success = Sophia has something she'll actually use. Solution is open: use an
   existing app, enhance one, or a thin custom harness - whichever is fastest to
   usable.
2. **Productized app (later, Performance - only if warranted).** Considered
   *only if* the MVP proves useful to other people **and** building it out is
   not a distraction from the main purpose (getting a job). If both hold,
   promote a separate Project under Performance via `/project-planner`. Until
   then this stays a parenting tool, not a venture.

The substance below (five moves, rubric, scenarios) is the *content* Sophia
practises - it applies whichever solution the MVP uses.

## Design spine: the five rehearsed moves

The core skill is **not** the clever line. It is the emotional non-reaction -
denying the other kid the reaction they are fishing for. The moves are just
vehicles for that. Sophia rehearses these five to reflex; the coach always
steers her back onto them rather than inventing a new zinger each time (brains
cannot retrieve a hundred lines under fire; they can run five to reflex).

| # | Move | Sticky name | Mechanism | Example reply |
|---|------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Bored deflection | **"So?"** | Deny the reaction; flat and unbothered | "ok" / "and?" / "so?" |
| 2 | Agree & amplify | **"Own it bigger"** | Agree and pile on yourself, harder than they did - removes the ammo | "Yeah, worse in person." |
| 3 | Beat them to it | **"Say it first"** | Name your own soft spot before they can | "I know, gave up on my hair this morning." |
| 4 | Flip it | **"Why do you care?"** | Calm, curious question back - puts it on them without heat | "Why're you so bothered about it?" |
| 5 | Bin it | **"Move on"** | Disengage: one beat, then turn to a friend / carry on | shrug + change subject |

Co-designing these *with* Sophia (let her pick her own five, name them, tweak
the examples) is worth more than handing her a finished list - they need to be
**hers** to be retrievable.

## Coaching rubric

The coach scores *her* reply on four dimensions, in priority order, then gives
one thing to change and the move it maps to.

1. **Cool held?** (primary) Did she avoid giving the reaction they wanted - no
   visible sting, no upset, no over-explaining? This is the whole game.
2. **Short?** Long, defensive replies are blood in the water. One line beats a
   paragraph.
3. **Bait refused?** Did she avoid defending the insecurity, and avoid
   escalating into a fight or a wound?
4. **Move used?** Which of the five did her reply map to - and if none, which
   one would have fit best?

**Coaching output shape (every round):**
- One line on what worked.
- **One** thing to change (never a list - one).
- The rehearsed move it maps to, by sticky name.
- One or two bankable lines she could reuse.
- Never reward cruelty back or escalation. "Cool and boring" beats "savage".

**Convergence principle:** the coach must pull her *toward the same five moves
repeatedly*, not expand her repertoire. Repetition to reflex is the goal, not
variety.

## Safeguarding (non-negotiable in the design)

Not everything is banter to be "won". The app must teach the difference between
banter and bullying, and model that **some bait you exit, you don't answer**:

- Repeated targeting, cruelty about family/appearance, threats, or an exclusion
  campaign = bullying, not banter. The move there is **exit + tell an adult**,
  not a comeback.
- Sophia must not learn that she has to defeat every jab. Given her RSD /
  rejection-sensitivity pattern (see [[sophia]]), "I don't have to win this one,
  I can walk and tell someone" is a *success* state, not a failure.
- Family landmines (scenario 10, the [[clodagh|Clodagh]]-type jab) are the
  clearest case: model the calm exit, never a witty counter.

## Pre-build conditions

Two things to do / decide **before** the first build session. Not funnel fields,
not Jira tasks yet - they live here so they resurface at promotion.

1. **Sophia's buy-in - design for it, don't assume it.** Given her RSD pattern,
   a tool that "corrects" her can land as criticism and backfire. Design the
   tone so it feels like a game she is *beating*, not a deficit she is fixing:
   score/streak on "cool held", let her set difficulty, celebrate the
   non-reaction, avoid "you did that wrong" language, and co-design the five
   moves *with* her (see the spine above). Her buy-in is a real dependency, not
   a nicety - if it feels like a lesson, she won't use it.
2. **Start in Tough Tongue AI (free 25 min) - and it may be the MVP itself.**
   Feed it the five moves + a few scenarios and let Sophia try it. If the loop
   works for her, stop there: that is a usable MVP with zero build. Only drop to
   a custom harness if it genuinely falls short. What to watch: does the timed
   pressure land, does the coaching help or nag, does she actually want to use
   it again.

## MVP solution options (simplest wins)

Ordered by speed-to-usable. Pick the first that gets Sophia practising; don't
build more than the objective needs.

1. **Use Tough Tongue AI as-is.** Free 25 min, then $12-20/mo. Feed it the five
   moves + scenarios as context. If it's good enough, this *is* the MVP - no
   build. Fastest path.
2. **Lightly enhance / configure an existing app.** Same, with custom scenarios
   - note custom scenarios sit on their Business tier ($299/mo), so only if the
   free/standard config falls short.
3. **Thin custom harness (fallback, not default).** Only if 1-2 don't give her
   something usable. Haiku 4.5 (~$3/mo), text-first, voice later. Loop:
   scenario + banter line in persona -> countdown -> coach against the rubric.
   This is also the natural starting point *if* it later graduates to the
   Performance productization track.

Whichever path: the five moves and the coaching rubric are the content; the
safeguarding rule is non-negotiable in all of them.
