## Who she is

### Identity
- Self-identifies as **sporty, academic, and smart** (now owns the academic label, which is good)
- Strong ambition: **wants to act** in film versions of her favourite novels; wants to be famous to "show everybody who got at her" - the revenge narrative under the dream is load-bearing (she uses ambition as armour against rejection)
- Loves reading (hyperfocuses), music, and acting; does NOT identify as a "cool girl" - no makeup, no boyfriends, her identity is achiever not socialite
- **Body conscious** - bigger build than her peer group who are very slim; currently working on this with Julian's support in a healthy way; this is a live vulnerability, especially in a new school PE environment

### Personality pattern
- **Extroverted energy:** talking charges her up; she initiates contact, pops in for chit-chat, comes alive when she has an audience
- **Uncertain in new situations:** the extroversion drops away when she doesn't know the rules; she needs familiarity before she can lead
- **Tries to lead among peers, often gets rejected, then follows.** The core social pattern: she comes in enthusiastically with ideas (same trait as Julian and his mother), gets shut down or ignored, and takes it to heart. Watching her in groups, she follows in practice even though she tries to lead in theory.
- **Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) pattern** - Julian's read is correct. Intelligent, sporty, strong-willed girls attract jealousy; she has been repeatedly targeted for being "too much" (too clever, too sporty, too confident). The RSD may be standalone or may sit alongside ADHD traits - note that she hyperfocuses on reading and performs well academically, which argues against classic ADHD, but the social rejection sensitivity is real and should be treated as its own thing regardless of label.
- **Stands up for herself and is a good logical arguer.** This is a real strength - she has backbone and doesn't collapse under pressure, even though she feels the rejection deeply.

### Emotional processing style
- **Delayed processor.** She doesn't come to Julian immediately when something is wrong. If she's not ready to talk, she'll go quiet, process internally, and then open up - often at bedtime. Trying to force the conversation before she's ready will close her down.
- **Pattern under stress:** shuts down first, gets angry second, talks it through when ready. Not a crier in front of Julian in normal circumstances - he's rarely seen her cry. When she does cry, it signals something has genuinely landed hard.
- **Two crying events so far:** (1) when Julian mentioned having a girlfriend - fear of losing him to someone else; (2) today, when she saw signs of Joanne at the house AND heard "Wimbledon" from Julian. Both times: fear of losing her primary anchor.
- **Catastrophising tendency:** irrational fears of flying and boats (inherited in part from Clodagh; reinforced by anxiety pattern). When her mind runs away with her, it runs far. This is worth naming to her directly - she can learn to spot the spiral.

## Her relationships

### With Julian
- **The central and most secure attachment in her life.** She prefers talking to Julian over Clodagh; she comes home to Julian; she tells him she loves him unprompted; the bedtime cuddle is a non-negotiable anchor (she gets visibly upset if it doesn't happen).
- **She is hyper-attuned to his emotional state.** If Julian is distracted or stuck in his head, she notices immediately and asks "Is something wrong?" or "Are you angry with me?" - she scans for rupture in this relationship constantly. This isn't neediness; it's the vigilance of a child whose world has been uncertain before.
- Julian gives her space and freedom; she comes to him on her own terms and he always receives her when she does. This has built trust over years.
- **She will suppress her own distress to protect Julian.** She knows he has a lot to carry. She will "go along and try to make it work." This is her strength AND the reason Julian needs to create explicit permission for her NOT to be okay.
- She was reassured when Julian left Joanne in a restaurant to come home to her ("you always come first"). That framing worked. It is a commitment that will need to be demonstrated more than once.

### With Clodagh
- **Complicated, painful, and currently strained.** Sophia is angry with Clodagh for being an alcoholic. She's scared of her when she's drunk or hungover. She had to go through school counselling after living in a household with Clodagh and a violent partner.
- **Triggers:** conflict, drunk people, raised voices. These cause panic, not just discomfort. This is a real trauma response.
- **She does NOT see Clodagh's UK proximity as an upside right now.** She was "genuinely concerned" when she thought Julian might be dating Clodagh again - this reads as fear that Clodagh would have power over Julian and destabilise the household, not just teenage embarrassment.
- **But there is warmth when it's safe.** When Julian invited Clodagh for dinner, Sophia was initially sulky (not about being a teenager - specifically about Clodagh being there) but warmed up significantly as the evening went on. She showed off for both parents together. She clearly still wants the family unit feeling; she just needs to trust that Clodagh won't blow it up. That trust has to be earned by Clodagh over time, not assumed.
- **The recovery arc matters long-term.** If Clodagh recovers and is stable, the London proximity could eventually become a genuine positive for Sophia. This is a 12-18 month play, not a tonight play.

### With Joanne
- Knows Joanne exists and knows she is the Jojo Bake cake-maker; has not formally connected the two
- The primary anxiety is: **will Joanne take Julian's attention and love away from her?** This is existential for Sophia given that Julian is her entire safety system.
- The reassurance that worked: "you always come first - I left her in a restaurant to be with you." This is the language she understands.
- Today she saw evidence of Joanne at the house and cried. This is a live, unresolved anxiety - not something one reassurance conversation has settled. It will need demonstrating over time, not just stating.
- They have not meaningfully met. Introduction, if it happens, needs to be on Sophia's timeline with no pressure.

## Her social world (Hong Kong)

### Current state: disillusioned, not heartbroken
- **The HK attachment is weaker than it appears on the surface.** She's not leaving a thriving social life; she's leaving a complicated one she's become somewhat disillusioned with.
- Two main friends who are individually fine but **gang up against her when together** (subtle bullying - always agreeing with each other and disagreeing with her; both jealous of her achievements). She's sensitive to this but hasn't fully exited the friendships.
- Jiani: fun, different, less academic - a lighter friendship without as much weight.
- **She has never had a "best friend" that was exclusively hers.** She's always been the alternative choice. This is the wound under the social anxiety.
- She romanticises past relationships that were actually imperfect or even bullying-heavy. Previous school was worse than current - but she's starting to remember it fondly. This is the rose-tinted-spectacles pattern: when the present disappoints, the past becomes golden. **This will happen with HK once she's in London.**
- Mia (now gone) and the "popular best friend from years ago" are the relationships she keeps referencing. Neither is currently available to her.

### The HK departure cost: real but limited
- She's not leaving a golden social chapter. She's leaving a complicated, somewhat painful one.
- The cost of departure is lower than Julian feared. The risk is less "grief for what I'm losing" and more "I'll have to do the hard thing of building again, and what if it goes the same way?"

## Her London pull factors

### Edie
- Real connection; Edie was excited about Sophia coming. This is significant - a 12-year-old peer enthusiastically wanting you to come is more powerful than any adult reassurance. **Getting Edie on the phone is probably the single highest-impact practical action Julian can take** - but do it AFTER the initial conversation so Sophia feels heard first, not sold to.
- Edie's dad Jamie is one of Julian's best friends - the social bridge is already there.

### Acting and London's cultural environment
- **Completely untapped in conversations with Sophia so far.** Her ambition is to act in film versions of her favourite books. London is the global capital of British acting: West End, RADA, youth drama schools, film culture, casting. HK has no equivalent scene for a serious young actress. This could reframe the move from "loss" to "the only place this dream actually has a runway." This is worth introducing - but not tonight.

### Being English in England
- She has already started to "lean into being English" - her HK school is predominantly Asian and she has felt somewhat out of place. Going to an English school in London removes that misfit feeling entirely. She belongs there in a way she has never quite belonged at DBIS.

## Key vulnerabilities to watch

1. **RSD replaying in new school.** If she tries to lead and gets shut out again, she'll spiral. The intervention is finding her tribe (sport, drama) rather than trying to lead the general social hierarchy first.
2. **Body image + new PE class.** Her physical self-consciousness is live. A new school means being physically appraised by strangers. Her sport achievements are actually an IN - frame it that way proactively.
3. **The rose-tinted HK retrospective.** Within months of being in London, HK may become "the perfect life she left." Help her build an accurate picture of HK now so she has a realistic anchor.
4. **Joanne as a parallel threat.** The move and Joanne are entangled in her mind. She may resist London partly because London = Julian's new life = less of him for her. This needs continued demonstrating, not just telling.
5. **Clodagh's departure from HK.** Even though she's angry, Clodagh leaving HK is also a loss - her mum's physical geography is changing too. She may not have processed this and it may hit later.
6. **She performs strength for Julian.** Her default is to suppress distress and "make it work for Dad." Julian should actively create permission to not be okay.
