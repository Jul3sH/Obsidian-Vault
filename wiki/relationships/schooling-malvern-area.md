# Schooling - Malvern Area

Schools in and around Malvern for Sophia's education (Year 8 onwards), researched as part of the [[uk-relocation-decision]] Malvern interim scenario.

## Admissions Process

**In-year entry (Year 8 and mid-year transfers):**
1. Complete the CA1 in-year application
2. Send it to **Worcestershire Children First School Admissions**
3. Contact schools directly for tours and to confirm availability

**Key dates:**
- School term starts: **3 September**
- Available for tour appointment: ~7 September onwards

## ⚠ Risk E1 - late in-year application misses the Sept place

> Raised 15 Jul after reading the Chase admission policy. Scored in the [[uk-relocation-decision]] risk register's comparative format (Impact x Probability, 1-5, across the three options). A **new, significant** risk to the Malvern path.

- **Cause:** Family in HK until 28 Jul; Worcestershire processes standard in-year applications only after May half-term and advises applying mid-June; July / school-holiday applications carry "no guarantee" of processing for a September start.
- **Event:** The in-year application lands too late to be processed for a 3 Sept 2026 start.
- **Effect:** **Sophia misses her preferred place and starts at a school she would be unhappy in.** In Malvern the fallback is **Dyson Perrins** (Attainment 8 41.6, no sixth form, thinnest girls' sport of the three); in London the fallback floor is Raynes Park. Either way: academic + sporting setback and, if she starts at the fallback then moves, a second disruptive transition.

| Stay-HK | 1 | 1 | 1 | Keeps her current place; no application. Not applicable. |

- **Key mitigation:** Submit the Worcestershire in-year application NOW from HK (do not wait for 28 Jul); get the Education History Form from her current school before term ends; chase Sarah Cridge for the waiting-list size + written confirmation a summer application is processed for 3 Sept; keep Hanley Castle (catchment-prioritised via the Hanley Swan address) as co-first. - **Residual / Julian's judgment:** **Material to London-vs-Malvern.** It bites the Malvern path hardest and undercuts the very thing that was pulling toward Malvern, Sophia's excitement about The Chase. If the Chase place is likely to be missed on timing, the Malvern pull weakens and "settle her once in London" strengthens. If she lands at Dyson Perrins she would accept a later move to London, but that is exactly the **double-disruption** Fable named as London-direct's winning argument, so it is a cost either way. Immaterial to Move-vs-Stay. Live status: awaiting Sarah Cridge's waiting-list reply.

## Sports & Academics Comparison (key findings)

Source: [[malvern-state-schools-comparison]] - full Perplexity report with metrics, facilities tables, and sources.

| | The Chase | Dyson Perrins | Hanley Castle |
|-|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| **Ofsted** | Good (sixth form Outstanding) | Good | Good |
| **Attainment 8** | 50.8 | 41.6 (below England avg) | **50.9 (top of the three)** |
| **Progress 8** | +0.08 | +0.05 | **+0.23 (best)** |
| **Age range** | 11-18 (sixth form) | 11-16 (**no sixth form**) | 11-18 (sixth form) |
| **Athletics** | Curriculum only | Curriculum only | **Dedicated 10-lane 400m track** |
| **Competitive netball** | U12/U13 teams | Fewer published teams | **U12-U18 teams, ex-international coach** |
| **Girls' football** | In curriculum; girls-only teams less visible | KS3 club (mixed) | **County U16 cup winners** |
| **Swimming** | No on-site pool (leisure-centre partnership) | No confirmed pool | No pool, but Inter-College Swimming Gala |

**What this resolves for Sophia (straight-A, football + athletics):**
- **Hanley Castle is the strongest all-round** - best academics (Progress 8 +0.23), best competitive girls' netball and football, and the only school with a dedicated athletics track. This is the school we are in catchment for and prioritised on via the Hanley Swan address. The report directly supports pushing hardest for Hanley Castle.
- **The Chase** is the best academic school in Malvern proper (3rd in Worcestershire) with the widest breadth of unusual sports (equestrian, kayaking, scuba, sailing), and now has **a confirmed Year 8 place** - the strong, available fallback.
- **Dyson Perrins concern CONFIRMED** - the report validates Emma's flag: it is the smallest school (~125/year), weakest on competitive girls' sports, and has **no sixth form** (transfer required at 16). Warm pastoral ethos, but the weakest fit for a sporty, academic girl.
- **Swimming is weak across all three** - none has an on-site pool; investigate Malvern Splash / local clubs separately if swimming matters.
