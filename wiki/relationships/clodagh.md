# Clodagh

> Julian's ex-wife and Sophia's mother. The source of his marriage-related fears - and, unexpectedly, a soft emotional pull toward the UK. Captured 2026-07-02 for the [[b1-relationship-belief|UK-move B1 belief test]].

## The relationship

- Ex-**wife**; ~10 years younger than Julian (a much smaller age gap than [[joanne]]).
- Struggled with **addiction**. Julian supported her relentlessly through it for years, despite being "kicked around," and despite it being **her choice to leave** the marriage.
- **He still deeply loves and cares for her.** He often wishes they could be back together.
- **Now:** they get on when not fighting (recently had lunch together). His settled read is that they are **better as good friends** - he doesn't think they could have a sexual/romantic relationship again: too familiar, they'd fight, and living with her would not be stress-free.
- **He still cares for her immensely and in many ways loves her**, alongside a live financial/court dispute (she has not contributed properly to Sophia for ~4 years; see below). Both things are true at once.

## Current situation (7 Jul 2026)

- **Addiction has come to a head in the last year.** Her partner left her; she is, in Julian's words, "completely destructing." He has tried for years to get her to leave HK to recover.
- **She is now returning to Ireland - for good** - to live with her parents and get sober. Julian largely convinced her; he believes prolonged recovery near her parents is her best path back.
- **If she recovers**, she could resume being a mother to Sophia and start contributing financially (she is ~10 years younger, with working years ahead) - lifting some of the burden Julian has carried alone. *Currently modelled at £0 (see [[sophia]]); any contribution is upside, contingent on recovery.*
- Julian gave her a **London property in the divorce - on the same street, ~5 minutes from Cecil Road.** So a recovered Clodagh could later live back there, minutes from him and Sophia. On the call where he raised this, he felt it give her a renewed sense of **purpose**, and felt a change in her.

## Bearing on the UK/London move

- **Non-contingent and important:** Clodagh is leaving HK *regardless*. So the move does not separate Sophia from her mother - staying in HK would (mother gone to Ireland anyway). **The UK brings Sophia onto the same landmass as her recovering mother; HK would not.** This removes a latent anti-move consideration and is a genuine pro-move point.
- **Contingent upside (do not over-weight):** *if* Clodagh recovers and returns to London, the same-street property enables an effortless mother-daughter proximity that **Malvern could never offer** - a real London-specific benefit for Sophia. But it rests on a hard, uncertain recovery; London must stand (and does, per [[fable-review-london-vs-malvern|Fable]]) **without** it.

## Why she matters to the decision

- **She is the root of the marriage fear.** Julian's core anxiety - that "people change once they get married and secure" - comes from this marriage. It's a *pattern from experience*, which is why it must be separated from evidence about any current partner (see [[relationship-views]] and [[b1-relationship-belief]]).
- **A latent UK pull.** The romantic fantasy of "going back to London and carrying on where we left off" is one he names and then dismisses as unrealistic - but it's evidence that the UK is *not* the emotional relationship-desert that belief B1 assumes. There is warmth and companionship history located there.
- **A data point on age gap.** The relationship that endures as genuine affection is the *smaller* age gap (~10 years), which he weighs against wanting someone 20 years younger. See the younger-woman calculus in [[relationship-views]].

## Key Takeaways

- The change-after-security marriage fear traces to Clodagh, not to [[joanne]] - a crucial separation when testing whether the marriage fear should block anything.
- Clodagh represents a **UK-located companionship pull** (even if only as friends), which softens rather than supports the "single/isolated in the UK" belief.
- Best understood now as a close friendship and co-parent, not a live romantic option.
