# Relationship Dynamics: Julian, Wendy & Hilary

> Analysis of the three-way relationship dynamics around Felix Hart's care and estate, grounded in the documented evidence in [[dad-finance-discussions]], [[dad-health-discussions]], the 2011 Will (`raw/Felix Hart Will.md`), and the profiles [[wendy-profile]] / [[hilary-profile]]. Compiled for legal/strategic reference.

## The structural fact at the centre of everything

The 2011 Will does **not** disinherit Julian on its face:
- **Clause 6** — Wendy receives **all jointly-owned property** outright (outside the residue).
- **Clause 7.2** — the **residue** is split **50/50** between Wendy and Julian.
- **Clause 4** — Hilary receives **£10,000**.

Functional disinheritance is produced by the **interaction** of two things:
1. The high-value property is **joint** and therefore bypasses the residue entirely (goes 100% to Wendy under clause 6).
2. The **residue** (the only pool Julian shares in) is **liquid cash that is being depleted** by Felix's living costs (HK$37k/month) and now very large medical bills.

So by the time of death, Julian's "50%" is 50% of a pool approaching zero. Julian's own estimate: ~£100k / ~10% now, falling to ~£66k / ~4% in 18 months, and trending to ~0%. **This is why Julian describes himself as disinherited despite the 50/50 clause.** Wendy is both the sole executor/trustee (clause 3.1) and the beneficiary who gains from the residue being depleted rather than preserved.

## 1. Julian ↔ Wendy — adversarial, asymmetric, legally fraught

**State:** Broken-down and adversarial. Was functional/cordial until the will structure came to light (Feb 2023).

**Power asymmetry:** Wendy holds every lever — control of Felix's finances, control of medical information, control of physical access to Felix, executor status, and independent wealth. Julian holds almost none: unemployed, limited cash runway, an 11-year-old dependent (Sophia), health risks, and depleted assets.

**The dynamic:**
- Wendy manages the narrative; Julian challenges it with facts. Each factual challenge (the HK$37k/month, the property equity, the "1% and it could be 0%" moment) is met with a shifting account or a deflection ("playing the victim", deaf ear, "run to your lawyers").
- **The live risk is the contribution request.** Wendy repeatedly invites Julian to "contribute" to Felix's costs. Because she is wealthy and Felix has assets + insurance, the request is not driven by need. Julian (lawyer-confirmed, 6 Feb 2026) treats it as a possible tactical manoeuvre: a written commitment to contribute could later be used against him, and the group chat serves as an evidentiary record.
- Julian's settled position (documented, consistent): *Wendy controls Felix's finances; Felix has his own assets and insurance; Felix expressly said he did not want to burden Julian; therefore Julian will not contribute.* This is the position the [[contribution-response-draft]] is built to defend.

**Strategic posture for Julian:**
- Never agree, in any wording, to a contribution. Avoid emotive or hostile language in writing (it becomes Wendy's evidence).
- Keep every response factual, brief, principled, and free of admissions.
- Treat all written exchanges as if they will be read by Wendy's lawyers.

---

## 2. Julian ↔ Hilary — warm, trusting, but not a war alliance

**State:** Warm and trusting. Hilary is the one family relationship that has remained intact and is Julian's primary confidant on the matter.

**The dynamic:**
- Julian shares his concerns and analysis with Hilary; she listens, sympathises, and corroborates key facts (Felix's distress, his loss of capacity, his true wishes).
- **But Hilary pulls toward peace, not confrontation.** She repeatedly warns Julian that a legal dispute with Wendy would be "disastrous" and that "this is not the time." Her instinct to de-escalate has, on Julian's own account, once talked him into backing off (Narrative 9).
- Hilary also relays Wendy's narrative ("more or less run out of money apparently") — sometimes uncritically — so she is partly a conduit for Wendy's framing.

**Strategic posture for Julian:**
- Hilary is his **most valuable independent witness** — preserve the relationship and her goodwill at all costs.
- Use her for corroboration and emotional support, **not** as a partner in a confrontational strategy; her advice will always lean toward avoiding conflict.
- Never put Hilary in a position where she must choose between Julian and Wendy (Julian's own stated constraint) — it would cost him the witness and the relationship.

---

## 3. Hilary ↔ Wendy — cordial on the surface, Wendy-managed underneath

**State:** Outwardly cooperative and polite. They coordinate over Felix's care in the group chat; Hilary offers to help with costs; Wendy thanks her warmly.

**The dynamic:**
- Wendy actively manages Hilary's perception — positioning herself as Felix's devoted protector and (per Julian) framing Julian as the source of distress. Hilary has voiced concern to Julian about Wendy's "bullying" and been shown emotionally-charged pictures by Wendy.
- Hilary's voluntary offer to contribute to medical costs (2 Feb 2026) gives Wendy a **precedent to cite** when asking Julian ("similar to what Hilary has offered"). Hilary, acting purely out of care, inadvertently strengthens Wendy's leverage over Julian.
- Hilary's conflict-aversion makes her receptive to Wendy's "keep the peace / it's too late" framing, which aligns with Wendy's interest in the will not being challenged.

**Strategic implication:**
- This axis mostly works to Wendy's advantage: it isolates Julian by keeping the one neutral party oriented toward peace and partly within Wendy's narrative.
- Julian cannot disrupt this axis without risking Hilary — so the play is not to turn Hilary against Wendy, but to keep Hilary as an honest witness whose own observations (distress, incapacity) already support Julian's case.

## Key Takeaways
- Functional disinheritance comes from joint property (100% to Wendy, clause 6) **plus** depletion of the cash residue Julian shares — not from the 50/50 clause itself.
- Wendy holds every lever (finances, information, access, executorship, wealth); Julian holds almost none — so the contest is asymmetric and Julian's only safe ground is documented facts and conceding nothing.
- The contribution request is the active threat; treat every written reply as evidence for Wendy's lawyers.
- Hilary is the swing party: a genuine, neutral witness who supports Julian's facts but counsels peace and is partly within Wendy's narrative — preserve her, don't weaponise her.
