## Standing strategy (the non-negotiables)

These are settled and apply to every interaction. Detail and drafts in [[contribution-response-draft]].

1. **Never concede a contribution in any form** - no commitment, no admission of a prior offer, no conditional "if I could" willingness, no engaging on amount. Decline on **principle and fact** (Felix's own assets, Wendy's control of his finances, Felix's express wish not to burden Julian), **never on affordability**.
2. **Don't re-litigate the equity in messages.** Hold the simple line - *"Dad has his own assets"* - and let the documented record of Wendy's shifting equity story speak for itself if ever needed. Anchor fact: her 17 Feb 2023 written line *"we will have to move some equity."*
3. **Decline the decision-making role on care.** Don't endorse, rank, or advise on treatments/placements. Endorsing a costly choice = a basis to argue Julian co-owned it. Defer care decisions to Wendy as the person who manages his finances and care.
4. **Acknowledge, don't accept.** If Wendy asserts something (e.g. "Felix has no assets"), never let silence or a polite reply read as agreement. The model line: *"I note your position; I don't accept it."*
5. **Keep care and money separate.** Every reply keeps a genuine note of care for Dad and refuses the "uninvolved/uncaring son" bait. Presence (visits) does this better than words.
6. **Never sign anything** accepting payment responsibility (admission forms, guarantees, care-home contracts). This - and a formal attorney/guardian role - is the one real HK liability trap. See [[hong-kong-filial-liability]].
7. **Consistency over time is itself a defence.** Never soften the position in a later, friendlier message; one inconsistent line can be quoted against the rest.

### 2026-06-16 - Do NOT respond to Wendy's rehab "affordability" statement

**Trigger:** Wendy (16 June group chat): *"...waiting to hear from the center the confirmations of the cost to see if **I and my family could afford it**, if not Felix will have come to home."* She did not ask Julian for anything.

**Decision:** **Let it stand. No challenge, no request to "put it in writing," no acknowledgement of the money point.** Continue only on the human/visit thread (Father's Day visit). Hold the standard no-contribution response in reserve for *if* she actually asks.

**Rationale:**
- **The statement is favourable as-is.** For the first time Wendy has framed care costs, in writing, in front of Hilary, as *her family's* responsibility, and has not named Julian as a funder. Untouched, that is good for him. You don't improve a favourable line by prodding it.
- **Challenging has zero evidential upside.** The only value of a challenge would be to extract a written admission that Felix has his own assets/can pay. Wendy has declined to put that in writing three times on record (7 Feb 2026 "I noted all the above"; 30 Apr 2026 unanswered "what are your thoughts?"; the 16 Sep 2023 USRC refusal). She won't do it now. Julian predicted exactly this, and he's right.
- **Challenging re-opens a dispute he's currently winning** and invites her to re-frame: pivot to an actual contribution ask, or re-run the "no equity" narrative and muddy the clean record she just created.
- **Proactively raising "Dad has his own assets" backfires here.** With no ask on the table, *Julian* becomes the one dragging a care conversation back to money - which feeds the very "it's about the inheritance for him" narrative he's worried about. State that line only in **response** to a request, never as an opener.
- **The will-case worry is moot.** Silence doesn't meaningfully help her sole-carer narrative (that's fought on the care/involvement axis, which visits cover), and Julian isn't contesting the will anyway.

**Watch item (open loop):** Wendy ended with *"I will let you know,"* paired with affordability framing. If the centre comes back expensive and she circles to "we can't afford it," that may be the runway to a contribution ask. If/when that lands: standard no-contribution response (principle and fact, never affordability), short and non-hostile. Do not pre-empt it.
