# Profile: Hilary (Hilary Ann Leslie Twist / "Auntie Hil")

> Behavioural and motivational profile of Hilary, Felix Hart's sister, built from documented WhatsApp evidence and Julian's analysis notes. Cross-references [[dad-finance-discussions]] and [[dad-health-discussions]]. Compiled for legal/strategic reference.

**Relationship:** Felix Hart's sister. Calls him "Flick". Based in Warwick / Cornwall, UK (the Will lists Long Ridge House, St. Mellion, Cornwall). Caring for her own husband Alan (declining health, frequent reference point in her messages). Maintains **independent relationships with both Julian and Wendy** throughout.

**Role in the Will (2011):**
- Receives a **£10,000 pecuniary legacy** (clause 4) with an unusually affectionate note from Felix ("unconditional love and lifelong friendship").
- May select one memento from Felix's watches/cufflinks (clause 5.1).
- **Third-in-line executor** (after Wendy, then Julian).
- **Long-stop residue beneficiary** (clause 7.6) — inherits the entire residue only if both Wendy's and Julian's shares fail.

## Position and disposition
- **Genuinely concerned for Felix's wellbeing** above all else. This is her consistent driver — not money.
- **Conflict-averse and de-escalating.** Repeatedly urges everyone to avoid arguments about money and warns Julian against legal disputes.
- **A credible third-party witness.** Her messages corroborate key facts and she has no documented financial motive to favour either side.

## Behavioural patterns (evidence-tagged)

### 1. Independent witness who corroborates Julian on capacity
- **[WhatsApp]** 25 Feb 2023 — Felix "very distressed by the discussion about money… incredibly upset for the last few weeks." Corroborates Felix's fragile state during the will-change period.
- **[WhatsApp]** 22 Jul 2023 — "I really don't think he is competent to discuss either his will or finance any more." Independently supports the capacity question central to any undue-influence claim.
- **[WhatsApp]** 15–16 Sep 2023 — relayed Felix's three wishes (Sophia as substitute beneficiary; not wanting Julian/Sophia to get nothing; wanting to discuss together). Important corroboration of Felix's *true* intentions.

### 2. Offered to contribute to medical costs — sets a precedent Wendy cites
- **[WhatsApp]** 2 Feb 2026 — "I am happy to contribute to the cost… but uncertain if the procedure is painful and if there are any risks."
- Note: Wendy then frames her request to Julian as "similar to what Hilary has offered" (6 Feb 2026). Hilary's voluntary offer is being used to normalise the ask to Julian. Julian's position is distinct: Hilary chose to offer; Julian has not, and Felix has his own assets.

### 3. Consistently de-escalates and warns against legal action
- **[WhatsApp]** 6 Feb 2026 — "Please let us all refrain from any arguments about money."; "I think Wendy just wanted to see how you would react. This is not the time to debate the financial situation."
- **[WhatsApp]** 4 May 2026 — "I honestly think getting into any legal dispute with Wendy would be disastrous for you… your Dad would have hated [this]."
- **[Opinion]** Julian's notes flag a risk here: Hilary's "it's too late now" position once "convinced me to back off" (Narrative 9). Her conflict-aversion, while well-meant, has at times worked against Julian pressing his case.

### 4. Reports Wendy's narrative — sometimes uncritically
- **[WhatsApp]** 12 Jan 2024 — relayed Wendy's claim that Felix "has more or less run out of money apparently." Hilary passes on Wendy's version; Julian disputes it ("Financially he's fine, she's lying," 10 Dec 2023).
- **[Opinion]** Julian's notes record Hilary voicing concern about Wendy's "bullying" and being shown emotionally-charged pictures by Wendy — i.e. Hilary is also a target of Wendy's narrative management.

## Strategic assessment
- Hilary is **Julian's most valuable independent witness** — particularly on (a) Felix's distress and (b) Felix's lack of capacity by mid-2023, both of which support an undue-influence / lack-of-capacity analysis.
- **But she is not an ally in conflict.** She will counsel against litigation and toward peace, and she cannot be put in a position that damages her relationship with Wendy (Julian's stated constraint).
- Handle with care: keep her informed and on good terms, preserve her witness value, but **do not rely on her to advance a confrontational strategy.** Her instinct is to smooth things over.
- She has a small contingent financial interest (long-stop residue under 7.6) but it is remote and there is no evidence it motivates her.

## Key Takeaways
- Hilary's sole driver is Felix's wellbeing; she has no financial motive to take sides.
- She is Julian's strongest independent corroborating witness on Felix's distress and loss of capacity (Feb–Jul 2023).
- She is conflict-averse and consistently warns Julian off legal action — valuable counsel, but not an ally for a confrontational approach.
- Her voluntary offer to contribute is being used by Wendy to normalise the ask to Julian — Julian's position is materially different and should be kept so.
- Preserve the relationship and her witness value; never force a choice between Julian and Wendy.
