# Profile: Wendy (Lee Siu Man Wendy / 李少文)

> Behavioural and motivational profile of Wendy Hart, built from documented WhatsApp evidence and Julian's analysis notes. Cross-references the message logs in [[dad-finance-discussions]] and [[dad-health-discussions]]. Compiled for legal/strategic reference.

**Relationship:** Felix Hart's wife (married 1999 per Julian's notes; the Will dates the relationship to June 1995, Clearwater Bay Golf Club). Based in Hong Kong (House 7, Capital Gardens, Sai Kung). Primary carer and de facto controller of Felix's finances since his cognitive decline.

**Role in the Will (2011):**
- **Sole executor and trustee** (clause 3.1). Julian is only the substitute executor if she predeceases or is unwilling.
- Receives **all jointly-owned property** outright (clause 6) — outside the residue pool.
- Receives **50% of the residue** (clause 7.2.1).
- This combination is the mechanism of functional disinheritance — see [[relationship-dynamics]].

## Financial position
- Per Julian: immensely wealthy independently; owns ~20 properties (claimed by Wendy on 24 Feb 2023, **[Recollection]**). Not all her wealth is in property.
- She does **not** need Felix's money. This is central: her conduct is not driven by need.
- Controls Felix's accounts; took over his financial affairs (her own admission, 29 June 2023 **[WhatsApp]**) citing his cognitive decline.

## Julian's characterisation
- **[Opinion]** "A wealthy, vengeful narcissist" (6 Feb 2026). Treats the property equity as hers; systematically transferred Felix's assets "under various guises"; will not spend Felix's money on his granddaughter (£5 toy, Christmas).

## Strategic assessment
- Her financial interest makes her **incapable of acting as a neutral mediator** in any father–son or inheritance discussion — Julian's documented position.
- She does not need the money, which means **need does not explain the contribution requests** — pointing instead to either (a) genuine cash-flow management of Felix's depleting liquid funds, or (b) a tactical attempt to extract a commitment from Julian. The drafted response treats it as the latter risk regardless of intent.
- Any agreement to contribute — even informal, even partial, even sympathetic wording — could be used to assert Julian accepted an obligation. **Never concede this in writing.**

## Key Takeaways
- Wendy is sole executor/trustee and the dual beneficiary of (a) all joint property and (b) 50% residue — she controls both the assets and the process.
- She is independently wealthy and does not need Felix's money; her contribution requests cannot be explained by need.
- Her requests for Julian to contribute are the live legal risk; they are worded to avoid admitting Felix can self-fund.
- Documented pattern: control of finances + denial of assets + information-withholding + recasting Julian's words + litigation threats.
- Rule for Julian: never agree, in any form, to contribute in writing. See [[contribution-response-draft]].
