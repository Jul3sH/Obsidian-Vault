# Julian's Views on Relationships

> Julian's own framework for what he wants from relationships and the recurring fears that shape his choices. "Relationship" here is an umbrella with three elements he weighs differently: **marriage**, **long-term companionship**, and **short-term / casual**. Captured 2026-07-02 as part of the [[b1-relationship-belief|UK-move B1 belief test]]. This is the general view; specific people live in their own profiles ([[joanne]], [[clodagh]], [[hannah]]).

## The core tension: stated preference vs revealed pattern

- **Stated preference:** if money were handled, he'd rather *not* settle down for the next ~5 years - be based in Asia, stay active, have fun, enjoy the abundance of dating options while he still has the energy.
- **Revealed pattern:** he is **not** a transactional man who sleeps around. Historically he ends up in a relationship almost as soon as he meets someone he connects with (met [[hannah]] fast; also [[clodagh]]). He gravitates to connection, not churn.
- **His own conclusion:** there may be a **"false economy"** in the abundance he thinks he wants. What he actually reaches for is *someone half-decent to settle down with* - which cuts against the "play around for five years" fantasy.

## Element 1 - Long-term companionship

- The real long-game goal: a loyal partner who will still be there and look after him as he ages.
- **The younger-woman calculus (a caution he applies to himself):**
  - A partner 20 years younger raises the risk of being "screwed over": gold-digger dynamics, being treated as an ATM for her and her kids, tolerated rather than loved later in life.
  - Data point *against* the big age gap: his ex-wife [[clodagh]] is only ~10 years younger and, when not fighting, they genuinely get on.
  - Emerging realisation: he may not actually want someone 20 years younger.
- **Equal vs hierarchical dynamic:** with [[joanne]] the bond includes a parental/elder-mentor element (he advises, she looks up to him). He worries this erodes as she matures. This surfaced a bigger question: **companionship with a Western woman - a more equal footing - might be more rewarding** than a relationship built partly on a mentor dynamic.

## Element 2 - Marriage (concerns)

The dominant frame is fear, rooted in experience, not this-person evidence:

- **The change-after-security fear (the load-bearing one):** he is genuinely scared people change once married and secure. This is grounded in what happened in his marriage to [[clodagh]], not in anything a current partner has done.
- **Protection is only partial:** even with a prenuptial agreement he could lose money and go through significant stress. "Freeloading isn't enough to save me" - so he fears being pressured into compromising more than he wants, just for peace and quiet, if a partner turns demanding.
- **Autonomy loss:** a spouse could start dictating terms - e.g. where they live (pushing for London when he wants his mum's village) - eroding his freedom.
- **Business-partner risk:** bonding over building a company together is attractive, but running a business together is also a well-known source of stress and conflict - possibly rose-tinted.

## Element 3 - Short-term / casual

- **Where HK scores:** if single, HK offers an abundance of dating options - it's relatively easy to meet an attractive partner. This suits the stated 5-year "have fun" preference.
- **The catch:** see the core tension above - his behaviour says he doesn't actually live this way for long. And the abundance is weighted toward exactly the younger-partner profile he's flagged as higher long-term risk.

## Key Takeaways

- Julian's relationship goals are **internally divided**: a stated wish to stay unattached and enjoy Asia, against a lifelong pattern of quickly partnering up and wanting a loyal long-term companion. He calls the "play around" plan a possible false economy himself.
- The **marriage fear is a trauma pattern from [[clodagh]]** ("people change once secure"), not evidence about any current partner. It recurs as the change-after-security fear, autonomy loss, and partial-protection worry.
- The **younger-partner advantage HK seems to offer is double-edged**: he independently rates a big age gap as higher risk for the loyal old-age companionship he says he actually wants.
- An **equal-footing Western companionship** is something he's newly weighing as potentially more rewarding than a mentor-dynamic relationship.
