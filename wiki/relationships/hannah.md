# Hannah

> Julian's former long-distance partner (Indonesian). Now moved on. Light profile for now - may be expanded later. Captured 2026-07-02 for the [[b1-relationship-belief|UK-move B1 belief test]]. She is also the subject of the founding lesson in the [[decision-journal]] ("Whether to stay with Hannah").

## The relationship

- Indonesian; very beautiful; a **"serve-your-man" disposition** - would put her partner first regardless of her own mood (Julian's benchmark against [[joanne]]'s more forthright style).
- Became **long-distance after she was deported** (following a prison sentence) and could not return to HK. Marriage was the route that would have let her stay - the decision Julian never closed on (see below).
- Ran ~4 years: **loyal and committed for the first ~2 years** (he flew to see her roughly every 3 months), then **going through the motions** in the last ~2 years (roughly once a year) once he'd concluded there was no future - she couldn't come back to HK.
- **Cause of the split:** she wanted a **child**; Julian was clear he did not. Rather than end it cleanly, they drifted.

## How it ended

- ~3 months ago (early 2026) she found a **new (Australian) boyfriend** who moved fast: proposed, is buying a house, and will give her the child she wanted. She has moved on. This is what freed up / reframed the [[joanne]] relationship.

## Why she matters to the decision

- Her exit is what prompted [[joanne]] to ask where things stand - the trigger for the live marriage question.
- The **decision-journal parallel:** Julian's failure to commit ("marriage so she could stay" vs end it) and her leaving for someone else is a documented **commitment-avoidance** lesson - directly relevant to how the UK decision must be forced to a close. See [[decision-maker-profile]].

## Key Takeaways

- Now a closed chapter - not a live option and not an HK anchor.
- Serves as (a) the trigger for the current Joanne marriage question, and (b) a commitment-failure case study feeding the [[decision-journal]].
