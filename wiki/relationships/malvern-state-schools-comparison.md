# Girls' Sports & Academics: The Chase, Dyson Perrins & Hanley Castle

> Perplexity research report comparing the three non-fee-paying Malvern-area state secondaries on girls' sports and academics for Sophia (Year 8+). Key findings are summarised in [[schooling-malvern-area]], which holds the live admissions status; this file holds the full comparison and sources. Anchors the Malvern interim scenario of [[uk-relocation-decision]].

## Overview
This report compares three non-fee-paying state secondary schools in and around Malvern — **The Chase School**, **Dyson Perrins CofE Academy**, and **Hanley Castle High School** — focusing on girls' sports (especially netball, girls' football, swimming, and athletics) and academic performance, relevant for a girl aged 12 upwards entering Year 8.

### The Chase School
The Chase ranks **first among Malvern schools** and **third in Worcestershire** among state secondaries for GCSE outcomes. In 2024, the school achieved a record-breaking 80% of all subject entries at Grade 4 or higher, compared with a national pass rate of 67.6%. Twenty per cent of entries were awarded the highest Grades 8 and 9. Notable top performers included a student who achieved a clean sweep of Grade 9 across all 10 GCSE subjects. The school's sixth form holds a separate **Outstanding** Ofsted rating, making it particularly strong for students planning through to A-levels. EBacc APS of 4.55 is above the England average of 4.08.[^4][^10][^8]

In 2025, The Chase surpassed its own 2024 record with its best ever GCSE results: Attainment 8 rose to 51.8, Grade 4+ in English and Maths reached 75.8%, Grade 5+ in English and Maths was 51.5%, and 30% of entries were awarded the top grades 7-9. A further Ofsted inspection on 20 January 2026 (report published March 2026) confirmed the school's Good rating across all areas.[^30][^31]
### Dyson Perrins CofE Academy
Dyson Perrins is a smaller, faith-based school with a deliberately inclusive approach. Its Attainment 8 score of 41.6 is below the England average of 45.9, though its positive Progress 8 of +0.05 confirms that students make better progress from their starting points than similar pupils nationally. The school does not have a sixth form; students transfer at 16 to institutions such as The Chase or Heart of Worcestershire College. The 2023 Ofsted inspection rated it Good across all domains, praising the "ambitious curriculum" and calm behaviour. Strengths lie in the arts, food technology, and sciences.[^2][^5][^11]
### Hanley Castle High School
Hanley Castle has the highest Progress 8 score of the three — **+0.23** in the most recently validated dataset, meaning pupils achieve roughly half a grade higher across each qualification compared to similar pupils nationally. In the 2023 GCSE cohort, 88% achieved Grade 4 or above in English and English Literature, and 78% in Maths; over a fifth of all passes were at the top grades 7, 8, or 9. At A-level in 2024, 28% achieved A*/A, 60% A*-B, and the pass rate was 100%. The school was rated **Good** across all domains in its 2023 Ofsted inspection, including sixth form provision.[^12][^6][^7][^11][^3][^13][^14]

In 2025, Attainment 8 came in at 47.8 (down from 50.9 in 2023, though still above the England average of 45.9), while Progress 8 held at +0.23, confirming that the school continues to add strong value relative to pupils' starting points. Grade 5+ in English and Maths was 49%. A-level results in 2025 showed 24%+ at A*/A, 56% at A*-B, and 81% at A*-C.[^32][^33]

## Girls' Sports in the Curriculum
### The Chase School
The Chase's PE curriculum covers five broad content areas: invasion/net/wall/striking games, dance, gymnastics, health-related exercise, and athletics. The Year 8 curriculum includes netball, football, and athletics as part of the standard rotation, with girls and boys completing the same broad programme. The school website explicitly lists **netball** among girls' extra-curricular sports fixtures, with documented Girls U12B, U13A, and U12C netball teams competing against other schools including The King's School Worcester and RGS Worcester. **Football** features in the KS3 curriculum throughout Years 7, 8, and 9. **Athletics** is taught at all Key Stage 3 year groups. The Chase does **not have an on-site swimming pool**, but references a "partnership arrangement" with the nearby Malvern Swimming Pool and Leisure Centre for extended sports facilities.[^20][^17][^19]

The school's extracurricular sports listing includes rugby, hockey, basketball, football, badminton, dance, tennis, and netball. Notably, the Ofsted report mentioned "unusual sports such as kayaking and scuba diving" as part of the enrichment offer.[^21]
### Dyson Perrins CofE Academy
Dyson Perrins has a detailed, mapped PE curriculum for Year 8 that includes **netball**, **football**, **athletics** (both track and field), basketball, gymnastics, dance, badminton, rugby, fitness, and outdoor adventurous activities (OAA/orienteering). For **netball** specifically, Year 8 students reteach and develop core skills (footwork, ball handling, passing, shooting when stepping, and passing over mid-long distance). Football in Year 8 builds on core dribbling, passing, marking, and shooting with non-dominant foot work. Athletics covers both track events (starting, posture, cadence, bend running, pacing) and field events (throwing and jumping).[^18]

The school's extra-curricular clubs currently listed for 2025 include **KS3 Football** (Years 7, 8, and 9, every Tuesday after school on the astro), **Badminton** (Wednesdays), **KS3 Kinball**, **KS4 Volleyball**, **KS4 Fitness**, and **Year 7/8 Rugby**. The PE department uses an Instagram account (@dysonpe) to share fixtures, results, and sporting news. The on-site **Sport Dyson Perrins Leisure Centre** includes a large sports hall and dance studio, with netball and football available to students during the day. No dedicated netball girls' fixtures page or swimming programme was identified in publicly available information.[^22][^2][^23][^24]
### Hanley Castle High School
Hanley Castle has the most comprehensively documented sports programme of the three schools, with a well-resourced PE department of **six full-time and one part-time staff**. The school's formal PE Faculty Overview confirms the following for Year 8:[^15]

- **KS3 Year 8 curriculum (Terms 1 & 2):** Gymnastics, outdoor and adventurous activities, dance, hockey, health and fitness, football, rugby, netball, and badminton[^25]
- **KS3 Year 8 curriculum (Term 3):** Rounders or cricket, tennis, and **athletics**[^25]

For KS4 (Years 9–11), the curriculum broadens further to include girls' rugby, hockey, volleyball, dodgeball, handball, pickleball, aerobics, boxercise, body pump, and yoga alongside the core sports. This is the broadest KS4 girls' sports offering of the three schools.[^15]

The school's **girls' netball** programme is active and competitive. Recent results show girls' netball teams at U12, U13, U14, U15, U16, and U18 levels competing in fixtures against local schools including Pershore High, CWLC, and The Chase. The **Good Schools Guide** specifically notes that the netball programme "is having its day," with an ex-international coach who has "done wonders" for the team. The school actively fields **girls' football** teams and has a history of competitive success: it won the **Worcestershire Schools FA Girls' Under 16s Cup** in 2016, beating South Bromsgrove High School 2-0 in the final. Recent results show girls' football fixtures at various age groups.[^26][^27][^14][^28]

**Swimming** is highlighted as a whole-school event: the school runs an **Inter-College Swimming Gala**, listed among major annual events alongside Sports Day and the historic Hanley Run. This suggests competitive swimming is embedded into school life, though Hanley Castle does not appear to have its own on-site pool — the gala is a hosted inter-school event likely using a local facility.[^15]

**Athletics** is a curriculum staple, supported by the school's own **10-lane 400m athletics track** (summer use) — the only school of the three to have a dedicated on-site track.[^15]

## Overall Assessment for a Girl Aged 12+
### Best for competitive netball: **Hanley Castle High School**
Multiple age-group teams (U12 to U18), fixtures confirmed against regional schools, and an ex-international coach specifically noted by the Good Schools Guide.[^26][^14]
### Best for girls' football: **Hanley Castle High School**
Only school with a confirmed county-level girls' football achievement; multiple girls' fixtures at different age groups confirmed.[^26][^27]
### Best for athletics: **Hanley Castle High School**
The only school of the three with a dedicated 10-lane 400m athletics track, giving girls the ability to train and compete properly in track and field.[^15]
### Best for swimming access: **Slight edge to Hanley Castle** (Inter-College Swimming Gala), though none of the three schools has an on-site pool. Families serious about swimming should also investigate links to **Malvern Splash** leisure centre and local clubs.[^15]
### Best breadth of unusual sports: **The Chase School**
Offers the most distinctive non-mainstream options for girls including equestrian, kayaking, scuba diving, and sailing.[^4][^21]
### Best academic outcomes: **split picture (2025)**
The Chase now leads on Attainment 8 (51.8 vs Hanley Castle's 47.8 in 2025) and is third in Worcestershire among state secondaries. Hanley Castle leads on Progress 8 (+0.23 vs The Chase +0.08), meaning its pupils achieve more relative to their starting points. For a girl entering at a strong academic baseline, The Chase's higher absolute outcomes may be more relevant; Hanley Castle remains the stronger value-added school. Both have sixth forms with good A-level outcomes.[^6][^7][^14][^31][^32]
### Best for pastoral/smaller community: **Dyson Perrins CofE Academy**
Smallest of the three (~125 per year group), with a distinctly warm, values-led Christian ethos and strong pastoral care, though weaker on competitive girls' sports provision relative to the others.[^2]

***
## Key Caveats
- **Swimming pools:** None of the three schools has a confirmed on-site pool for regular lessons. Families should ask each school directly about swimming in the PE curriculum and what community facilities they use.[^15][^19]
- **Girls' football at The Chase:** The school has football pitches and includes football in the KS3 curriculum, but published fixtures and confirmed girls-only football teams are less visible than at Hanley Castle.[^20][^21]
- **Dyson Perrins sports detail:** As a smaller school (11–16), its sports fixtures and extra-curricular listing are less extensively documented publicly. Families should contact the PE department or check @dysonpe on social media for current clubs and teams.[^23]
- **Dyson Perrins — no sixth form:** A significant consideration for a girl aged 12 looking ahead — she would need to transfer at 16.[^2]
