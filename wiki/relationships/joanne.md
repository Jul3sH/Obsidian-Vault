# Joanne

> Julian's current partner. Profile, relationship history, current status, and the live marriage question. Supersedes the earlier `joanne-brainstorm` (folded in here 2026-07-02). Feeds the [[b1-relationship-belief|UK-move B1 belief test]].

## Who she is

- Filipino, from the countryside; ~20 years younger than Julian.
- In HK on a **domestic helper visa** - which caps what she can legally do: she cannot scale a business under it.
- **Entrepreneurial:** started a cake business in HK (children's birthday cakes / events, rich-parent market) with **demand already proven via her Instagram following, with zero active marketing.** Julian has mentored her and they've bonded over business. **Marriage is the enabler:** her domestic-helper visa bars her from legally working a separate business, so Julian has deliberately *not* marketed it - a legal, scalable venture (her baking, him marketing via AI) needs the marriage. His confidence if they married and built it together: **1M+ HKD paid business.** This is the anchor of decision scenario S2 (see [[alternatives|the UK-move alternatives analysis]] and [[risks|marriage-exposure risk]]).
- **Active and community-minded:** teaches swimming, keeps fit, proactive, goes out and does things. Has her own community in HK.
- **Two sons** being raised in the Philippines.
- Julian's read: inherently a nice person, with integrity; forthright and fairly direct (will push back / assert her opinion).

## Relationship history

- Together ~2 years, seeing each other about once a week.
- Throughout, she knew Julian was in a long-distance relationship with [[hannah]] and accepted the **mistress** arrangement - taking the bet that it might become more.
- The bond deepened through Julian's business mentorship as her cake business grew.

## Current status (mid-2026)

- ~3 months ago [[hannah]] moved on (engaged to an Australian who offered marriage, a house and a child). That removed the other relationship and prompted Joanne to **ask where she and Julian stand**.
- ~4 weeks ago Julian told her he has to **evaluate the relationship and seriously consider marriage** so she knows where she stands. They're discussing it in pieces; he's running "if I got married, then X" scenarios with her and has acknowledged he's uncomfortably in the driving seat.
- **She has offered to move to the UK with him without her sons** - keeping them in the Philippines and visiting ~twice a year. However: **as a Filipino national, she cannot relocate to the UK without marriage** - she would need a spousal visa. Her offer is real but marriage-conditional, not freely portable.
- **She has made clear that long-term she is looking for marriage.** If Julian does not intend to marry her, he needs to move on - regardless of whether he stays in HK or moves to the UK.

## The marriage question - concerns

1. **Sexual reciprocity / desire signals.** Sex has been very good, but on occasions she hasn't wanted it / been tired, even seeing each other only weekly. Julian raised it (said if sex isn't good it's a non-starter); she attributed it to resenting being "a mistress." The following week she was proactive and initiating; but the most recent weekend she again didn't want to. His **gut fear:** this reluctance *before* marriage predicts pulling back *after* she has the security she wants - risk of drifting toward a sexless marriage. Comparison point: [[hannah]]'s "serve-your-man" disposition regardless of her own mood.
2. **Change-after-security.** The overarching fear (rooted in [[clodagh]], not in Joanne's conduct): people change once married and secure. See [[relationship-views]].
3. **Forthrightness vs easy-going.** She's more direct/willing to push back than the very easy-going [[hannah]]. As Julian gets older and more tired - and after a prickly, argumentative marriage - part of him wants an easy life. He flags he may be being unfair to her here.
4. **Age gap / outgrowing / affair risk.** 20 years younger. As he loses energy in later years, she'll still be active and could get bored - and, married and secure ("feet under the table"), might have an affair. He rates her as probably loyal (countryside Filipina, less conventionally striking than [[hannah]], which he cynically counts as lowering flight risk) - but in the UK, with her personality, he expects plenty of male attention.
5. **Business motive.** She's money-oriented and driven (poor background, single mother of two). He can't fully tell how much her interest in him is entwined with what he can enable. Marriage would let her run a real business; she might succeed independently regardless. He lands on: a risk, but he thinks she has integrity.
6. **Inheritance dilution via her sons (the heavy financial risk).** She must support her two sons in the Philippines (~£4k/yr minimum - small, and she could likely earn it). The real exposure is open-ended: over years together she'd progressively want more of his savings for her boys, eroding what's available to Sophia. A parallel to the [[Dad/relationship-dynamics|Dad dynamic]] (dependency → leverage). **Mitigants:** prenup + ring-fencing Sophia's inheritance (will/trust) *before* marrying; the residual is behavioural, not legal. Worked as [[risks|R1 marriage exposure]] / belief B5 in the UK-move decision.

## Location interaction (feeds the UK decision)

- **Won't work long-term at his mum's village:** he thinks she'd get bored, can't drive there, would be isolated. For it to work in the UK it would likely have to be **London**.
- **Autonomy risk:** if she's unhappy in the village she may push to move to London when he doesn't want to - eroding his freedom. This is a real, relationship-specific concern about *where the relationship takes him*, distinct from whether to be with her at all.
- **Sophia:** would need to get on with her, but Sophia is older now, so Julian sees this as manageable, not a big obstacle.

## The ambivalence pattern

- **Apart / thinking pragmatically:** he leans toward calling it off.
- **With her:** he enjoys her company and the shared vision of building a company together.
- He acknowledges the "run a company together" appeal may be rose-tinted - joint ventures are also a major source of couple conflict.

## Key Takeaways

- Joanne is willing to relocate to the UK, but **only via marriage** - she is Filipino and requires a spousal visa. Marriage is therefore the gate to two things simultaneously: (a) the cake business legally operating in HK, and (b) her presence in any UK scenario. The marriage question is **not separable from the location question**: moving to the UK without marrying her effectively ends the relationship.
- Her long-term expectation is marriage. If Julian does not intend to marry her, he needs to move on, in either country.
- Julian's stated marriage motivation (2026-07-03): largely **(a) to unlock the business** and **(b) fear of not finding someone in the UK** - not a deep want to marry her specifically. This distinction matters: a marriage entered for those reasons, not for the relationship itself, carries its own risk.
- Julian's doubts are **relationship-quality doubts** (desire signals, change-after-security fear, age gap, autonomy), not location doubts - they'd travel with him.
- The marriage sub-decision has its own strand in the [[decision-journal]]. See [[b1-relationship-belief]] and [[risks|R1 marriage exposure]].
- Consistent thread: fear of what happens once the incentive to "perform" is removed by security - the same trauma pattern documented in [[relationship-views]] and traced to [[clodagh]].
