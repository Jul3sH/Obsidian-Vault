# TTI — AI Leadership Brief

## Story
> *As a job applicant, I want a polished Google Doc brief making the case for an AI-leadership role at TTI (CAIO / Director of AI / equivalent at director-level) — anchored to the AI productivity gap brief Stephan read at Cipriani, supported by the ~75%-of-enterprises trend, and demonstrated through a worked example of role-redesign (using the recently-advertised TTI systems admin role as a case study) — so that Stephan has a complete argument he can put in front of the right internal audience.*

## Anchoring & Framing
- **Build on:** [[tti-ai-productivity-gap]] — the strategic brief Stephan demonstrably read at Cipriani. This is the natural anchor; the AI leadership brief is the explicit extension of that argument.
- **Do NOT anchor on:** [[tti-value-proposition]] — Stephan didn't engage with it. That document gets its second life through [[tti-consulting-brief]].
- **Strategic positioning:** Director-level (Julian's positioning post-Cipriani — Stephan has not co-signed it, so the argument must be tight). See [[tti-stephan-relationship-analysis]] for nuance.
- **Tone:** thought leadership, forward-looking. Not retrospective criticism of any specific hiring decision.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. Opens by anchoring to the AI productivity gap brief (the document Stephan read at Cipriani) — explicit continuation of that argument, not a fresh pitch
2. Macro-trend section: data-backed view on AI-leadership role adoption (~75% of enterprises, named companies, regional cadence) sourced via deep research
3. TTI-specific section: maps the trend to TTI's stated AI mandates from AR25 (Steve Richman / Ty Staviski / Shane Mall) — see [[tti-2025-ar-key-initiatives]]
4. Role-redesign worked example: uses the recently-advertised TTI systems admin role as a case study, showing how AI restructures (not just accelerates) operational roles. Framed as forward-looking, not as critique of Tony Chung's hiring.
5. Julian's capabilities mapped against the role at director level — interim capacity or alongside a future global appointment also presented as options
6. Closes with directional statement framed as "who internally would best appreciate this scope?" — not "can you help me get a specific role?"
7. Document polished, Julian satisfied it's ready for Stephan, and Julian has a clean answer ready if asked "were you hoping to fill that systems admin role?" (likely answer: "open to it as a stepping-stone but the more interesting question is whether TTI is designing roles for the AI era")

## Definition of Done
A polished Google Doc brief exists meeting all 7 acceptance criteria. The argument chain (trend → TTI mandate → role-redesign worked example → Julian's positioning) is complete and tight. Julian is satisfied it's polished and ready to present or send to Stephan as Door 2 of the two-door re-engagement (see [[role-context]]).
