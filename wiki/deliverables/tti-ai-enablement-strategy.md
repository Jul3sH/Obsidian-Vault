## Anchoring & Framing
- **Focus:** Employee AI enablement / AI-enabled workflows / governed proliferation of AI skills across teams with similar responsibilities. The genuinely unoccupied layer between Pivar's platform and Ty's EBIT target.
- **Differentiating credential:** Service design — Julian's 12-year track record managing complex service design for global multi-outsourced environments. The existing TTI AI team is data-science-led and does not have this capability.
- **Commercial anchor:** Ty Staviski's explicit AI-to-EBIT mandate (10% EBIT by 2027). Employee AI productivity gains are the conversion mechanism.
- **Political discipline:** Position as *additive* to Brian Pivar's platform mandate, never *competitive*. The platform investment is what makes this work possible.
- **Tone:** Thought leadership with concrete ROI, not abstract strategy. Defensible to a finance executive.

## Acceptance Criteria
1. Opens by anchoring to the AI productivity gap argument (the brief Stephan read at Cipriani) — explicit continuation, not fresh pitch.
2. Diagnoses the specific gap at TTI: between platform (Pivar) and business outcome (Ty's EBIT mandate) — the employee enablement / AI-enabled workflows layer is structurally unowned. References [[tti-ai-roles]] evidence.
3. Articulates *why service design and process architecture* (not data science) is the discipline that closes the gap. Frames Julian's background as the correct profile.
4. AR25 mapping section: explicit traces to Ty Staviski (G&A / EBIT) and Steve Richman (supply chain AI) mandates. References [[tti-2025-ar-key-initiatives]].
5. Defensible NPV / ROI section: order-of-magnitude productivity gain with explicit methodology — headcount estimate (sourced), hourly cost loading (rationale), productivity uplift % (comparator benchmark from McKinsey / BCG / MIT enterprise AI productivity research with citations). The maths must hold up to a finance executive's scrutiny.
6. 90-day pilot proposal: concrete first workflow candidate (suggest 1–2 specific APAC operational functions — supply chain coordination, financial reporting, or quality reporting), pilot cohort size, measurement framework, success criteria, expected pilot-stage ROI.
7. *Closes with a small concrete ask* — a 30-minute conversation with Ty (or named recipient) about pilot scope. NOT a request for a role; the role conversation belongs in the meeting that the ask creates.
8. Includes the "two modes" framing in the close: pilot can be run as a 90-day consulting engagement OR as the first deliverable of the EA Director role per the JD Stephan already has.
9. Document polished, Julian satisfied it's ready to send to Stephan — and ready to be forwarded to Ty without further editing.
10. Length: 25–40 pages. Production-grade visual design (charts, framework diagrams, clear typographic hierarchy).
