# Hanley Castle High School

Mixed state school for boys and girls aged 11-18, near Malvern, Worcestershire. Relevant to the [[uk-relocation-decision|UK Relocation Decision]] as the likely secondary school for Sophia under the **Move to Malvern** scenario. This page collects due-diligence facts as they are gathered.

## Key Takeaways
- Mixed (co-ed) state school, ages 11-18.
- **Girls' football is offered** - in PE lessons and as part of core school sport, with school teams and fixtures.
- Football sits alongside rugby, cricket and netball as a staple sport in the PE and games programme.
- For current girls' football teams and fixtures, contact the PE department / office (office@hanleycastlehs.org.uk).

## Girls' football

Confirmed: Hanley Castle offers football for girls, both in PE lessons and as part of its core school sport.

**In PE and games:**
- The PE and games programme lists football as a staple sport alongside rugby, cricket and netball.
- As a mixed school, the PE curriculum includes football for girls at Key Stage 3 and 4, so girls play football in lessons and games afternoons.
- The published Year 7 and Year 8 PE plan lists football among activities in the same way as netball and hockey (both played by girls); the Key Stage 4 programme lists football alongside girls' rugby, netball and other mixed-gender activities.

**Competitive:**
- The Good Schools Guide review notes football among the main competitive sports the school runs (with rugby, cricket and netball).
- In practice this means school teams and fixtures in football that girls can join. Exact age-group teams (e.g. Year 7-8 vs older year groups) are organised by the PE department each year.

**To confirm current provision:** email the PE department / office (office@hanleycastlehs.org.uk) to ask specifically about girls' football teams and fixtures for the relevant year group.
