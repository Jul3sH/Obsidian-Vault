> *Work expands to fill all available time. Family doesn't wait.*

tags: [wellbeing]

## The Core Problem

Work has no natural ceiling — there is always more to do. Family and personal relationships have a finite window, and neglecting them doesn't produce a visible error message until the damage is already done.

Common failure pattern: over-committing at work, failing to say no, and then being mentally or physically absent for family — not through malice, but through a failure to prioritise explicitly.

## Practical Rules

- **Say no at work** — the inability to push back on work demands is the root cause of most family neglect, not lack of affection. See [[working-with-others/assertiveness-and-risk-dont-over-commit]]
- **Finish work before switching off** — half-working during family time helps no one. Either be present at work or present at home; attempting both simultaneously means you're doing neither well
- **Give full attention when you're there** — family members notice partial presence. A shorter window of genuine engagement is worth more than extended physical presence with a distracted mind
- **Don't neglect important personal commitments** — a speech for a parent's milestone, attending a school event, being emotionally available during a partner's difficult period. These moments are not recoverable

## The Right Sequence

1. Push hard to complete work obligations
2. Create a clean break
3. Recover and invest fully in relationships and personal restoration

This cycle — full effort, full rest — is more sustainable than the blurred middle state of always-half-on.

## My Pattern

Over-commitment from inability to say no at work bleeds into family time as mental absence. The half-working pattern does the most quiet damage. The cycle of "push hard, finish completely, recover fully" requires the saying-no muscle to be exercised consistently.

## Key Takeaways

- Family neglect is usually a by-product of poor work boundaries, not bad intentions
- Learn to say no at work so you can say yes at home
- Full presence in recovery and relationships requires a clean break from work
- Missed personal milestones are not recoverable — protect them proactively
