# Defend locked decisions; a wobble is not a reopen

**The pattern (complements [[feedback-commitment-forcing]] - that forces closure; this defends it).** Julian's weak link has moved from "never decides" to **commitment durability**: he commits, then emotionally *un-decides* within ~72 hours. His conviction re-anchors to the **last emotionally warm voice, not the evidence** - in the UK-move decision every lean-change timestamped to a human contact (a weekend with his partner reopened a closed decision; a phone call minted "100% convinced"), never to a document. His absolute certainties ("100%") are emotional spikes that decay in days; his honest analysis speaks in ranges.

**Why it matters:** the decision is the easy part - he now makes it. *Staying* decided is the skill he lacks. Wobbles arrive off-session (late at night, mid-other-topic), so this defence cannot live only in a skill he must invoke.

**How to apply - when he revisits a decision the [[decision-journal]] marks COMMITTED/LOCKED:**
- Treat it as a **reopen event**, not a fresh question. Do NOT dive back into the substance first.
- **48-hour rule:** ask if there's been an emotionally significant contact/event in the last 48h. If yes → **stand-down**, no state change now, revisit after 48h.
- **Run the Reopen Test (F-N-M-T):** is it a **F**act (external, verifiable - not a feeling/forecast/mood), **N**ew (unknown *and* not knowable at lock time - knowable-but-unexamined is a session note, not a reopen), **M**aterial (would the lock-time analysis have concluded differently?), and absent from the anchor doc's fear **T**able (if present, it's the same fear re-dressed)? **All four pass → legitimate reopen** (book a session, decision unchanged until then). **Any fail → it's the drift:** quote the anchor's answering fact back, log one line, do not move the decision.
- Treat self-reported conviction **>~90% as a warning light, not a green light**.
- Julian is the principal - if he overrides, comply, but **log the override** so the record shows a lock broken without a passing fact.
- The line to say: **"the feeling that follows a locked decision is forecast, not evidence."**
