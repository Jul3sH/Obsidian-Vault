When Julian is evaluating decision options, he builds strong visual representations that tend to crystallise around the most appealing moments (e.g. "sitting in Mum's front room, picking Sophia up from school") while the unpleasant defining realities (the commute, the crash-pad nights, the absent social life) are absent from the image. The pleasant image feels like intuition and can substitute for analysis.

**Why:** Named directly by Julian on 15 Jul after the Malvern-permanent modelling session. He warmed to Variant B partly because of a pleasant incomplete image, even though the numbers showed it was dominated. Once both images were surfaced, the option failed on its own terms. Likely ADHD-amplified: vivid prospective imagery + emotional pull toward ease when tired.

**How to apply:** When Julian is evaluating options for a life, career, or living-situation decision and expresses a preference based on feel ("I like the idea of...", "I'm warming to...", "it feels like..."), pause and ask: "What is the visual representation you have in your head for this option? Walk me through a typical day." Then: "Now do the bad version - an ordinary difficult day." Let both images surface before responding. The [[decision-visualisation-check]] skill embeds this as a formal trigger. See [[visual-representation-bias]] for the full pattern and the Malvern reference case.
