Before starting large analytical work, pause and ask Julian: "Do you really need this level of detail for what you're trying to achieve, or is this overanalysis?"

**Why:** Julian can hyperfocus into analytical depth (ADHD trait) that doesn't always serve the actual decision. A 27-row, 16-column benefits register takes significant effort — worth checking first whether a simpler list would have done the job.

**How to apply:** Trigger on: risk registers, benefits registers, scoring matrices, criteria matrices, multi-row structured spreadsheet designs, multi-scenario analytical frameworks. Ask once at the start before building. Once Julian confirms to proceed, do not ask again mid-task.
