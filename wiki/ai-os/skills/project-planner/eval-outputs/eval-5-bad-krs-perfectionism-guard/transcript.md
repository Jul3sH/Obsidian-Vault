# Epic Planner Interview — Leadership Development Epic
**Date:** 2026-05-05  
**User:** Julian (Hong Kong, Performance workstream)  
**Skill:** epic-planner

---

## Opening Prompt
"I want to create an epic for becoming a better leader. My key results are: read more books, attend a leadership course, improve my communication, get better at giving feedback, and work on my executive presence."

---

## PHASE 1: DEFINE THE EPIC

### Step 1.1 — Workstream
**CLAUDE:** Which workstream does this fall under?  
**USER:** Performance.

### Step 1.2 — The Objective (with pushback)
**CLAUDE:** In one sentence, what outcome are you trying to achieve?  
**USER:** I want to become a better leader.

**CLAUDE:** [Pushback] That's broad. What will others *notice* about you? What will be different? Over what timeframe?  
**USER:** I want to be more effective with my team — better at giving feedback, clearer communication, and people see me as more leadership-like. Over the next six months.

**CLAUDE:** [Rephrased] "Be recognized as a more effective leader within my team by August, with stronger feedback and communication skills"?  
**USER:** Yes, that works.

### Step 1.3 — Key Results (max 3, with heavy pushback on vagueness and activities)

**CLAUDE:** What are the 2–3 *measurable results* that would prove this objective is achieved? Each must be binary, measurable, and time-boxed.

**USER:** But they're all important! I feel like I need to do all five.

**CLAUDE:** [Constraint enforcement] The Epic framework works best with ruthless focus. If we try five things, none get proper attention. Also, your list includes *activities*, not *outcomes*. "Read books" and "attend a course" are how you might learn, but they're not proof you've become a better leader.

Example:
- Bad: "Attend a leadership course by July"
- Good: "Deliver structured feedback (SBI model) to all 3 direct reports by June 30"

Which three areas matter most: feedback, communication, executive presence?

**USER:** Yes, those three.

**CLAUDE:** Great. Let's make each outcome-focused.

**KR 1 — Feedback:**  
What does "good at giving feedback" look like in a measurable way? Not "take a course" — what will you *deliver*?

**USER:** Real feedback to my team members. Maybe get them to say I've improved?

**CLAUDE:** [Tightening] How many people? What kind of feedback? By when?  
**USER:** All 3 direct reports. Using the SBI model by end of June.

**CLAUDE:** Perfect. **KR 1: "Deliver structured feedback (SBI model) to all 3 direct reports by June 30."**

**KR 2 — Communication:**  
What does "improved communication" mean in measurable terms?

**USER:** People understand me better. I communicate more clearly in meetings.

**CLAUDE:** [Tightening] More specific — is it about meetings, 1-on-1s, or something else?  
**USER:** The 1-on-1s. That's where real communication happens.

**CLAUDE:** So: **"Complete 6 structured 1-on-1s with documented action items and follow-up check-in by July 31"?**  
**USER:** Yeah, that works.

**KR 3 — Executive Presence:**  
What would prove you have stronger executive presence? Not "be more present" — what's the outcome?

**USER:** It's about how I come across in larger meetings or presentations. People take me seriously.

**CLAUDE:** [Tightening] So it's about presenting specifically?  
**USER:** Yes, presenting.

**CLAUDE:** [Pushback] Okay, but "giving 3 presentations" is an activity. What's the *outcome*? Is it that people give you feedback saying you're more credible?  
**USER:** Yes, the feedback on credibility is what matters.

**CLAUDE:** Then: **"Deliver 3 presentations to leadership/all-hands; gather peer feedback showing 80%+ 'credible' rating by August 31"?**  
**USER:** That feels right.

### Step 1.4 — T-shirt Size
**CLAUDE:** Which size feels right for the whole Epic?

| Size | Hours | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| XS | 1–2h | One focused session |
| S | 3–8h | One day |
| M | 1–3 days | A few evenings + weekend |
| L | 1–2 weeks | Sustained effort |
| XL | 2–4 weeks | Major commitment |

**USER:** Maybe M or L?

**CLAUDE:** [Reasoning] Six 1-on-1s = 3–4 hours. Three presentations = 6–8 hours prep. Feedback = 2–3 hours. Total ~12–15 hours focused work over three months. That's **M**.

**USER:** Yeah, M is fair.

### Step 1.5 — Confirm the Epic
**CLAUDE:**
```
Workstream: Performance
Objective: Be recognized as a more effective leader within my team by August, 
           with stronger feedback and communication skills
Key Results:
  1. Deliver structured feedback (SBI model) to all 3 direct reports by June 30
  2. Complete 6 structured 1-on-1s with documented action items and follow-up by July 31
  3. Deliver 3 presentations to leadership/all-hands; gather feedback showing 80%+ "credible" rating by August 31
T-shirt: M (1–3 days focused effort)
```

Does this look right?

**USER:** Looks good.

## PHASE 2: DEFINE AND SCORE PROJECTS

### Step 2.1 — List the Projects

**CLAUDE:** What are the distinct pieces of work needed to deliver these KRs?

**USER:** For KR1: prepare the feedback, maybe learn SBI first, then have conversations.  
For KR2: design a template, then run the 1-on-1s.  
For KR3: prepare presentations, deliver them, gather feedback.

**CLAUDE:** So these projects:
1. Learn & prep the SBI feedback model
2. Conduct 3 SBI feedback conversations
3. Design a 1-on-1 template & agenda
4. Schedule & conduct 6 structured 1-on-1s
5. Prepare 3 presentations
6. Deliver 3 presentations & gather feedback

Are all six necessary?

**USER:** Yes.

**CLAUDE:** [Later catch during scoring] Actually, projects 5 and 6 should be one project — "Prepare and deliver 3 presentations; gather feedback."

**Revised Project List:**
1. Learn & prep SBI model
2. Conduct 3 SBI feedback conversations
3. Design 1-on-1 template & agenda
4. Schedule & conduct 6 structured 1-on-1s
5. Prepare and deliver 3 presentations; gather feedback

### Step 2.2 — Score Each Project with WSJF

#### Project 1: Learn & prep the SBI feedback model

**Value:** What specifically gets better if you do this well?  
**USER:** I'll give feedback that lands and my team understands what to change.

**CLAUDE:** Concrete and significant. **Value = 3**.

**Time Criticality:** What gets worse if you delay one month?  
**USER:** The mid-year review window closes, and I need to learn SBI before giving feedback.

**CLAUDE:** Real deadline blocking the next project. **Time Criticality = 3**.

**Risk/Opportunity:** What other projects become easier?  
**USER:** Once I learn SBI, I can use it in presentations and 1-on-1s. It's foundational.

**CLAUDE:** Enables downstream work. **Risk/Opp = 2**.

**Job Size:** How long?  
**USER:** 2–3 hours of learning and practice. S.

**CLAUDE:** **Size = S (2)**

**WSJF = (3 + 3 + 2) ÷ 2 = 4.0**
