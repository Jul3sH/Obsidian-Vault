## Phase 1 — Define the Epic

### Step 1.1 — Workstream

**[User]:** "I should probably do something about building my personal brand. I'm not sure where to start."

**[Claude]:** "Good place to start. Personal brand can live in a few different spots — which workstream does this feel like it belongs under? Career, performance, finance, personal, relationships, technical, or wellbeing? My guess would be career — things like LinkedIn visibility, being known in your field. Does that sound right?"

**[User]:** "Yeah, probably career?"

---

### Step 1.2 — The Objective

**[Claude]:** "Ok, career it is. Now the key question: in one sentence, what outcome are you trying to achieve? Not what you'll do — what will be *different or better* when this is done? For example, not 'post more on LinkedIn' but something like 'be seen as a credible expert so that opportunities come to me.'"

**[User]:** "I guess... I want people to know who I am and what I do? Like, I want to be visible in my field."

**[Claude]:** "That's the right direction but let's sharpen it. 'Visible in my field' is a means, not an end. What does visibility *unlock* for you — is it inbound job opportunities, speaking gigs, client leads, something else? What changes in your career if you pull this off?"

**[User]:** "Probably job opportunities, yeah. I'm technical and work in AI/tech, and I feel like nobody knows who I am outside my current company. It would be good to be the kind of person that recruiters or interesting companies reach out to."

**[Claude]:** "Good. So something like: 'Be credibly positioned as an AI-native technical professional so that inbound opportunities (roles, collaborations) come to me, rather than me always having to hunt.' Does that capture it?"

**[User]:** "Yes, that's much better actually."

---

### Step 1.3 — Key Results

**[Claude]:** "Perfect. Now: what are the 2–3 measurable results that would prove this is achieved? Each one needs to be binary or clearly measurable, and have a time box or effort cap. Maximum 3."

**[User]:** "Hmm. Maybe something like... have a proper LinkedIn presence, post some content, maybe write some articles? I'm not sure how to measure it though."

**[Claude]:** "Let's make those concrete. 'Proper LinkedIn presence' isn't measurable — let's pin it. How about: 'LinkedIn profile fully optimised with a clear AI/technical positioning statement, in place by end of May.' That's binary — done or not done. For posting — how many posts feels like proof you've actually built a habit, not just posted once?"

**[User]:** "Maybe 8 posts? Over 2 months?"

**[Claude]:** "I'd say: 'Publish 8 LinkedIn posts in 8 weeks, minimum 2 demonstrating technical depth.' That gives you a pace and a quality bar. And the third — you mentioned articles. Longer-form content, or is that the same as posts?"

**[User]:** "I was thinking longer-form — like proper LinkedIn articles or maybe a newsletter. But I'm not sure I can commit to loads of them."

**[Claude]:** "Let's keep it achievable. One long-form piece — an article or newsletter issue — with measurable distribution. Something like: 'Publish 1 long-form article or newsletter issue that gets shared beyond your first-degree network by July 31.' Does that feel right, or too ambitious?"

**[User]:** "That feels doable, yeah."

---

### Step 1.4 — T-shirt Size

**[Claude]:** "Great. Here's the t-shirt size table:

| Size | Hours | Meaning |
|------|-------|---------|
| XS | 1–2h | One focused session |
| S | 3–8h | One day |
| M | 1–3 days | A few evenings + weekend |
| L | 1–2 weeks | Sustained effort |
| XL | 2–4 weeks | Major commitment |

Looking at all three KRs together — optimising LinkedIn, 8 posts over 8 weeks, and one long-form piece — what size does the whole epic feel like?"

**[User]:** "Probably L? It's not a one-weekend thing but it's not like a full month project either."

---

### Step 1.5 — Epic Confirmation

**[Claude]:** "Agreed — L feels right. Let me read this back:

```
Workstream: career
Objective: Be credibly positioned as an AI-native technical professional so that inbound opportunities come to me, rather than me always having to hunt.
Key Results:
  1. LinkedIn profile fully optimised with clear AI/technical positioning, in place by 31 May 2026.
  2. Publish 8 LinkedIn posts in 8 weeks, minimum 2 demonstrating technical depth — by 30 June 2026.
  3. Publish 1 long-form article or newsletter issue that gets shared beyond first-degree network, by 31 July 2026.
T-shirt: L (1–2 weeks of focused effort)
```

Does this look right before we break it into Projects?"

**[User]:** "Yes, that's solid."

## Phase 2 — Define and Score Projects

### Step 2.1 — Listing Projects

**[Claude]:** "Now let's break it into the distinct pieces of work. For each KR, what needs to be built, written, or done?"

**[User]:** "For the LinkedIn profile thing — I need to rewrite my headline and About section, probably update my experience descriptions too. For the posts — I need some kind of system, otherwise I'll just not do it. And for the article, I need to pick a topic and actually write it."

**[Claude]:** "Good. Let me add one more that tends to get missed: a content angle definition — figuring out *what your voice and perspective is* before you start posting. Otherwise posts feel scattered. So I'd suggest these projects:

1. LinkedIn profile overhaul (KR1)
2. Content angle & posting system setup (KR2)
3. 8-week LinkedIn posting execution (KR2)
4. Long-form article — research, write, publish (KR3)

Does that look complete, or anything missing?"

**[User]:** "That looks right. Maybe I should also think about a newsletter platform? Like setting up Substack?"

**[Claude]:** "Let's fold that into the long-form article project — 'platform setup + first piece published' as one project, since the platform is just the vehicle. Keeps it tighter. Agreed?"

**[User]:** "Yeah, makes sense."

---

### Step 2.2 — WSJF Scoring

#### Project 1: LinkedIn Profile Overhaul

**[Claude — Value]:** "What specifically gets better in your career if your LinkedIn profile is properly optimised?"

**[User]:** "Recruiters and interesting people can actually understand what I do. Right now it's a bit of a mess."

**[Claude]:** "I'd score that a 3 — concrete career impact, credibility and discoverability directly improved. Agree?"

**[User]:** "Yes."

**[Claude — Time Criticality]:** "What specifically gets worse if you delay this by one month?"

**[User]:** "Not much changes in a month I guess... but it's the foundation everything else builds on."

**[Claude]:** "I'd say 2 — delay causes gradual compounding effect. Your posts land on a weak profile the whole time. Not a hard deadline but there's real cost. Agree?"

**[User]:** "That's fair."

**[Claude — Risk/Opportunity]:** "What other projects or opportunities become easier once this is done?"

**[User]:** "Everything else — if the profile is good, my posts and article all point to something coherent."

**[Claude]:** "Score 3 — it's the foundation that unblocks coherence across all other projects. Agree?"

**[User]:** "Yes."

**[Claude — Job Size]:** "How long to overhaul headline, About section, and experience descriptions?"

**[User]:** "Maybe 3–4 hours focused."

**[Claude]:** "That's S — 3–8h. WSJF = (3 + 2 + 3) ÷ 2 = **4.0**"

---

#### Project 2: Content Angle & Posting System Setup

**[Claude — Value]:** "What gets better if you define your content angle and set up a posting system?"

**[User]:** "I'll actually post consistently instead of staring at a blank screen every time."

**[Claude]:** "Score 3 — directly enables KR2; without this the posts won't happen."

**[Claude — Time Criticality]:** "What gets worse if you delay this by a month?"

**[User]:** "I lose 4 weeks of the 8-week posting window."

**[Claude]:** "Score 3 — direct measurable cost. You can't recover those posting weeks."

**[Claude — Risk/Opportunity]:** "What does this unlock?"

**[User]:** "The whole 8-week posting run."

**[Claude]:** "Score 3 — directly unblocks Project 3. WSJF = (3 + 3 + 3) ÷ 2 = **4.5**"

**[User]:** "Got it."

---

#### Project 3: 8-Week LinkedIn Posting Execution

**[Claude — Value]:** "What gets better if you publish 8 quality posts over 8 weeks?"

**[User]:** "My network grows, I get seen as someone with views on AI, maybe recruiters notice."

**[Claude]:** "Score 3 — direct KR2 achievement; career visibility."

**[Claude — Time Criticality]:** "What gets worse if you delay starting by one month?"

**[User]:** "I lose a month of the window and push my article timeline back."

**[Claude]:** "Score 2 — gradual momentum loss, timeline compresses but the opportunity doesn't close."

**[User]:** "It feeds into the long-form piece — if I've been posting, I know what resonates."

**[Claude]:** "Score 2 — some downstream benefit; informs article topic and angle."

**[User]:** "Each post is probably 30-45 mins. So maybe 6-8 hours total. Plus checking comments, etc."

**[Claude]:** "I'd round that to M — once you include the ongoing engagement it's spread across 1–3 days total. WSJF = (3 + 2 + 2) ÷ 3 = **2.3**"

**[User]:** "OK."

**[User]:** "It proves I can think deeply, not just post hot takes. And if it gets traction, people follow me."

**[Claude]:** "Score 3 — strong credibility signal; directly achieves KR3."
