## Principles

Hard rules that apply to every Performance decision, regardless of circumstance. Must always adhere.

1. **Face Resistance Immediately** — As soon as resistance to doing something is felt, address why. If it should be done, do it. Avoidance is not an option.
2. **Learn by Doing, Apply Everything** — Only learn through doing. Apply all knowledge while acquiring it. If knowledge cannot be applied, question why it is being learned. Only develop what can be practised.
3. **Structured Reading — SQ3R** — All reading must follow SQ3R or it is recreational, regardless of how interesting it feels. 
4. **Mandatory Value Gate** — Before starting any new development activity, run a formal gate: does this align with current priorities? Is there a clear, measurable return? Can I articulate why this, and why now? Only proceed if it passes. 
5. **Establish a Feedback Loop** — Every skill, project, or development activity must have a feedback mechanism. Find a way to get feedback on everything. No feedback means no learning.
6. **Execution Discipline** — Single handle: one focus at a time for continuity. Always set a deadline — Parkinson's Law applies. Make a decision, commit, start, and stick to it.
7. **Develop Skills in a Safe Environment** — Never practice new or underdeveloped skills in high-stakes situations. Build competence in a safe environment before going live.
8. **Do The Thing** — Researching, reading, planning, and preparing are not doing the thing. Don't overthink, don't overplan, don't over-research. Start. Deliver. The only valid measure is output, not effort invested in getting ready.
9. **Mandatory Review Cadence** — All scheduled reviews must be completed religiously, without exception. Daily: morning review, daily prioritisation, Gmail triage, evening review. Weekly: weekly prioritisation, weekly review. Periodic: performance review, workstream review. Skipping reviews is how priorities drift and commitments are missed.
10. **AI-First Execution** — Default to AI as the first tool for any task: research, synthesis, drafting, structuring, coding, analysis. Before reaching for manual effort, ask whether an AI workflow does it better or faster. Brake clause: if designing the AI approach takes longer than doing the task manually, do it manually. The leverage must justify the overhead.
11. **Act on Reality, Not Emotion** — All decisions must be grounded in honest assessment of reality — not wishful thinking, not other people's opinions, not fear. When choosing between guaranteed income and potential income, conduct a thorough risk assessment before deciding. Dreams are not a plan; reality is the only valid basis for action. Any opportunity must be proven capable of delivering value with a clear, credible plan to do so.

## Values

What I care about in performance — directional guides that shape how I develop and operate.

1. **Maximise Feedback** — Always seek more feedback than feels necessary. Feedback is the fastest path to improvement; the instinct to avoid it is the instinct to stagnate.
2. **Continuous Learning** — A lifelong love. Always find time for it, regardless of other pressures.
3. **Coaching, Mentoring, and Helping People** — Helping others develop and giving help in general brings real satisfaction. Find ways to do as much of it as possible, whether in a formal role or outside work.

## 10-year Ambitions

> *Where I want to be by 2036. Directional and aspirational — not strictly measured, but each should have a loose sense of "how would I know I'm on track?"*

- **Curiosity-Driven Development** — Learning and development time is spent primarily on things I'm genuinely fascinated by, not things I must master to earn. The obligatory skill-building phase is behind me; the exploration phase has begun.
- **Enjoy Service to Others** — The arenas where I apply my skills and energy have shifted from earning to giving back. Philanthropy, mentorship, or other forms of contribution are the primary performance focus — pursued for the enjoyment and fulfilment they bring, not obligation.

## 5-year Directions

> *Where I need to be by 2031 to be on track for the 10-year Ambitions. More grounded — aligned to the Ambitions above.*

- **Revenue-Driven Development** — All significant development investment is directed at skills that either earn money immediately or build direct foundations for future earnings. The curiosity-driven phase comes later; the next 5 years are about compounding career capital that pays off.
- **Capitalise on Service to Others** — Mentoring and teaching are natural strengths and genuine sources of enjoyment. Actively map them into how I earn — through consulting, a role that formally includes coaching, or other structures where helping others is the product. Enjoyment and income should not be separate here.
