> *Choosing between options feels easier than deciding from scratch — but easier isn't the same as better.*

# System 1 Thinking and AI-Generated Options

## The Options Advantage

When an AI presents you with a set of options rather than a single answer, it creates a genuinely better workflow:
- The AI does the grunt work of generation
- You retain the final decision as the human in the loop
- The cognitive load is lower than producing the output yourself
- You get exposure to approaches you might not have considered

This halfway point — between "AI does everything, you approve it" and "you think of everything manually" — is often the most effective mode of working with AI tools.

## The System 1 Risk

The ease of option selection may also be its vulnerability. Choosing between presented options is a System 1 task:
- **System 1** (fast, intuitive, pattern-matching) kicks in — you scan the options, one feels right, you pick it
- **System 2** (slow, deliberate, analytical) is not automatically engaged just because options are presented

This matters because System 1 is where cognitive biases live:
- **Anchoring** — the first option shapes how you evaluate the others
- **Confirmation bias** — you're drawn to the option that matches what you already believed
- **Framing effects** — how the options are worded influences the choice independently of their actual merit
- **Satisficing** — you pick the first option that feels "good enough" rather than evaluating all of them rigorously

## The Implication for AI Workflows

Presenting options doesn't guarantee rigorous evaluation — it just makes the interface easier. If you're reviewing AI-generated options with System 1, you may be:
- Approving outputs that reinforce existing biases rather than challenging them
- Missing the option that would have been best if you'd thought it through
- Confusing "this one resonates" with "this one is correct"

## The Antidote

- **Consciously engage System 2 before selecting** — ask "would I have generated this option myself if I'd thought it through independently?"
- **Evaluate each option with equal effort** — not just the one that immediately appeals
- **Add your own option to the list** before reviewing the AI's — this forces independent thought first
- **Name the bias you're most likely to apply** — if you already have a preference going in, flag it before you look at the options

## Key Takeaways

- Options-based AI interaction is a powerful workflow — grunt work delegated, decision retained
- But ease of selection can engage System 1, not System 2 — making it feel more rigorous than it is
- System 1 is where cognitive biases operate: anchoring, confirmation, framing, satisficing
- Deliberately apply System 2 evaluation before selecting: force independent assessment, not just recognition
- Related: [[confirmation-bias-and-assumptions]], [[decision-making-under-pressure]]

## My Pattern

The pull toward the option that "resonates" — without rigorous evaluation — is especially relevant when reviewing AI outputs under time pressure or when fatigued. The ease of the interface creates a false sense of rigour; the decision still requires deliberate thinking even when the generation was delegated.
