# Knowledge and Mental Models

## The Problem With Passive Knowledge
- Accumulating lessons, notes, and insights without applying them is waste — it is "stock" in the lean manufacturing sense: held cost with no output
- The test of useful knowledge is not *"did I read it?"* but *"did I change my behaviour because of it?"*
- Repeating the same mistake at a second company after documenting it at the first is the clearest sign of this failure mode

## Principles

### Read the whole before building the model
- Building mind maps or notes from only the first section of a document leads to incorrect mental models that require expensive reworking
- Read the entire document once (skim for structure) before making any notes or structured summaries
- The full context changes what the parts mean — early detail is often explained or corrected later

### Always start with the basics
- When troubleshooting (technical or otherwise), the most complex-looking problem frequently has a simple, fundamental cause
- The creative brain enjoys interesting theories; resist this and check the fundamentals first
- Apply to non-technical problems too: *"Am I generating creative solutions for the wrong problem?"*

### Evaluate new patterns before adopting them
- When encountering a new approach or principle, evaluate whether it actually holds — don't force-fit it into existing frameworks
- New patterns require conscious testing; old habits will fill the space otherwise
- Force habits around principles that have been proven to work

### Review lessons learnt regularly
- A lesson learnt that is never re-read is equivalent to a lesson never learnt
- Schedule periodic reviews of lessons learnt; otherwise the same situations recur and the same costs are paid again
- Knowledge that just sits in notes is stock — activate it or it depreciates

## The Knowledge Activation Loop

1. **Acquire** — read, observe, experience
2. **Structure** — extract the principle, not just the anecdote
3. **Store accessibly** — in a form you will actually revisit (hence this wiki)
4. **Review** — schedule regular re-reads of key lessons
5. **Apply** — consciously change behaviour based on the principle
6. **Evaluate** — did the new behaviour produce better results? Refine the model

## My Pattern

Lessons documented but not revisited lead to repeating mistakes. The Personal Log section exists to surface and review lessons regularly. The challenge is moving from accumulation to activation (reviewing, applying, evaluating).

## Key Takeaways
- Knowledge is only valuable when it changes behaviour — everything else is stock
- Read whole documents before making notes; partial context creates wrong models
- Start troubleshooting with fundamentals; resist the pull of interesting complexity
- Lessons learnt reviews are not optional — without them, you pay for the same lessons twice
- Force positive habits; left unmanaged, default patterns will reassert themselves
