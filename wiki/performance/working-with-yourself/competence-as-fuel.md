# Competence as Fuel

## The Pattern

Terminal work -- building things, analysing systems, learning technical concepts -- gives immense positive energy. It feels good. It produces a reliable dopamine hit, likely amplified by ADHD hyperfixation. The state is one of genuine contentment: sitting, working, making progress in this mode is intrinsically rewarding.

This has been the default operating pattern for years. The risk is not the pattern itself; it is undervaluing it.

## What Happens When It Stops

When terminal work stops (e.g., avoided because it felt like a distraction, or because cert-mode was deprioritised), the confidence source goes quiet. Without the repeated reminder that "I am good at this," self-esteem drifts down. You forget you are good at stuff.

The 2026 experience confirms this: avoiding learning mode because it might distract from other priorities had a kernel of truth, but the cost was a slow, unnoticed erosion of self-belief. Picking up AI work reversed it, not because AI is special, but because the underlying pattern of building-and-getting-better was restored.

## The Lesson

- Never underestimate how much getting better at things fuels confidence. It is not a nice-to-have or a distraction -- it is a primary energy system.
- Terminal work is not "avoiding real work." It is a legitimate, productive mode that produces output, skill growth, and emotional regulation.
- When the mode is absent for extended periods (weeks+), check for self-esteem drift. It will not announce itself.
- Low-value tasks and political work drain energy disproportionately. Recognise this as a feature of the operating system, not a character flaw, and design around it.
- The confidence feedback loop: build something -> remember you are good at this -> feel better -> build more.

## Key Takeaways

- Terminal work (building/analysing/learning) is a primary energy and confidence source, not a distraction
- Extended absence from this mode causes silent self-esteem erosion
- The confidence feedback loop is self-reinforcing: build, remember capability, feel better, build more
- Low-value and political work drain energy -- design work allocation to protect the fuel source
- Schedule competence-building time explicitly; do not leave it to spare cycles
