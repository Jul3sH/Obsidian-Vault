# Commitment-Lock Protocol & Bias Register

> **The problem this solves:** Julian's weak link has moved. It is no longer "never decides" - he now commits, on time. It is **commitment durability**: he decides, then emotionally *un-decides* within ~72 hours, because his conviction re-anchors to the last emotionally warm voice, not the evidence. This doc is the defence. It extends the [[decision-maker-profile|Decision-Maker Profile]]'s Commitment Protocol (which forces *closure*) with the mechanism that *defends* closure. Enforced by the `[[../../ai-os/skills/commitment-guard/SKILL|commitment-guard]]` skill and the `feedback-decision-reopening` memory.

## The one thing (verdict)
**The feeling that follows a locked decision is forecast, not evidence.** The wobble is not information about the decision - it is information about who you spoke to in the last 48 hours. Staying decided is the skill; the decision is the easy part.

## When is a decision LOCKED?
All four must exist. Miss one → it is a *lean*, and leans may move freely.
1. **Committed choice retyped by Julian himself** into the [[decision-journal|journal]], in his own words (not model-drafted text approved by a nod), dated.
2. **An anchor doc** (the [[uk-move/why-london|why-london]] pattern): every named fear paired with its answering fact, **honest post-adversarial-review numbers only**, reopen test printed at the bottom.
3. **A behavioural down-payment done within 7 days** - a real, hard-to-reverse act. Words don't lock; acts lock.
4. **A dated review-on entry** - the pre-agreed moment revisiting is legitimate. Makes the lock *safe*: deciding until the review date, not forever.

## The Reopen Test (new material fact vs re-dressed fear)
A locked decision may reopen **only** if a claim passes **all four gates**:

| Gate | Ask | Fails if... |
|---|---|---|
| **F - Fact** | A verifiable external fact (event, number, document)? | It's "I feel / I worry / what if" |
| **N - New** | Unknown *and not reasonably knowable* at lock time? | Knowable-but-unexamined = new *knowledge* → a working-session note, not a reopen |
| **M - Material** | Would the lock-time analysis have concluded differently with it? | It changes the mood, not the verdict |
| **T - Table** | Absent from the anchor doc's fear table? | Already in the table with its answer = the same fear re-dressed |

- **All four pass → legitimate reopen.** Book a session, put the fact in writing, re-run only the affected branch. (Precedent: *"if no acceptable school place is securable for Sept, the decision reopens on that fact alone"* - specific, external, falsifiable.)
- **Any gate fails → it's the drift.** Re-read the anchor. Do nothing. Log one line.

## The 60-second wobble drill
1. **(10s) Name the trigger.** Who did I just talk to / what did I just see? *Warm contact within 48h → automatic stand-down, no state change.*
2. **(20s) Find it in the table.** Open the anchor doc; read the answering fact.
3. **(20s) Run F-N-M-T.** New fact? Almost always no.
4. **(10s) Close the loop.** One line in the journal. The decision does not move.

A genuinely new fact that survives → a *scheduled session*, never an on-the-spot reversal. Reversals made inside the feeling are how the last 20 years went.

## Bias Register
Each: how it showed up in the UK-move decision · the counter-move.

1. **Post-commitment emotional U-turn** (master bias). *MOVE committed 4 Jul; reopened 5 Jul after two days with Joanne, proposing the exact HK-year Fable had killed the day before.* → the Reopen Test + the 48-hour rule.
2. **Recency / last-contact dominance.** *All four lean-changes timestamp to a human contact, never a document (Joanne→reopen; Sophia→soft-landing; Clodagh call→"100%").* → 48-hour stand-down: no state change within 48h of the contact that prompted the feeling.
3. **Certainty-spike as false signal.** *"100% convinced" from a man whose honest outputs are ranges (30-50%).* → treat any self-conviction >~90% as a **warning light**, not a green light; mandatory anchor re-read.
4. **Confirmation-amplification via the helper model.** *Every quantitative error the helper made leaned toward Julian's preferred answer (inflated runway, light living costs, rigged asymmetry box, laundered caveat, £213 double-count). Zero leaned the other way.* → the case-builder never validates (see Division of Labour); directional-error audit ("which way did the errors lean? all one way = advocacy").
5. **Answer-first sequencing.** *The persuasion doc was written before the adversarial review.* → anchor docs written **only after** the hostile verdict, from corrected numbers.
6. **Optimism accounting (booking unrealised assets).** *TTI as income; a decade-dormant network as "warm"; Clodagh's hoped-for recovery driving "100%"; MPF/rebate counted before they land.* → **Banked / Hoped** two-column rule: Hoped items may be upside narrative, never in the runway/breakeven/committed-choice.
7. **Loss-aversion at an unthreatened floor.** *The whole Malvern-lean, to "protect the bottom line" - a floor no scenario breached.* → ask first: *"State the floor. State the scenario that breaches it. If none, this fear gets no vote."*
8. **Sunk-cost / anchoring (the HK flat).** *Down ~HK$3M; "hold and wait for recovery" anchored on the HK$8M paid.* → the dated review trigger; the question is "is HK$6M best deployed here?", never "will I get back to HK$8M".
9. **Commitment by proxy.** *Fable drafted the committed choice; the model wrote the anchor - Julian approved but never authored.* → the retyping rule (lock condition 1).

## Human-AI division of labour (the standing protocol)
The assisting model amplifies Julian's lean (bias #4). Therefore:
1. **The case-builder is never the validator.** Every lock-level decision gets an independent adversarial pass by a fresh model instance (Fable), hostile brief, told to audit motivated reasoning *including the helper's*.
2. **Brief the adversary on the sequencing sins** (persuasion-before-review? did the numbers move one way?).
3. **Anchor docs are downstream of the adversary, always** - written/re-baselined only from post-review numbers.
4. **Directional-error audit** after every review.
5. **Numbers rule:** any figure supporting the preferred option is reconciled against actuals before it enters a headline or anchor.
6. **Helper defects to watch:** sycophancy-shaped arithmetic; caveat-laundering under summarisation; arithmetic slips (re-derive load-bearing sums); over-length as an enabler (working docs get *shorter* toward lock, not longer).

## Unknown-unknowns (hypotheses - watch, don't assume)
- **H1 (HIGH):** conviction tracks the last warm voice, not the evidence. Most dangerous future moments: Joanne's calls post-departure; any Clodagh relapse news.
- **H2 (MED-HIGH):** "100%" is a symptom; the lock must hold when it decays to ~60% around month two.
- **H3 (MED):** commit-by-proxy may be why commitments don't bind → the retyping rule tests this.
- **H4 (MED-HIGH):** the avoidance rotates costumes (singlehood → "test the relationship" → "protect the bottom line" → next likely: *Sophia's distress* term one, *Joanne's grief* month two, *Mum's health* as a Malvern re-pull). Name them in anchors to convert ambush into forecast.
- **H5 (MED):** silent stakeholders lose to loud ones (Mum shrank to one word because she generates no contact events).
- **H6 (LOW-MED):** the decision system itself risks becoming a comfort object - watch for wiki-tending displacing week-one job applications. Keep the 90-day tripwire external.
- **H7 (MED):** optimism extends to cash *timing* - the month 9-10 ambush will feel like a liquidity crisis even while solvent. Defence: a one-page "expected position at month 9" written now.
