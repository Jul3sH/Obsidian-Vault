# Choosing a Decision Framework

There are many decision frameworks. The risk with ADHD is analysis paralysis - spending more time choosing a framework than making the decision. This article solves that with a two-question filter.

## The Two-Question Filter

**Q1: Do I know what problem I am actually solving?**

- **No - still fuzzy** → Run [[sharran-simple-decision-framework|Sharran's Steps 1 and 2]] first (understand context, isolate the issue). Then return here.
- **Yes - clear** → Go to Q2.

**Q2: Is this decision reversible?**

- **Yes, easily reversible** → Use [[sharran-simple-decision-framework|Sharran's framework]]. Act fast, get to next steps, review later.
- **No, hard to undo or medium-high stakes** → Go to Q3.

**Q3: Do I have time to research?**

- **No (under an hour)** → Use [[brain-brand-framework|BRAIN/BRAND]]. Structured evaluation without deep research.
- **Yes (hours to days)** → Use the [[seven-step-decision-process|7-step process]]. Full deliberate process.

| Situation | Framework |

## Framework Comparison

| | [[sharran-simple-decision-framework\|Sharran's]] | [[brain-brand-framework\|BRAIN/BRAND]] | [[seven-step-decision-process\|7-step]] |
|--|--|--|--|
| **Speed** | Minutes | 15-60 min | Hours to days |
| **Best for** | Clarity + commitment | Pre-commitment evaluation | Complex or irreversible |
| **Covers alternatives?** | No | Yes | Yes |
| **Covers review/learning?** | No | No | Yes |
| **ADHD-friendly?** | High | Medium | Low (without structure) |

## The ADHD Rule

**When in doubt, default to Sharran's.** A committed next step made in 5 minutes beats a perfect decision made never. The review step (Step 7 of the 7-step process) exists precisely to course-correct - you don't need the first decision to be perfect.

Irreversible decisions are the exception. For anything that cannot be undone - career moves, financial commitments, relationship endings - slow down and use BRAND or the 7-step process regardless of time pressure.

## Key Takeaways

- Analysis paralysis is itself a decision (to do nothing). Treat it as one.
- The two-question filter takes under 30 seconds. Run it before opening any framework.
- Most decisions are more reversible than they feel in the moment. Test that assumption before escalating to a heavier framework.
- No framework covers everything. Sharran's skips alternatives; BRAND skips execution; 7-step skips speed. Knowing the gaps prevents over-reliance on any one tool.
