# Fable adversarial review - Malvern-first reopen + TTI gate (10 Jul 2026)

> Hostile review of the 10 Jul reopen of the London-direct sub-decision (now leaning Malvern-first) and the TTI contingent gate. Briefed to hunt confirmation-amplification in BOTH directions, audit the corrected numbers, and test whether the reopen is a legitimate response to new facts or the drift pattern this system was built to stop. Companion to my own [[fable-review-london-vs-malvern|7 Jul review]], which this partially revisits - including where I got it wrong.

## Verdict: MALVERN-FIRST DOES NOT YET SURVIVE. NEITHER DOES A NAKED RETURN TO LONDON-DIRECT.

The money deterioration is real and I own part of it: the 7 Jul money leg I certified as "survives degraded" was still too optimistic. London all-in is now ~2.7-3.2 years, not ~4, and the liquid-only window is ~10-12 months, not ~20. That is a genuine, material correction.

But the reopen has NOT earned its conclusion. It changed the money and silently dropped the two legs that actually carried London-direct on 7 Jul (Sophia's single-vs-double disruption; the buffer defeating its own goal). Neither leg has been rebutted anywhere. The tenancy trigger as designed is misaligned with the school calendar and structurally converts "one year" into two-plus. The corrected numbers contain real one-sided choices that flatter Malvern. And the reopen bent the protocol's own rules to pass. Both options as currently framed are worse than the honest third option neither document states (see §8).

## 1. Procedural integrity of the reopen - four violations on the system's own terms

1. **The N gate was waived, not passed.** The protocol's own wording: "knowable-but-unexamined = new knowledge → a working-session note, not a reopen." Both "new facts" fail this. The pre-move July burn and moving one-offs were fully computable on 7 Jul (departure date already planned, burn rate known). The car / cleaner / babysitting omissions were not merely knowable - my 7 Jul review explicitly flagged "London living costs - still an estimate; itemise" as a load-bearing unknown, and the 9 Jul reconciliation listed "confirm London car assumption" as open. These are new outputs of continued analysis of old inputs, not new external facts. The journal entry asserts N passes ("not knowable/examined at lock") - that is false by the record.
2. **The 48h stand-down was bypassed the same day it was imposed.** Wobble #2's verdict (same date): "48h stand-down; no state change now." Hours later the same entry chain records a passed reopen and a state change to "leaning Malvern-first." The stand-down exists precisely to prevent same-day escalation. Either the stand-down means something or it does not.
3. **London-direct was never actually LOCKED.** Lock condition 1 (Julian retypes the committed choice in his own words) is still marked outstanding in the journal. So the reopen machinery was run against a lean wearing a lock's clothes. This cuts both ways: procedurally, leans may move freely, so no rule was broken by moving - but it also means the one condition that tests H3 (commit-by-proxy) was skipped, and the decision promptly unwound. Treat this as live evidence FOR H3, and do not let the next lock skip the retyping step.
4. **The reopen quietly un-does the behavioural down-payment.** The Cecil Road notice served 7 Jul was THE hard-to-reverse act anchoring the decision. Malvern-first means re-letting Cecil Road - reversing the down-payment. Per the protocol's own theory, words don't lock, acts lock; un-acting is how drift starts. If Malvern-first proceeds, it needs its own down-payment of equal hardness (see conditions, §9).

**Why this matters even though the money correction is real:** a system that waives its own gates whenever the correction feels big enough is not a system. The honest route was the one wobble #2 itself prescribed: working-session note, 48h stand-down, scoped Fable pass, THEN a state change. The destination might have been the same; the path taken is the drift pattern with paperwork.

## 2. Numbers audit - honest core, one-sided dressing

### 2a. The pot (~£50-55k): plausible but UNVERIFIABLE as presented - fails its own F gate
The reopen's F gate claims "verifiable pot + burn." The burn is itemised. **The pot is not.** No document shows the bridge from £91,869 (7 Jul) to £50-55k. Reconstructing it: minus the £12,475 anticipated tax rebate (correct per Banked/Hoped - I flagged it 7 Jul), minus ~£9k July HK burn, leaves ~£70k; reaching £50-55k requires ~£15-20k of moving one-offs that are nowhere itemised. Demands:
- **Write the bridge table** (start £91.9k → each deduction → landing pot). Until it exists, £50-55k is an estimate that happens to support the current lean, exactly the class of number the numbers rule says must be reconciled before it enters a headline.
- **State whether London-specific one-offs (temp accommodation £3-4k/mo Sept-Nov) are inside the deduction.** If yes, the same pot cannot be used for the Malvern runway row - Malvern's pot is then understated... or rather London's is being double-hit. One pot for two scenarios with different one-off profiles is sloppy either way.
- **The rebate is probable, not dead.** If it lands (typically Aug-Sep), the pot recovers £12.5k (~+2 months London). Correct to exclude it from the runway; wrong to then narrate the cliff-edge as if it does not exist. Show it in a Hoped column.

### 2b. The burn (£5,450 settled / £4,630 lean): the tier was chosen to flatter Malvern
- The headline verdict sentence ("cut London's runway from ~20mo to ~10mo - below a normal search") divides by the **settled** tier. But the runway question is about the **search phase**, and the settled tier includes £900/mo of additions (cleaner 8h/wk £460, babysitting £215, car £225) that are discretionary during an unemployed search in which Julian is home all day. The search-phase divisor is the lean tier: **~11-12 months**, which reaches the bottom of the 12-18 month realistic band rather than sitting clearly below it. Still short - the correction survives - but the headline picked the worse framing when both were available. That is the same flattering-number selection I flagged on 7 Jul, with the sign reversed.
- **The lean tier's composition is documented nowhere.** £4,630 is not £4,550 plus or minus any stated combination of the three additions (5,450 − 460 − 215 − 225 = 4,550). What £80 survives into "lean"? Itemise it or it is a soft number.
- **The car was added to London and not to Malvern.** Wimbledon has £220/mo of TfL already budgeted; a car there is a lifestyle choice. Malvern is a small rural town where a car is close to essential (interviews, Sophia's activities, any West-Corridor access). Adding £225/mo to London only, on the day the answer favoured Malvern, is a directional error. Symmetric treatment: car in both, or car in neither during the search.
- **Malvern's £1,683 has never received the hostility London's number got.** Board at Mum's is £0 ("Mum absorbs"). A £1.2M-net-worth 55-year-old and a teenager living free off an elderly widow for 12+ months is neither realistic nor decent - budget a contribution (~£400-600/mo). Add the car. Malvern honest is plausibly ~£2,300-2,700/mo, and its liquid runway nearer ~1.7-2yr than ~2.5-2.7yr. Malvern still wins the burn comparison decisively - the point is not that the conclusion flips, it is that every un-audited estimate in this file currently leans the same way, and the protocol's own directional-error audit (bias #4) has not been run on today's numbers. I am running it: **since 9 Jul, five corrections moved the comparison toward Malvern/stay (council tax, cleaner, babysitting, car, pot) and one moved it away (DB agency fee one-off). Before 7 Jul, when London was the lean, the errors ran the other way (inflated runway, light living costs, rigged asymmetry box). The analysis amplifies whichever lean is current. That is the single most important sentence in this review.**

### 2c. The MPF framing: a frame change presented as a fact change
"London forces early MPF/pension drawdown" treats the MPF as a sealed pension pot. But the plan of record (model §10, unchanged) is to withdraw the MPF before departure regardless, pay off the £54,264 Cecil mortgage (guaranteed 4.74%, kills the 2033 balloon, cuts London burn by £213/mo), and ISA-shelter the remainder. Post-withdrawal it is a lump of cash whose deployment is a choice. £54k of it moving into Cecil equity is not "spending retirement" - it is transferring it into the asset he lives in. What genuinely changed: total resources deplete faster in London (true, material) and the liquid window before the MPF lands shrank to ~10-12 months against a withdrawal process with execution risk (true, material - this is the strongest version of the reopen's money case and the reopen never states it this precisely). What did not change: the 7 Jul anchor already carried, in writing, "the runway spends the MPF - my only private pension. Affordable, but true." The pension-drawdown fear was in the fear table with its answer. On the T gate, this reopen is closer to the line than the journal admits.

### 2d. Arithmetic
The runway divisions themselves check out (50-55k/5,450 = 9.2-10.1mo; /4,630 = 10.8-11.9mo; 1,683 → 29.7-32.7mo; +MPF figures consistent). No arithmetic errors found.

## 3. The rewritten history - the M gate was run against a claim the record contradicts

The reopen's M-gate argument: "the ~20-month liquid runway was THE reason London beat Malvern; it's gone." **False by the written record.** My 7 Jul verdict, verbatim: London-direct "survives because its two load-bearing legs hold under attack: (1) Sophia's single-vs-double disruption, and (2) the Malvern plan defeats its own goal... The money leg survives degraded." The why-london anchor rests on the ~£216k / ~4yr all-in figure, not on 20 months liquid. Money was the enabler, never the reason.

The honest M-gate question is therefore: **does a ~10-12 month liquid window (with £124.5k landing behind it, ~2.7-3.2yr all-in) overturn a verdict carried by Sophia's single disruption and the buffer's self-defeat?** That is a real question with a defensible yes ("the enabler degraded enough to matter") - but it was never asked. The reopen log does not mention Sophia's double disruption once. Recasting the old decision as resting on the one factor that just moved, so its movement looks automatically decisive, is confirmation-amplification in its purest form, and it is in the canonical journal.

## 4. Steelman - what the reopen gets genuinely right

Both directions means saying this clearly:
- **The 7 Jul money leg I certified was still too optimistic.** Two consecutive hostile passes each found London costlier and the pot smaller. I flagged "itemise" and passed the verdict anyway; the pre-departure pot drawdown was a miss in my own review. The correction direction is credible precisely because it is against the grain of the pre-7-Jul errors.
- **The month-9 ambush and the cash cliff now coincide.** My 7 Jul review named the biggest risk: "the money survives it; the mind may not," locating the psychological ambush at month 9-10. On corrected numbers the liquid cliff arrives at month 10-12. The runway must fund Julian's psychology as well as his search, and a plan whose known psychological failure point lands on its cash cliff is genuinely worse than the 7 Jul paperwork showed. This is the strongest pro-Malvern argument available and the reopen never makes it.
- **Pension preservation at 55 is a legitimate weight**, not loss-aversion at an unthreatened floor, because the MPF is the only private pension and a failed London search really does consume part of it. The 7 Jul "affordable, but true" caveat under-weighted sequence risk.
- **Wobble #2's honest correction of wobble #1** (cost-neutrality confirmed by Test A) is exactly what the system should do. Credit where due.

## 5. The Cecil-tenancy trigger - a forcing function pointed at the wrong calendar

The trigger's design intent is right (structural, not willpower: extending requires an active written act). But as specified ("~12mo let → move into Cecil by default") it fails on three counts:

1. **It is misaligned with the school year, and the school year will win.** A ~12-month let starting ~Nov 2026 frees Cecil ~Nov 2027 - the middle of Sophia's Year 9 autumn term. Nobody moves a settled teenager mid-year by default. The ready-made, perfectly reasonable extension ("finish the school year") pushes to summer 2028 - which lands her London entry at the start of Year 10, after GCSE options are chosen at the Malvern school. My 7 Jul review called a mid-GCSE move "near-disqualifying." The next clean move point after that is post-GCSE 2031. **The trigger as designed does not produce a one-year Malvern stay; its most probable outcomes are two years or five.** This is the buffer-that-becomes-permanent trap, built into the trigger that was supposed to prevent it.
2. **It also locks the good case OUT.** If a London job lands at month 3-5 (the plan's own hope), Cecil is let and unavailable for 7-9 more months. The trigger prevents drift and responsiveness alike: it converts the safety net into a straitjacket exactly when the plan succeeds.
3. **Re-letting is one email.** The "active written decision" barrier is the kind of barrier Julian's documented pattern steps over (H3: approvals without authorship). Compare with the 7 Jul down-payment (notice served, real-world clock). A trigger whose override cost is a signature is willpower dressed up.

**The fix, if Malvern-first proceeds:** anchor the trigger to the school calendar, not the tenancy. Let Cecil on an **8-month or 10-month fixed term (or 12-month with a mutual break at month 8) ending June-July 2027**, and pre-commit in writing, now, dated: **Sophia starts a London school in September 2027; the let is engineered to make that the default.** Then the tenancy serves the trigger instead of ambushing it. If a London job lands early, the break clause is the responsiveness valve. A trigger without this alignment should be treated as decorative.

## 6. The commute / pied-a-terre claim - hand-waving, and it breaks the plan's own paperwork

"Commute + 3 nights pied-a-terre for hybrid roles, relocate once the job is secure" fails three tests:

1. **It contradicts the canonical positioning doc.** [[target-role-profile]] defines viability as fully-remote OR daily-commutable from **Wimbledon** (London + West Corridor). From Malvern, the entire commutable-on-site category dies: Malvern to London is ~2h45 each way; Malvern to Reading/Basingstoke is not daily-commutable either. The reopen's own condition ("confirm remote/West-Corridor job-access from Malvern") pre-books an answer geography does not support. Either the role profile is rewritten to remote-only for the Malvern year (shrinking the viable set to the minority slice, in a market where the profile's best lanes - SI presales, BFSI in-house - are hybrid-heavy), or the plan silently assumes the pied-a-terre from day one of employment. Neither reconciliation has been done. **These two documents currently describe different plans.**
2. **The pied-a-terre re-imports the cost and the promise-breach.** A 3-night London base is ~£800-1,200/mo plus £200-400/mo travel - eroding a third of Malvern's burn advantage during employment - and recreates precisely the "Sophia parented midweek by Grandma in her first UK year" breach of the "as long as we're together" promise that the 7 Jul review flagged as a load-bearing strike against the crash-pad plan. That strike was never answered; it has just stopped being mentioned.
3. **"Relocate once the job is secure" collides with the trigger.** Job secure at month 4, Cecil let until month 12+ (see §5.2). The sentence assumes a flexibility the plan's own anti-drift device removes.

## 7. The TTI gate - defensible box, loophole-shaped contents

**Structure: mostly sound.** Pre-registered branch (not a wobble), genuinely dated events (Stephan→Ty this weekend, Horst Ops review Tuesday), pre-committed rule written before the answer, explicit "warm maybe ≠ yes." This is what a disciplined contingency looks like on paper. Four holes:

1. **The original Branch A expired on 9 July and Branch B fired.** The journal's own good-enough bar: "Decide on the information known by 9 July. Do not wait for more information beyond 9 July." Branch A required a positive signal *before 9 July*; none came; Branch B ("lead dies / no signal by 9 July → biggest factor pushing toward the UK") is the branch that actually triggered. The 10 Jul gate is not the pre-registered branch - it is a deadline extension of an expired one, revived by a warm contact (Justin, ~8 Jul). Under H1 (conviction tracks the last warm voice) that provenance demands maximum suspicion. Allowed only because it is short; it must not be extendable.
2. **The prize is a construction, not an offer.** "Permanent, low-tax HK employment while Sophia finishes school" appears in no TTI communication. Justin invented the bridge framing ("I think he needs a bridge scenario"); the permanent conversion is Julian's inference stacked on a stay-biased intermediary's advocacy. Ty has never heard the ask. Stephan has not replied to Julian since 4 Jul (read, no reply; one-pager unopened). The gate's trigger condition is a thing nobody at TTI has ever said.
3. **Even a REAL 6-month consultancy is not Branch A.** Julian's own 4 Jul message: the work is US-aligned and location-independent - "if I move to London, it's the buffer that lets me settle Sophia," and London/US hours beat HK/US. A confirmed 6-month remote gig therefore argues for the MOVE (income bridge), not against it. Only a credible permanent-HK commitment distinguishes Branch A from a move-funding bridge. The gate wording knows this; the risk is that in the warmth of a positive Stephan call, "they want me for 6 months" gets felt as "I can stay." Pre-write the classification: **6-month gig confirmed, no written permanent-conversion intent = Branch B with a bonus. The move executes, funded better.**
4. **"Concrete, credible signal" has no defined artifact and no named date.** Julian, graded by Julian, 48 hours after a warm call, will pass a warm answer. Harden it: the gate clears ONLY on (a) a **written** statement from Ty or Horst (not Stephan's verbal relay, not Justin's summary) that the engagement is intended as a try-before-permanent on-ramp to HK-based employment, with (b) scope, rate, and duration sufficient to bank Sophia's full school year, received by (c) a **named calendar date** (recommend Friday 17 Jul, end of day HK time), with (d) an explicit **no-renewal clause**: this box is not extendable for any reason, including "Horst was positive, give it another week." Anything less, from anyone, by any channel = Branch B, move executes.

**Honest base rate.** Chain: Stephan actually raises it credibly with Ty (his one conversion in this record came from a face-to-face decidable ask; his written-ask record is 100% absorb-don't-act; call it 40-60% given the meeting is real) × Ty agrees a 6-month engagement on a friend's vouch with no scoped need (20-30%) × anyone commits in writing to permanent-conversion intent up front (10-20%, companies do not pre-promise conversion, and Justin's own optimism dates real movement "Q4/Q1") - and all of it inside ~7 days. **P(gate-clearing signal by the deadline): under 10%. P(this path ends in real permanent HK employment at the senior recovery level): ~5-15%.** The gate is worth its 7-day cost only because the deadline is absolute. Its danger is not the week; it is the renewal.

## 8. The under-weighted cases (both directions, as briefed)

**Strongest case AGAINST Malvern-first that the current reasoning under-weights:** the burn saving is dwarfed by the income-timing cost it induces, and the reopen never models search duration by location. Malvern saves roughly £3,000-3,800/mo while jobless. But from Malvern the target-role profile's entire commutable-on-site category is dead, the warm network is 130 miles away, and presence-based networking (the market briefing's #1 prescription) does not happen - the 7 Jul finding "London-direct likely shortens the search; Malvern likely lengthens it" was never rebutted. If Malvern lengthens the search by even four months, the forfeited net income at the £120k level (~£6k+/mo) exceeds a full year of burn savings, and Julian enters the market at 56 with the ageism clock running and ~8-10 working years left. Add the unrebutted Sophia leg: her single move to the city she asked for versus two moves, the second landing mid-Year-9 or at GCSE start (§5.1). The reopen weighed pension preservation against none of this.

**Strongest case that the entire reopening is commitment-avoidance:** the timing signature. Every approach to the irreversible act has produced a softening: 4 Jul commit → 5 Jul Joanne reopen; 7 Jul lock + notice served → 8 Jul wobble; and now, two days before the UK trip and three weeks before departure, a same-day sequence of spreadsheet churn (wobble #2's own named trigger) → threshold crossed → reopen passed → lean flipped → and, the same day, a stay-flavoured HK branch resurrected via a warm, stay-biased intermediary. The "new facts" were endogenous - generated by re-analysing old inputs until a number crossed a line - which is precisely what the N gate exists to catch, and it was waived. The lock's authorship condition was never completed. The behavioural down-payment is being reversed. If a hostile observer wanted to design a drift episode that could pass audit, it would look exactly like this: numerically impeccable, procedurally waved through, destination softer, HK door reopened, all inside 24 hours. **The defence is equally real: the money correction is genuine, the direction of both hostile re-audits was against London, and Malvern-first is still a form of GOING.** The tiebreaker is behavioural, not analytical, and it is the conditions below.

## 9. What would make Malvern-first sound (the conditions)

Malvern-first survives a future review ONLY as a dated staging camp, never as a base. Note the structural fact both docs under-use: Cecil Road is tenanted until ~November regardless, so even "London-direct" meant temp accommodation and address-juggling until then. The honest reframe is not London vs Malvern; it is **"London by [date], staged via Malvern" vs "Malvern and we'll see."** Only the first is admissible.

1. **School-calendar-anchored trigger (replaces the raw tenancy trigger):** written, dated, now: *Sophia starts a London school September 2027 at the latest.* Cecil let on a fixed term ending June-July 2027 (or 12-month with mutual break at month 8). The let's end date IS the London date. Any extension of the Malvern stay requires re-running this decision through the journal with a Fable pass, not a re-letting email.
2. **A behavioural down-payment of equal hardness to the one being reversed**, within 7 days of committing: e.g. the London school application/waiting-list entries submitted for Sept 2027 entry AND the fixed-term tenancy agreement signed with the June/July 2027 end date. Words re-let houses; acts lock decisions.
3. **The pot bridge table written and the lean-tier burn itemised** (§2a, §2b) before any number from this reopen enters an anchor doc. Symmetric Malvern re-baseline (car + board contribution).
4. **Search-phase design that answers the location penalty:** the role-profile location section formally amended for the Malvern year (remote-first; on-site London roles handled via a pre-priced pied-a-terre budget line with the Sophia-midweek question answered in writing, not discovered at offer stage); the floor-income job (wobble #2's own mitigation) started within 30 days of landing, from Malvern.
5. **TTI gate hardened as §7.4:** named date (17 Jul EOD HK), written-artifact bar, Ty/Horst source requirement, no-renewal clause, and the pre-written classification that a location-independent 6-month gig = Branch B, move executes better-funded.
6. **The retyping rule finally executed:** whatever is decided after this review, Julian types the committed choice into the journal in his own words. Two locks have now failed to complete this step; H3 is no longer a hypothesis to watch but a pattern to break.

## Bottom line

The money moved; the reasons did not. A ~10-12 month liquid window colliding with the documented month-9 ambush is a real, material degradation of London-direct, and I under-certified that risk on 7 Jul. But Malvern-first as currently designed is not the answer to it - it is a two-to-five-year stay wearing a one-year costume, contradicting the plan's own job-market paperwork, with its forcing function pointed at the wrong calendar, passed through a reopen that waived its own gates on the same day a stand-down was imposed, while the one HK branch that would dissolve the whole move was revived on the word of an intermediary who demonstrably filters what Julian is allowed to hear. If Malvern-first is chosen, it is admissible ONLY in the §9 form: London dated in writing (Sept 2027), the let engineered to enforce it, the down-payment made within 7 days, and the TTI box sealed with a written-artifact bar and a no-renewal clause. Otherwise the 7 Jul verdict stands: go direct, lean budget, floor job early, MPF deployed per plan.
