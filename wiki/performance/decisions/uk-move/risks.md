## R1 - Marriage exposure (gates S2's upside; = belief B5)

**The entanglement:** S2's best case (the marriage-enabled cake business, Julian's confidence **1M+ HKD**) and its biggest financial risk come from the **same act - marrying Joanne.** You cannot take the cake upside without taking the marriage risk. So this is one coupled decision, not two.

### The exposures, sized

| Exposure | Size | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct support of Joanne's two sons** (Philippines) | **~£4k/yr minimum** | Small and manageable. Julian's read: she's hardworking and could likely earn this herself. Not the real risk. |
| **Divorce loss** | Potentially large | Mitigable, not eliminable - prenup gives *significant* (not absolute) weight in England post-*Radmacher* ([[analysis|A5]] fact-check). Needs a family solicitor. |
| **Long-run inheritance dilution away from Sophia** | **The real risk** | Julian's honest fear: over years together she'd want more of his savings for her children, eroding what's available to Sophia. Open-ended, and the exposure that matters most to him. |

### Why the dilution risk is the heavy one

It's not the £4k/yr (trivial) or even the divorce settlement (partly legislated by prenup). It's the **open-ended, relational** drift: years of pressure to divert savings to her sons at Sophia's expense. This is the same pattern Julian names in the [[../../../relationships/Dad/relationship-dynamics|Dad dynamic]] (dependency becoming leverage) and the same **change-after-security fear** rooted in [[../../../relationships/clodagh|Clodagh]] (see [[../../../relationships/relationship-views|relationship views]]).

### Mitigants (and their limits)

1. **Prenup** - real but partial protection ([[analysis|A5]]). Do it properly: independent advice both sides, full disclosure, 28+ days pre-wedding.
2. **Ring-fence Sophia's inheritance** *before* marrying - will/trust structuring that keeps Sophia's assets separate from marital property. A family solicitor can build this. **This directly attacks the heavy risk.**
3. **Residual, un-legislatable part:** long-term relational pressure to concede "for peace and quiet" - exactly Julian's documented [[../../../relationships/relationship-views|pattern]] (B5, Clodagh). Legal structure caps the *asset* risk; it cannot cap the *behavioural* one. This is the piece Julian must own with eyes open, not wish away.

### Verdict on B5 ("if I marry I'll be trapped and forced to give in financially")

**Survives as a real but substantially mitigable risk - not a veto.** The mechanism is genuine (open-ended dilution pressure), but the *financial* exposure is largely addressable (prenup + ring-fencing Sophia's inheritance done properly, pre-marriage). What remains is a behavioural risk that matches Julian's own history - to be managed with structure + awareness, not treated as destiny. **Shape mirrors B1/B7: felt as a veto, tests down to a manageable, bounded cost.**

### Decision implication

- S2 is only worth pursuing if Julian is willing to (a) marry Joanne **and** (b) put the legal protections (prenup + Sophia ring-fence) in place *first*. Without the protections, the dilution risk runs uncapped.
- This keeps the marriage decision **coupled to S2** but **decoupled from the UK scenarios** (S3/S4 don't need it). So: choosing an HK-build future means choosing the marriage + its risk-management; choosing a UK future does not force the marriage question at all.
- **Update 2026-07-03 (see [[b1-relationship-belief|B1 deeper clarification]]):** Julian's clearer stated want is to **stay single and free** - he's "scared of" the marriage and doesn't want the commitment for its own sake. If that holds, the marriage is **unwanted except instrumentally** (to enable the cake business), which **substantially weakens S2** - you don't marry against your core desire for a business. Net: the genuine HK pull is not S2's business case but singlehood + dating optionality; S2 should be down-weighted accordingly at B/D.

## R2 - Execution risk (build-or-drift) - shared by S2 and S3

**The threat:** both build scenarios assume Julian *delivers* on a self-directed build. His track record says that's the open question. The base-rate evidence is not kind, and honesty requires stating it plainly.

### The evidence against (the base rate)

- **The killer data point:** Julian has effectively been **semi-retired in HK - learning AI, building things - and it produced no income** ([[capture]]). He had the time, the tools, and the freedom, and no revenue resulted.
- ADHD pattern: "terrible at *starting* businesses - never gets started"; procrastination; rejection-avoidance keeps him from putting himself out there.
- Documented [[commitment-avoidance]] profile.
- **The ADHD travels.** The same dynamics operate in the UK as in HK - location does not fix this.

### The evidence for (and the crucial distinction)

Julian's execution is **not uniform - it's environment-dependent.** He has two modes:

| Drift mode (fails) | Execute mode (delivers) |
|---|---|
| Startup from scratch, no demand | **Existing business with a clear target** |
| Open-ended self-directed build | Defined role + his skill (AI/marketing) |
| Mundane/low-value tasks | Big deals, flow-state building |
| No external accountability | Someone expecting delivery |

**Reinterpreting the killer data point:** the no-income HK period was *drift-mode work* - self-directed, no external target, no existing business, no proven demand. So it's strong evidence of his **drift mode**, but it does **not** test his **execute mode** (operating an existing business to a clear target) - which he has *not actually tried* here.

### So "what's different this time?" has a real answer - but it's not location

The honest answer cannot be "the UK" (ADHD travels) or "fire in belly" (he's felt motivated before). It has to be **structure**:

- **Anchor only to the existing-business legs** (cake, Adam's) with proven demand and clear targets. Treat the "own geographically-independent business" vision as *someday-maybe*, **not** part of the income plan - that vision is squarely his drift zone and is the "ADHD-friendly narrative that makes leaving feel better than it is."
- **Build external accountability:** a partner expecting delivery (Joanne, Adam), and a **hard revenue target by a hard date**.
- **A pivot trigger** (the commitment mechanism his profile requires): *"£X / 1M-HKD run-rate by month N, or I stop building and take a job."* Without this, the build has no floor and becomes the [[commitment-avoidance|drift]] failure mode under a nicer name.

### Verdict

**Execution risk is HIGH for both build scenarios, mitigable in principle by structure but UNPROVEN in practice.** The execute-mode hypothesis is real but Julian has not yet demonstrated sustained delivery even on an existing business. Therefore:

> **Build income must be planned as speculative upside, not as a floor.** The dependable floors are the *job* paths (S1/S4 - land a role and show up). A sound plan pursues the build only *on top of*, or with a hard pivot back to, a viable job fallback.

### Implication (carried to B/D, not concluded here)

- This does **not** cleanly favour either location on execution grounds - the ADHD travels. But note: three of four build streams are cheaper/equal from the UK ([[alternatives]]), and the UK's dependable *job* floor (S4 - bigger market, no language barrier) looks more reliable than HK's (S1 - P 30-50%). HK forces betting the high burn on either a below-even-odds job **or** an unproven build+marriage; the UK lets the speculative build run cheaply *behind* a more reliable job floor.
- **Both job paths carry their own execution risk** - the outreach/networking Julian avoids (rejection-sensitivity). That risk is already inside P(work); R2 is the *additional* delivery risk that sits only on the build paths.

## R3-R6 - The remaining beliefs (B4, B6, B8, B9)

### B4 - "HK is increasingly hard for an old white guy"

- **Attribution test:** is the difficulty *demographic/market* (real headwind) or *behavioural* (weak network + avoided outreach), externally attributed? Julian's own pattern leans to external attribution - HSBC "blocked" he himself called **"perceived"**; the weak network he traces to "embarrassment about reaching out" and RSD.
- **But partly real:** HK genuinely favours local, Chinese-language relationships and younger candidates; age bias exists everywhere.
- **Verdict:** **B4 does not support staying either way.** If it's *true*, it lowers P(HK work) → argues for **leaving**. If it's *attribution-cover*, discount it. It is never a pro-stay factor - it's a headwind on the HK path or an excuse, not a reason to take it.

### B6 - "I'm relying on luck in HK"

- **Honest read vs excuse:** partly honest (weak network → opportunities arrive semi-randomly), but it's really the **P(he executes networking) factor showing up as fatalism.** "Luck" is low *because* the networking offensive isn't being run - a description of a **choice**, not a fixed reality.
- **Verdict:** read B6 as *"relying on luck UNLESS I run the offensive I've been avoiding."* It doesn't argue for staying - it warns that the HK path only works if Julian does the exact thing (outreach) his history says he avoids. Reinforces the S1 P-skepticism and the R2 execution point. Neutral-to-pro-move.

### B8 - "Sophia will get bullied and disengage academically in UK" (⚠️ con-of-moving)

- Julian himself calls it **"a bit irrational."**
- **Probability-weight:** low. She's **academically strong** (15 A / 10 A*), getting older/more mature (Julian: "wouldn't be such a big deal"), a strong record travels, and **school choice is his to control** (the "where in the UK" sub-decision; leverage her Mandarin strength via GCSE - [[analysis|A8]]).
- **The real, non-irrational kernel:** *timing* - a mid-schooling international move has genuine social cost for a teenager; moving **before vs during GCSEs** matters. That's a **planning input** (sequence the move to her school stage), not a reason not to move.
- **Verdict:** not a veto. Low-probability, largely controllable; manage via school choice + move timing.

### B9 - "I'll be depressed for a while in the UK" (con-of-moving)

- **Transition dip vs lasting state:** the honest split. A real transition dip (leaving the sun/resort life, [[b7-lifestyle|B7]]) is likely and should be *planned for*, not catastrophised.
- **Why it's unlikely to be lasting:** the drivers of a *lasting* low - isolation, no purpose, no relationships - are actually **better** in the UK (close to mum + Sophia, golf/social, potential Joanne). A lasting depression would come from **drift/no-income/no-purpose** - i.e. it's the **R2 execution risk wearing an emotional mask**, managed by the R2 structure, not by staying in HK.
- **The genuine lasting component:** grey-winter seasonal low mood (he "hates grey/gloom"). Mitigable - sun holidays, the geo-independent flexibility, winter travel, SAD management.
- **Verdict:** real, mostly **temporary and plannable**; already accepted in the [[reconciliation|pre-mortem]]. Not a veto. Distinguish the dip (accept + plan) from lasting depression (= the drift risk, managed elsewhere).
