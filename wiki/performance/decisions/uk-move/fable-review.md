# Fable Adversarial Review - UK Relocation Decision

> Independent adversarial review of the BRAINED analysis completed by Opus/Sonnet. Reviewer stance: hostile to both the analysis and the gut. Model: claude-fable-5, run 2026-07-04. Feeds [[reconciliation|D]] and [[decision-journal|Decision Journal]].

## 1. What the analysis got wrong

**(a) The £2,100/week headline is inflated.** The honest delta is ~£1,100-1,300/week (HK burn minus UK living costs, not HK burn vs zero). Still decisive, but a commitment built on an inflated number is fragile - when Julian notices it, it becomes an excuse to distrust the whole analysis.

**(b) "Near-zero burn" + "+£1,814/mo rent" may double-count.** Either he occupies the house (no rent income) or keeps the tenant and pays rent elsewhere. Not both. Add tenant notice period: house may not be available for 2-6 months.

**(c) MPF unlock is not upside.** It is his own retirement money. Count it as runway only. The permanent-departure declaration is one-shot and hardens the exit.

**(d) "UK consulting (Westerner advantage)" is incoherent.** Westerner advantage is an Asia phenomenon. In the UK he is a 50-something contractor in a saturated market. S3's income stack is really: one warm unsigned lead (TTI), one partner-dependent handshake business (Adam, no contract), one mislabelled advantage, plus rent. Softer than presented.

**(e) The analysis contradicts itself on execution risk.** It rates drift risk HIGH on both build paths, then recommends a pure S3 build path. The defensible recommendation is S3 with S4 running in parallel from week one as the floor.

**(f) The stay case is internally contradictory - and the analysis under-hammered this.** The gut wants HK for singlehood. But single-in-HK is financed only by S1 (P=30-50%, full-effort assumed). S2, the stronger HK income case, requires marrying Joanne - which destroys the singlehood the gut is staying for. The gut's preferred life is the one HK scenario that cannot pay for itself.

**(g) P(landing) has no time horizon.** 30-50% over six months is a different bet from 30-50% over eighteen.

**(h) Missing: Joanne as a person.** The analysis prices her as a visa-gated revenue stream. Leaving HK ends or long-distances a live two-year relationship. That grief will surface around month two as "I made a mistake." Name it now so it is expected, not a surprise.

**Net verdict:** directionally right, locally sloppy. After correcting (a)-(h), S3+S4 in the UK still dominates. The conclusion stands; the confidence intervals were dressed up.

## 4. The gut-override, evaluated

**The verdict: real and insufficient.**

Real: the attraction-profile density gap (HK vs UK) is a fact. The autonomy want is genuine.

Insufficient, on three grounds:

1. **He does not exercise the option.** Twenty years of partnering up quickly regardless of stated preference. He is with Joanne right now. The single-in-HK life he would be paying £110k/yr to protect does not exist today, and his record says any vacancy gets filled in months. He is buying a season ticket he has never once used for a full season - at the price of his daughter's teens and his mother's remaining years, which are the only non-refundable items on the table.

2. **It is structurally the stall pattern.** "Preserving optionality" is the cleanest-sounding reason not to commit ever invented. Read baldly, the override statement trades non-recoverable family time for a dating pool. It does not survive its own bald form.

3. **It confuses residence with access.** Singlehood is a relationship status, not a postcode. A solvent UK-based Julian with TTI income can spend a month in Asia twice a year. Moving forecloses daily density; it does not foreclose access. He has also conceded he wants a companion in later life - the UK trajectory serves that at least as well.

**Override fails.** Not because the want is fake, but because he demonstrably does not live it, its price is paid in the only currency he cannot earn back, and the good it buys remains reachable from the UK.
