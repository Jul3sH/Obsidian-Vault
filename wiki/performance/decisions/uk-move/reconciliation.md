**Decision framework:** BRAINED (Benefits, Risks, Alternatives, Intuition, Need time, Decision). Hard commit date: **9 July 2026**, before a UK family trip departing 12 July.

**The four live scenarios:**
- **S1** - HK corporate: land a well-paid role in HK, stay in own flat, ~15% tax
- **S2** - HK build: build multiple revenue streams in HK; anchor = Joanne's cake business (marriage-enabled, HK-locked, demand proven, Julian's confidence 1M+ HKD); three other streams portable/weak
- **S3** - UK build: build from mum's; Adam's property business + UK consulting (Westerner advantage) + TTI-style remote engagement; near-zero burn
- **S4** - UK corporate: land a UK role, 1.2-1.8M gross ~50% tax; income-first

**Key facts:** P(HK corporate) = 30-50% (Julian's own honest estimate); annual HK burn = ~£110k/yr; UK property = +£1,814/mo net surplus; MPF pension ~1M HKD unlocks on leaving HK; Sophia gets UK home university fees (~£9,535/yr vs £60k+ overseas) if they move soon; interferon treatment confirmed free on NHS.

**Current relationship:** Joanne, Filipino, ~20 years younger; 2 years together; she wants marriage long-term; cannot relocate to UK without spousal visa; domestic-helper visa bars her from running a legal business in HK without marriage. Marriage motivation on Julian's side: largely (a) to unlock the cake business and (b) fear of not finding someone attractive in the UK. Not deep want to marry her specifically.

### Step 2a - The gut-override test (write it baldly)

The protocol requires Julian to state the override in writing before it counts. The form:

> *"I choose to stay in HK - accepting the ~£110k/yr runway burn on a 30-50% job hunt, the constraint on Sophia's schooling window, distance from my mum, and the loss of the UK's lower financial floor and better career launchpad - because keeping access to a high-density pool of women I find attractive and the freedom of a single, uncommitted life in Asia matters more to me than all of those things."*

**This is the gut-override test. Fable's job: evaluate whether this stated preference is sufficient, rational, and durable enough to override the analysis - or whether it is a form of the [[commitment-avoidance]] pattern dressed as a genuine preference. The override is only valid if it holds when stated this plainly.**

One honest question the override must answer: Julian's own stated pattern is to partner up quickly regardless of location. Is he choosing singlehood/optionality - or is he choosing to preserve *access* to singlehood/optionality, which is a different (and cheaper) thing that does not require staying in HK?

## Step 3 - The single deciding lever: P(well-paid HK work)

The financial model reduces to:

> If P(landing a well-paid HK role within runway) **≥ ~50%**, the HK bet is worth making as an *active, deadlined* bet. If **< ~50%**, the UK move is the higher-expected-value choice.

**Julian's honest P (2026-07-02): 30-50%.** Below the breakeven. Three additional tie-breakers widen the gap toward moving:

1. **Asymmetric downside:** moving is capped + PR allows return; the failed HK bet burns ~£110k then forces the move anyway, a year older and poorer
2. **Effective P is lower:** must be multiplied by P(Julian actually runs the outreach he habitually avoids due to RSD/ADHD)
3. **Drift gravity:** at this P the live temptation is not a disciplined HK bet but slippage into drift - Option C, which is forbidden

**Income-portability update:** the bar STAY must clear has risen because the MOVE option has a warm near-term income bridge (TTI remote engagement). So the breakeven P for staying is now effectively higher than ~50%; STAY needs to be the better bet even against an improved MOVE.

## Step 5 - Pre-mortem

*Completed by Fable (adversarial review, 2026-07-04). Full detail in [[fable-review]].*

### Pre-mortem A: Move happens, S3 first - what goes wrong

*March 2027, it failed. The specific sequence:*

1. TTI never signed. Warm stayed warm. Julian emotionally booked it as income and structured nothing else around it.
2. Adam's business produced nothing attributable. No written agreement on his share; awkwardness, not income.
3. The consulting pipeline was 6-9 months long and Julian did not run it. No office, no cadence, November darkness. Drift mode by week six. ADHD travelled, exactly as predicted.
4. Housing friction. Tenant on notice until January; double housing costs; the "near-zero burn" premise arrived four months late.
5. Sophia was polite but busy. Mum-time skewed toward admin and worry before it felt like reward.
6. The dating desert confirmed B1. The resentment narrative wrote itself. Julian browsed HK jobs from the weakest possible position.

**Mandatory controls that prevent this:**
- S4 applications from week one in parallel - not after S3 fails
- Weekly external cadence with one named human accountability contact
- **90-day tripwire:** no signed revenue by day 90 means the corporate search becomes the day job the next morning. No debate. No live-process exception.

**Worst case accepted:** twelve months on, no build income beyond rent, ~£45k burned, ending in a mid-level UK corporate role at £110-130k with 40%+ tax, in a life greyer than the Peak. That floor is employment near his daughter and his mother. HK's worst case is a drained account far from both. **The worst case of Option A beats the worst case of staying.**

### Pre-mortem B: Deadlined HK bet - what goes wrong

*January 2027, it failed. The sequence:*

1. Sprint starts strong for three weeks, decays to partial effort. Realised P falls below the quoted 30-50%.
2. Two real processes emerge in September/October.
3. Deadline week - the kill shot: one process says "final round next month." The deadline does not survive contact with a live process. Julian extends four weeks. Then four more. Drop-dead date is dead - the commitment-avoidance pattern wearing a deadline as a costume.
4. January: £55-65k poorer, same decision, weaker position, worst season to move.
5. Even winning is a trap: landing the role locks in 2-3 more HK years; the job consumes the singlehood he stayed for; Sophia and mum windows close anyway.

**Option B is only admissible with a pre-signed exit executed automatically on the date - flights booked, notices drafted, no live-process exception. Julian's documented record says he will not sign that clause. That refusal is the answer: Option B is not a bet, it is drift with a schedule.**
