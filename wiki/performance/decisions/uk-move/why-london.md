# Why London - read this when the fear comes back

> **Purpose:** Julian gets rationally confident at the screen, then fear takes over away from it and he drifts. This is the anchor. When the doubt hits, **re-read this before acting on the feeling.** It is not new information you're getting when anxious - it's the same fear with the evidence temporarily out of view. See [[commitment-avoidance]] and [[decision-maker-profile]].

## The one thing to remember
**London is both what I want *and* the prudent choice.** Usually the exciting option and the safe option pull apart. Here they've converged. That is rare, and it means choosing London is not reckless - **it's the low-risk move that also happens to be the one I want.** Fire in the belly *and* a clear conscience.

## The decision
Move to the UK (committed) → **land in London (live in Cecil Road), not a buffer year at Mum's.** Final gate: Fable review. Deadline discipline applies - decide, don't re-open on feeling.

## The asymmetry (why London is the *safe* choice, not the brave one)
Honest comparison - London vs a *properly committed, dated* Malvern-first (not a strawman):
- **London worst case:** a long (12-18 mo) search; ~£55-70k of a runway I can afford; land later than hoped - floor fully intact, in the city I want, network working, Sophia settled once.
- **Committed Malvern-first worst case:** a dull year I'd hate; ~£29k saved; then the plan defeats itself (any real job is London-hybrid → crash-pad → Sophia orphaned midweek in her first UK year); Sophia moved **twice**; I enter the market at **56**; a year away from my network.
London still wins the honest comparison - it doesn't need the thumb on the scale. The genuinely reckless option is the open-ended stall (HK, or "Malvern and we'll see"): burn, age out, drift.

## The test to run when a new doubt appears
Ask: **"Is this a NEW FACT, or the same fear re-dressed?"**
- New fact I haven't accounted for → bring it to the next working session; we'll test it honestly.
- No new fact, just the feeling → **it's the drift. Re-read this page. Do not act on it.**
