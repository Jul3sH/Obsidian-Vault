# Income portability - the 4 Jul insight that improves the MOVE case

> A new input surfaced while working the TTI engagement: **Julian's income-generation is location-independent.** This note records the insight and how it updates [[alternatives|A]], the [[uk-move-financial-model|financial model]], and the [[reconciliation|D]] step. Additive only - the BRAINED I/N/D reasoning continues in [[reconciliation]] / the [[decision-journal|journal]].

## The insight
The live TTI opportunity (via Stephan to Ty) is a **remote consulting engagement aligned with US stakeholders, deliverable from anywhere**, and TTI's own default is an **initial six months, then assess**. So there is now a **warm, near-term, location-flexible income source** on the table that the original model did not have.

It is a *better-quality* lead than anything in the current build column: not the speculative, cold "AI consulting" stream (weak), and not the cold "UK Westerner-advantage consulting" - it is a **warm, relationship-sourced, US-aligned remote engagement**, and it is delivered *better from a UK base* (London/US timezone beats HK/US by ~7-8 hours of overlap).

## How it updates the 2x2 ([[alternatives]])

**A new income engine for the income-engine table:**

| Income engine | Locked to | Powers | Reality check |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TTI / US-aligned remote consulting** (Pudwill lead) | Location-flexible; **better from UK** (timezone) | S3, S4 bridge (also S1/S2) | Warm, near-term (pitch sent 4 Jul). Initial 6 months, modest - a *bridge*, not the S1 jackpot. Not yet confirmed. |

**Effect per scenario:**
- **S3 (UK build) - strengthened most.** S3's one real weakness was execution/drift risk ("proven demand is not proven delivery by Julian"; the semi-retired-produced-no-income pattern). A **warm, structured client engagement is a far better failure mode than a self-directed build** - it is someone else's initiative, with a deadline and a payer, not Julian generating from a standing start. It also gives S3 a concrete near-term income bridge instead of only speculative ventures, and it is *better delivered from the UK*. [[benefits|B]] already found S3 maps best to Julian's stated ideal life; this de-risks its weakest point.
- **S4 (UK corporate) - supported.** Same remote/timezone logic; a bridge while a UK role is lined up.
- **S1/S2 (HK) - marginally, and not decisively.** The engagement could be HK-based, but it is modest and location-flexible, so it creates **no HK-specific advantage** and does not strengthen the "stay for the HK tax jackpot" case (that still needs a large HK-taxed role).

## How it updates the [[uk-move-financial-model|financial model]]
The MOVE column assumed income was roughly property surplus + speculative build. The insight **raises the MOVE income floor** to: property surplus + a warm remote engagement (TTI 6mo) + UK Westerner-advantage consulting. Therefore:
- **MOVE's expected value rises and its downside caps further** - the "move and drift with no income" worst case is now bridged.
- **The bar STAY must clear rises.** Staying was justified only if P(large HK-taxed role) was roughly at or above the ~45-50% breakeven. The alternative just got better, so staying now needs an even higher P - and the *only* location-specific prize is the HK tax advantage on a big role, which the modest TTI engagement does not provide.

## Honest caveats
- **Not confirmed.** The TTI engagement is a pitch (message + one-pager + CV to Stephan, for Ty), sent/going 4 Jul. Treat as a **warm probabilistic** income source, not a guarantee.
- **Modest.** An initial six months is a *bridge*, not the S1 tax jackpot. Its value is de-risking the transition, not matching HK upside.
- **Structural point survives even if TTI specifically fails:** Julian's earning capacity (US-aligned + UK remote consulting) is location-independent, so **moving does not forfeit his ability to earn** - it forfeits only the HK tax advantage on a large role he would still have to land.

## Net (for the D step)
This **strengthens the MOVE case, S3 especially** - the direction Julian is already leaning - by giving the UK build a warm, near-term, better-from-UK income bridge and de-risking its one real weakness (execution/drift). It creates **no** new reason to stay. For D: the move's downside is now more clearly capped, and its most-likely path (S3 with a TTI-style bridge) is *both* his stated ideal life *and* materially de-risked.
