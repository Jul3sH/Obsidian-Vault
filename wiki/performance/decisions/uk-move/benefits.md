# B - Benefits (upside per scenario, per domain)

> The **B step** of [[why-brained|BRAINED]]. Job: rank all four live scenarios on their genuine upside across all six domains. Asymmetric challenge applies: because the gut leans STAY, **S1/S2 benefits are challenged hard (⚠️)**; S3/S4 benefits get a confirm-they're-real pass. This is not a repeat of R (risks/downsides) - this is purely about upside. Feeds [[reconciliation|D]].

## The anchor: Julian's stated ideal life

From the [[capture|brain dump]], his clearest statement of what he actually wants:

> *"Working for myself, building my own company, not stressing myself out, living in the UK but with the flexibility to travel whenever I want, and creating enough money to retire. Maybe my lifespan won't be so long, but at least I will have had ten years of low-stress, healthy life."*

This is the North Star for B. The question is: which scenario delivers it best?

## Domain deep-dives (with asymmetric challenge)

### Wellbeing

**S1 ⚠️ challenge:** The "HK wellbeing is better" belief is true *in steady state with income*. Julian is not in steady state. He is mid-job-hunt with no income. Between now and landing (if he lands), the wellbeing picture is the opposite of the headline: financial stress, rejection from applications, RSD-loaded outreach, the drift anxiety that has been building. The high-wellbeing state is locked behind the 30-50% probability of landing. P-weighted, the HK-stay wellbeing benefit shrinks considerably.

**S2 ⚠️ challenge:** The "building together = purpose + excitement" framing is real as an upside - but conditional on two things that are currently uncertain: (a) the marriage Julian is ambivalent about, and (b) the execution Julian has not demonstrated after a year of semi-retirement. If drift mode kicks in, S2 produces the *lowest* wellbeing of the four scenarios: burning £110k/yr, no progress, building anxiety, the sun still out but serving no purpose. S2 is the highest-variance wellbeing bet: best case genuinely excellent, worst case genuinely the worst.

**S3:** Near-zero burn eliminates the financial stress that is already eroding Julian's HK wellbeing. Mum provides grounding. The transition dip (B9) is real and planned for - it is temporary. Lower ceiling on lifestyle enjoyment, but a floor that holds. If executing on Adam's/UK consulting, purpose arrives quickly.

**S4:** Income eliminates financial stress immediately on landing. External structure suits his ADHD. The "grey winter" emotional risk is real and persistent, but manageable (sun holidays, SAD planning). Wellbeing is reliable rather than exciting.

**Verdict:** S3/S4 offer more *reliable* wellbeing; S1/S2 offer higher *peak* wellbeing but on conditions that aren't currently met. At this moment in Julian's life, stable floor beats uncertain peak on wellbeing.

### Relationships

**S1 ⚠️ challenge:** Continuing with Joanne without marriage works in HK - she doesn't need a visa to stay. But it defers the marriage question she has made clear she wants answered. This is not a benefit; it is a holding pattern at her expense, and it runs out. The distance from mum and Sophia continues unchecked.

**S2 ⚠️ challenge:** "Building together" with Joanne is a genuine relationship benefit *if the marriage is entered genuinely* - a shared project, a shared vision, deepening commitment. But Julian has now stated his marriage motivation is largely business + fear of not finding someone in the UK. A marriage entered on those grounds is not a strong foundation for the "build together" relationship benefit. The headline is attractive; the condition under which it's real is not currently in place.

**S3:** Near mum and Sophia is the largest relationship benefit in the entire grid. Julian's mum is a person he wants more time with; Sophia's formative years are passing. This is not recoverable time. Joanne joins only if married - if that's off the table, Julian is single in the UK. Clodagh (warmth, even as friends) is a quiet pull. The equal-footing Western companionship Julian described as potentially more rewarding ([[relationship-views]]) is more accessible here.

**S4:** Same as S3 on relationships. Income security also makes Julian a more stable partner/father figure - worth noting.

**Verdict:** S3/S4 deliver the relationships Julian has said he values *at this life stage* (mum, Sophia). S1/S2 retain Joanne as-is but defer the decision she is owed, and keep Julian far from his family. The relationship "benefit" of staying is largely about not having to make the hard call - which is the [[commitment-avoidance]] pattern, not a genuine relationship advantage.

### Finance

**S1 ⚠️ challenge:** The headline is compelling - 1.2-1.8M HKD at ~15% tax. But it requires landing the role. P(HK work) = 30-50% - Julian's own honest estimate, and by his own words below the ~45-50% breakeven. The expected value calculation:

- Mean case if landed: ~1.5M HKD gross, say 1.2M net after tax = ~£120k/yr
- P(landing) = 40% (midpoint)
- EV of S1 income = 0.4 × £120k = **£48k/yr**
- Annual burn = ~£110k/yr
- EV of net S1 financial position = **-£62k/yr** until landed, then +£10k/yr if landed

The financial *benefit* of S1 is only real in the 40% scenario where Julian lands the role. In the 60% scenario he burns runway and ends up in a worse position than if he had left sooner.

**S2 ⚠️ challenge:** Cake business upside (1M+ HKD) is Julian's own confident estimate if married and building it properly. That upside is real in the world where the marriage happens and the execution follows. Two conditions, each uncertain. Additionally: the 1.1M HKD burn runs during the build period regardless. Financial benefit of S2 = (P(marry) × P(execute) × 1M+ upside) minus the burn. At any realistic probability estimate, the EV is highly sensitive to those two multipliers.

**S3:** No lottery ticket. But: near-zero burn removes the ~£110k/yr drain immediately. Property surplus adds +£1,814/mo (~£22k/yr) net. MPF pension ~1M HKD unlocks on leaving HK (~£100k one-off). Adam's business income: uncertain timeline but an *existing* business with proven demand and Julian's strong working relationship - a real revenue base, not a speculative one. UK consulting is viable in a way it isn't in HK. The financial *floor* here is genuinely better than S1/S2 in the near term, even if the ceiling is lower.

**S4:** Gross similar to S1 but taxed at ~50% → take-home ~600-900k HKD equivalent (~£60-90k/yr). Enough to live, save, and service obligations. Property offsets UK living cost. P(landing) is higher than S1 (bigger market, no language barrier, more roles) - perhaps 60-70%. Expected value higher than S1 despite the tax hit.

**Verdict:** S2 has the highest upside ceiling on paper but the most conditions stacked against it. S1's headline is misleading once P-weighted. S3 has the most reliable near-term financial floor. S4 is the most dependable income scenario. The financial case for S1/S2 requires landing events that Julian rates as below even-odds.

### Career

**S1 ⚠️ challenge:** Landing a well-paid HK role is a good short-term career outcome. But Julian's own words: it "dead-ends at 65," the HK market is declining for his profile, the language barrier reinforces his ceiling, and it doesn't build toward the geo-independent / own-business future he described as his ideal. S1 is the best near-term financial career move *if it lands* - but it is the worst long-term career positioning of the four.

**S2 ⚠️ challenge:** Building the cake business builds *some* entrepreneurial track record. But the AI consulting stream in HK is Julian's own assessment as weak and immature. The cake business, however successful, doesn't build the architecture/consulting/AI positioning Julian could leverage in the UK. S2's career benefit is real but narrow: it builds the "own-business" habit and the cake operation. It doesn't build the geo-independent consulting future.

**S3:** This is the strongest career scenario for Julian's stated goal. UK consulting is viable (Westerner advantage, English language, relationship-building). Adam's is an existing business with proven demand - Julian's best failure mode, and the clearest execute-mode trigger. The UK market for solution architects is recovering (IT contractor demand returned to growth May 2026 after 33-month dip). And S3 is explicitly designed to be geographically independent - working toward the "flexibility to travel whenever I want" from the ideal-life statement. **S3 is the only scenario that builds toward the life Julian described.**

**S4:** Bigger market, better odds, builds a UK network Julian currently lacks. But it's income-first and doesn't build toward the entrepreneurial goal. It is a career benefit *now*, not a career platform for later. The contrast: S3 builds the future Julian wants; S4 funds the present.

**Verdict:** S3 wins this domain clearly. S4 is second (reliable but not directional). S1/S2 are the wrong direction for Julian's stated career goal, despite the near-term financial appeal.

### Performance

This domain is largely location-neutral (ADHD travels) but structure matters:

- **S1/S4** (job roles): external accountability and structure is Julian's most reliable execute trigger. Both corporate scenarios provide this. S4 probably more so (bigger employer base in UK, less chance the one role he was counting on falls through).
- **S2/S3** (build): both are self-directed and carry the full drift risk. The key differentiator: S3's near-zero burn means a period of low output is survivable; S2's 1.1M/yr burn means drift is catastrophic within months.
- If S2 or S3 is chosen, the pivot trigger (hard revenue target by hard date) identified in [[risks|R2]] is mandatory for either to be a real performance scenario rather than drift in disguise.

**Verdict:** S1/S4 edge on performance due to external structure. Within the build scenarios, S3's lower burn gives marginally more runway before drift becomes a crisis.

### Personal

**S1/S2 ⚠️ challenge - the genuine benefit:** The **singlehood/optionality/dating access** benefit is real and survives honest testing (see [[b1-relationship-belief]]). Julian wants to live without commitment and have access to an attractive dating pool. HK provides this; the UK does not at the same density. This is the one S1/S2 personal benefit that the analysis cannot dissolve away - it is a genuine, location-specific advantage of staying.

The challenge is its size relative to its price. The bald statement: *staying in HK to preserve dating optionality costs ~£110k/yr, plus Sophia's schooling window, plus mum-time lost.* That is the real cost of this specific benefit. It is the user's decision to weigh whether the optionality is worth that price - not this analysis's job to decide. But the analysis must name the price plainly.

**S1/S2 other personal benefits:**
- Sunshine (real, mitigable from UK via sun holidays + geo-flexibility)
- Lifestyle (flat, restaurants, vibrancy) - but Julian's own B7 verdict: he under-uses it; doesn't go on boats much; idealises the lifestyle he actually lives modestly
- The HK energy/buzz - real, but partly the same thing as the optionality point

**S3/S4 personal benefits:**
- Near Sophia (formative years; can show up, not just video call)
- Near mum (time that is also not recoverable)
- Golf works in the UK
- Simplicity and low-stress - Julian named "low-stress, healthy life" as the ideal; that maps more naturally to mum's + build than to HK corporate or the burn-and-build pressure of S2
- The values Julian says he has *now* - relationships, simplicity, health - are portable to or better in the UK

**Verdict:** S1/S2 win on dating optionality (the genuine pull). S3/S4 win on every other personal metric Julian named when asked what he values now. The personal domain comes down to one question: how much of the personal score is singlehood/optionality vs everything else Julian said he values?

## What B adds to the decision

1. **S3 is the scenario that best matches Julian's own stated ideal life.** His brain-dump ideal - working for himself, geo-independent, low-stress, near family, flexibility to travel - describes S3, not S1 or S2. This is not the analysis talking: it's Julian's own words mapped onto the scenarios.

2. **S1's financial case collapses under P-weighting.** The 1.2-1.8M headline is real only in the 30-50% of worlds where he lands the role. In the majority of worlds he is burning runway in a declining market with high personal cost.

3. **S2's ceiling is highest but is stacked on conditions.** Marriage (ambivalent) + execution (unproven at this scale). Both must be true. Julian's honest read is that the marriage motivation is business + fear, not deep want - which is the weakest possible foundation for unlocking S2's engine.

4. **The one genuine S1/S2 benefit that survives every test is dating optionality.** It is real. It is location-specific. It cannot be argued away. And it has a named price: ~£110k/yr + family time + Sophia's window. Julian's call to make - but the number must be written plainly before D.

5. **S4 is the reliable fallback** - strong on every operational domain, weakest on the long-term career build Julian said he wants. A S4 entry with a deliberate S3-pivot plan (build on the side, hard trigger date to go independent) is a viable combination: S4 as floor, S3 as direction.

## What this hands to N and D

- **N (Need time / Nothing):** B doesn't surface a case for waiting. The scenarios that look best (S3/S4) are available now and get *better* sooner (Sophia's school window, MPF unlock, mum-time). The scenarios that require waiting (S1 - landing the role; S2 - marriage + build) are not improved by waiting more time in drift mode.
- **D (Committed choice):** The analysis + B + R all point the same direction. The single counter-signal is the gut's singlehood/optionality pull, which is real but named and priced. Ready for N, then D.
