# A - Alternatives (the four scenarios, evaluated head-to-head)

Job here: (1) reframe the four frozen scenarios so their real structure is visible, (2) **cost scenario 2, which was never evaluated**, (3) establish which alternatives are genuinely live and what each is gated on. Benefit/risk quantification comes next (B, R); this step defines the option *set*. Source: [[capture]] career + business sections.

## The real structure: strategy × location (a 2×2, not four islands)

The four scenarios are two questions crossed, not four independent options:

| | **HK** | **UK** |
|---|---|---|
| **Land a job** (corporate/contract) | **S1** - HK corporate | **S4** - UK corporate |
| **Build** (entrepreneurial) | **S2** - HK build | **S3** - UK build |

- **Location axis (HK vs UK)** = the headline decision.
- **Strategy axis (land a job vs build)** = orthogonal, but interacts with location through **tax** (a landed job favours low-tax HK) and **burn** (a build needs the low-overhead a UK base provides).

## Scenario 2 costed (the one we skipped) - corrected 2026-07-03

**S2 = "the runway play": stay in HK, own flat, build multiple revenue streams instead of landing a job.** Burn ~1.1M HKD/yr ([[uk-move-financial-model|financial model]]). Julian named **four** candidate streams - but they differ sharply on strength *and* on whether they actually need HK:

| Stream | Strength | HK-locked? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Joanne's cake business** (partners: he markets via AI, she bakes; convert proven Instagram demand) | **Strongest - demand already proven with zero marketing.** Julian's confidence: would convert to **1M+ HKD paid business.** **But marriage-enabled** (below). | **Yes** - rich-parent HK market, doesn't translate to UK |
| **2. Adam's property business** (extend via AI marketing) | Real - existing active business | **No** - Julian's own view: viable from HK *or* better from UK (removes burn) |
| **3. AI consulting** | **Weak** - immature market, demand unproven; two people Julian knows have tried for years and found it very tough | No - and UK has the Westerner-advantage variant instead |
| **4. Remote AI model training** | **Weak** - low-paid, demand unknown; some manage it | **No** - explicitly location-independent |

**The settled finding (2026-07-03, after Julian's clarification):** of the four streams, **only the cake business is genuinely HK-locked** - the other three are portable (Adam's, model training) or weak-everywhere (AI consulting). And the cake business, *as Julian would actually run it* (Joanne baking legally, as partners), **is marriage-enabled**: her domestic-helper visa bars her from working a separate business, so a legal, scalable venture needs the marriage. **This is exactly why Julian has not marketed it to date.** So:

> **S2's distinctive, must-be-in-HK value = the cake business, and that engine is unlocked by marriage.** Everything else on the list you could build from the UK just as well (and cheaper). So S2's real case = *marry Joanne + convert the cake demand to 1M+*. Its upside and its biggest risk therefore come from the **same lever**: marriage (see [[risks|R: marriage exposure]] / [[analysis|B5]]).

- **Two things decide S2** (→ R):
  1. **Marriage exposure** - the upside (cake 1M+) and the downside (divorce loss, supporting Joanne's two sons, long-run inheritance dilution away from Sophia) are inseparable. Worked in [[risks]].
  2. **Execution** - proven *demand* is not proven *delivery by Julian*; the [[capture|"semi-retired in HK produced no income"]] pattern. Shared with S3.
- **The other three streams tilt the build strategy toward the UK** anyway: three of four don't need HK, and running them from HK carries the full ~1.1M/yr burn vs near-zero at mum's.

## Four findings A surfaces (that the binary hid)

1. **S2's HK-specific case is one stream, and that stream is marriage-enabled.** Of the four streams only the cake business needs HK; and running it as a legal, scalable venture needs the marriage. So S2's real case = *marry Joanne + convert the cake demand*. **Its upside and its biggest risk share one lever** - marriage. The other three streams are portable/weak and build cheaper from the UK.
2. **Three of the four "HK" streams actually tilt toward the UK** - they don't need HK, and running them from HK carries the full burn vs near-zero at mum's. The build strategy's cheap runway is the UK.
3. **Both build scenarios (S2, S3) share one unresolved execution risk** - proven *demand* (the cake business) is not proven *delivery by Julian*. The "semi-retired in HK produced no income" pattern ([[capture]]) hangs over both. This is the decisive R-item; neither build path can be trusted until it's tested.
4. **Marriage gates Joanne's UK presence, not just the HK cake business.** Julian confirms (2026-07-03): Joanne is Filipino and needs a spousal visa to relocate to the UK. So marriage is the switch for two things simultaneously: (a) the cake business operating legally in HK (S2), and (b) Joanne appearing in any UK scenario (S3/S4). Don't marry → Joanne stays in HK; relationship ends or stays HK-only. Marry + move UK → Joanne joins Julian, but the HK cake market stays behind (doesn't translate - A12). The marriage decision is **more central than the earlier analysis showed** - it determines not just S2's viability but whether Joanne features in any scenario at all.

**Net:** all four scenarios are live. The HK-build case rests mainly on the cake business (the one HK-locked, demand-proven engine) plus whatever the corporate option (S1) adds. The marriage question is *not* a gate on S2's opportunity, but it re-enters as the clean **legal structure** for Joanne's role and still carries its own relationship exposure.

- **R (Risks):** the decisive open items - **the execution risk** on S2/S3 (build or drift), the **marriage exposure** on S2, and finishing the untested beliefs (B4, B5, B6, B8, B9).
- **The marriage sub-decision** now has to be at least provisionally resolved, because it toggles whether S2 exists.
