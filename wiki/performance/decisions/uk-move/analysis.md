# UK Move - Stage 1 Analysis (Structure & Label)

Structured output of the [[capture|brain dump]]. Consolidates the raw material into scenarios and a labelled Beliefs & Assumptions Register, ready for the Stage 3 asymmetric challenge. Feeds the [[decision-journal|Decision Journal]] entry. Framework: [[why-brained|BRAINED]].

## The headline finding

**The gut and the emerging analysis have separated - and they point in opposite directions.**

- **Gut (captured first, clean):** stay in HK. Driven by the flat/sunshine/lifestyle, the dating/attraction belief, and the felt "one-way trip" finality of leaving.
- **Emerging analysis (across all six domains):** trends toward the UK. Not decisively, but consistently, on multiple independent fronts:

| Domain | Where the analysis pulled | Strength |
|--------|---------------------------|----------|
| Wellbeing | HK better *under normal circumstances* - but these are not normal circumstances (no income, no fallback) | Neutralised |
| Relationships | Girlfriend will come (move ≠ end); HK friends "not precious"; dad not an anchor; mum a genuine plus | Softly UK |
| Finance | HK wins **only if** well-paid work lands. UK = robust damage limitation (near-zero burn, MPF unlocks) | UK unless work lands |
| Career | HK = "graft or luck," dead-ends at 65. UK builds the consulting/own-business future he actually wants | UK (structural) |
| Performance | Location-neutral | Neutral |
| Personal | Loves HK lifestyle - but admits he under-uses it; what he values now (simplicity, low stress, relationships) is portable | Softly UK |

**This is exactly the split BRAINED is built to resolve.** The rule: analysis wins unless the gut justifies its override *in writing* and the belief survives testing. So Stage 3 must challenge the gut's load-bearing beliefs hard - and equally challenge whether the analysis is over-rating the UK.

**Cross-cutting factors (analysed within scenarios, each partly its own decision):**
- **Marriage to girlfriend** - its own decision point. Interacts with scenarios 2 and 3. Her self-sufficiency is a key input: does he need to marry to enable/extend the business, or is she viable independently? To be analysed separately.
- **Does the girlfriend relocate?** - factor within scenario 3.
- **Geographic independence of the business** - a design choice within scenarios 2/3; if independent, unlocks later relocation flexibility (return to HK, or work from anywhere).

**Structural note:** scenarios 2 and 3 are close cousins - both "build AI-enabled businesses around Adam's + girlfriend's as anchor tenants." They differ on: (a) location/tax/lifestyle, (b) the UK-only consulting option, (c) UK's lower overhead at mum's, (d) HK's low tax if a stream earns well. **The real question between 2 and 3 is location, not business model.**

**Failure mode (not a scenario): Stay HK and drift** - semi-retire by accident, burn ~1M HKD/yr, decide nothing. The [[commitment-avoidance]] outcome.

## Fact-check results (2026-07-02, first pass)

Empirical resolution of the checkable assumptions. **Every checkable con-of-moving softened or resolved; two now actively favour moving.** The gut's "moving is risky" case leans heavily on fears that don't survive fact-checking.

| # | Was | Finding | Verdict |
|---|-----|---------|---------|
| A7 | Sophia = ~£60k overseas uni fees | UK home-fee status needs **3 years "ordinarily resident" + settled** before the course starts. If they move soon, Sophia clears the 3-year window and pays **home fees (~£9,535/yr capped), not overseas.** If they stay in HK and move just before uni, she pays the overseas rate. | **FLIPS: the £60k is a cost of *delaying*, not of moving.** Moving sooner avoids it. |
| A9 | Interferon costly / not NHS-covered (not first-line) | **Interferon IS an NHS-funded option for essential thrombocythaemia (all ages)** - peginterferon alfa-2a (Pegasys) and Besremi (ropeginterferon) are commissioned by NHS England for MPNs incl. ET. As a hospital-prescribed specialist drug it is effectively free on the NHS - well below the ~£75/injection every 2 months Julian pays in HK. | **RESOLVED, and favours moving.** Treatment continues on the NHS, likely cheaper than now. The "not first-line so I'd pay a lot" fear is unfounded. |
| A5 | Prenup may not protect | Not automatically binding in England, BUT courts give **significant weight** since *Radmacher v Granatino* (2010) if done properly: independent legal advice both sides, full disclosure, no duress, signed 28+ days pre-wedding, fair terms. A **June 2026 govt consultation** may make them binding by statute. | **Real protection exists.** Not absolute, but materially defuses the "trapped" fear (B5). Needs a family solicitor. |
| A8 | Sophia forced into remedial French | Languages **not compulsory at GCSE**; no national rule mandates French; **Mandarin GCSE exists** (she could leverage her strength). But 2/3 of state schools offer only one GCSE language. | **Addressable, not automatic.** No rule forces French - but how a *specific* school handles her sitting out French / benchmarking her vs the class is unknown. Confirm with the actual schools near mum. Not a blocker, not yet solved. |
| A10 / A11 | UK market weak, age bias, "lots of unemployment" | IT contractor demand **returned to growth May 2026 after 33 months down**; age bias real but unlawful, with a 2026 shift toward hiring over-50s. **Julian's correction:** he is a **multi-domain enterprise solution architect** with *emerging* AI-architecture skill, NOT an AI/ML specialist. The AI/ML shortage does not map directly to him; the relevant market is enterprise/solution-architect contracting, with AI as a differentiator in his story. | **Better than the anecdote, but don't overweight the AI signal.** Market direction is up; his real position is an experienced architect who can credibly claim AI capability.
