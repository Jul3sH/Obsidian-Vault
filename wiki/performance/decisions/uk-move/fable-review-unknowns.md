## Financial execution risks

### MPF withdrawal mechanics [HIGH - time-critical]
- Two separate claims (Principal Asia Smart + Sun Life Rainbow), each requiring its own statutory declaration before a Commissioner for Oaths (free at HK Home Affairs) or Notary/JP. **Must be completed before flying** - from the UK, a statutory declaration requires a notary, postal delay, and cost.
- Trustees must pay within 30 days of a complete application; realistic total is 2-6 weeks per trustee. Not a multi-month cliff, but also not a day-before task. Start in July.
- **Critical: check eMPF platform migration status for both trustees this week.** Trustees migrating onto the eMPF platform in 2025-26 have transaction blackout windows during onboarding. If Principal or Sun Life migrates during Julian's window, the claim freezes.
- **The permanent-departure declaration is a criminal offence if false.** Once signed, a future HK job offer is legally foreclosed as a casual option. This is a commitment-lock feature but Julian should know he is signing it.
- If funds land after UK arrival: keep the HK bank account open until both MPF amounts and the HK$124,748 rebate have cleared. The UK tax treatment of a post-arrival receipt is exactly the adviser question.
- **The tax adviser is still not booked as of 7 Jul.** This is the single most overdue action in the project.

### Retirement model [MEDIUM-HIGH]
- **State pension age may be 68, not 67.** Current legislation phases the rise 2044-46 for those born after 5 April 1977. Check DOB against gov.uk timetable.
- The ISA pot (~£327k) is being asked to do two contradictory jobs: backstop of last resort during the London search AND entire retirement fund. If the tail risk fires (long search + MPF burned on living costs), the "never touch ISAs" floor and "retire on ISAs" plan collide. Rough floor: ~£25k/yr (4% draw + full state pension + paid-off Cecil Road). Workable, but thin.
- The ~£45.7k MPF remainder after mortgage payoff takes three tax years to shelter (£20k/yr ISA allowance). Minor, but unmodelled.
- **NI record unchecked.** "1 year to qualify" should be verified via gov.uk, and voluntary Class 2/3 top-ups for missed years abroad are the cheapest retirement money available. No plan for pension contributions once UK-employed.

### DB flat break-even math [MEDIUM]
- The correct frame is not "get back to HK$8M" (sunk cost) but: *is this leveraged single-asset bet the best use of the cash-cost vs alternatives?* At ~98% leverage, modest recovery multiplies equity fast, so holding is not irrational.
- Unpriced risks: **rental void** (model 1-3 months, HK$22-66k); **consent-to-let from HSBC** (owner-occupier mortgage - has the bank agreed letting? unverified); rate basis (is 2.47% fixed or HIBOR-linked?); remote management friction; GBP/HKD FX.
- **The FIG 4-year CGT-free window gives the recovery bet a natural clock.** A sale that completes inside 4 years of UK residency is UK-CGT-free. An open-ended hold past year 4 silently converts a tax-free exit into a taxed one.
- **Action: set a defined review trigger with numbers now** (e.g. "sell by month 36 unless value ≥ HK$7M"), so a sale can complete inside the FIG window. An open-ended "await recovery" is the anchoring pattern identified in earlier reviews.

### Fidelity ISA composition [HIGH - lowest cost to fix]
- Still unreviewed since 22 June. 81% of the retirement fund. Composition unknown.
- Two problems, not one: (1) possible natural yield of ~£800-1k/mo tax-free going unclaimed; (2) unknown risk exposure - if concentrated or equity-heavy, a drawdown in the search window hits the fund that cannot be touched.
- 30 minutes to download a statement. Outranked by everything else for three weeks. Do it this week.

### FIG 10-year condition [MEDIUM]
- The confirmation test is **year-by-year against the Statutory Residence Test**, not a memory of when Julian left HK. He owned UK property throughout, had family ties, and visited. One heavy-UK-days year combined with accommodation/family ties could break the chain. Adviser item.

## Execution and timing risks

### School admissions address proof [HIGH - Julian's assumption is wrong]
- Merton's own guidance: **"ownership of an address does not prove residency."** Accepted evidence = council tax bill or new tenancy agreement. An Airbnb or hotel produces neither.
- Julian's belief that Cecil Road ownership satisfies the requirement is not correct. His exact scenario (owns in-borough, tenanted to ~Nov, needs a Sept Year 8 place) sits in a gap only the admissions team can resolve via a direct call.
- **Fix:** book the Sept-Nov temp accommodation as a proper **short-let inside Merton with a tenancy agreement**, not an Airbnb. This provides valid address evidence. Location relative to Ricards/Ursuline/Harris matters for distance-ranked waiting lists.
- The realistic bad case is not "no school" (the LA must offer a place) but an undersubscribed school not on the shortlist while Sophia sits on waiting lists.

### Two months in temp accommodation with a 12-year-old [MEDIUM-HIGH]
- Sophia starts the hardest school transition of her life from a suitcase: no bedroom, no anchor, possessions on a ship.
- **Air-freight a "Sophia's room" box** (bedding, books, physical anchors) so her space exists on night one in the short-let. The bedtime ritual is the one portable anchor and must survive the temp period untouched.

### Removals [MEDIUM - book now]
- HK-UK sea freight: 8-11 weeks door-to-door; July-August is peak season. Book within 1-2 weeks.
- Hidden convenience: a late-August pack-out arrives ~November, exactly when Cecil Road becomes available. The plan writes itself if booked now; it becomes storage fees and chaos if left until August.

### Sole-parent single point of failure [HIGH - completely absent from the model]
- Nothing anywhere covers what happens to Sophia if Julian is hospitalised or dies during or after the transition.
- **If Julian dies, Sophia's surviving parent - an alcoholic in early recovery - is her presumptive carer.** No UK will, testamentary guardian, letter of wishes, or life cover is mentioned in any document.
- No named London emergency adult for the ordinary case (Julian gets flu, Sophia is in week 2 at a new school).
- **Action: ask Jamie explicitly** (Edie's dad, Julian's best friend). Cheap, unglamorous, highest-consequence gap in the whole file.

### HK exit admin [LOW-MEDIUM]
- If a domestic helper is currently employed: termination = notice + long-service payment + repatriation flight.
- Keep HK accounts open until MPF and tax rebate land.
- Sophia's HK goodbyes need **protected time** in the departure plan, not remainder time. The eight weeks between now and ~3 Sept contain roughly twelve workstreams.

## Sophia Q&A

### Q1: Is Julian under-weighting Joanne in the move conversation? [YES]
Julian's own data proves it. Sophia has cried twice in the move-era: both times were Joanne-proximal. "Wimbledon" got a wavering "okay"; Joanne's traces at the house got tears. She can metabolise the move because the move is *with Dad*. Joanne threatens the "with Dad" itself.

**Rider:** if she raises Joanne directly tonight, it must be answered honestly on the spot. Deferring a question she asks out loud reads as confirmation the threat is real.

### Q2: Unknown unknowns in Sophia's profile

1. **Year 8 is the worst entry point for her exact social pattern.** UK friendship groups seal in Year 7; she arrives to fully-formed groups, which is precisely her "tries to lead, gets rejected, follows" trigger. Mitigation: tribe-first entry via sports team and drama club in the first two weeks, never a general assault on the social hierarchy.
2. **Edie is a single point of failure - and probably at a different school.** Edie has her own established group. If expectations are loaded onto her, Sophia risks replaying "the alternative choice" - her core wound - with the person the move was partly sold on. Frame Edie as a bridge, not the destination.
3. **Group-chat exile.** Her HK social world will continue visibly on her phone without her - a simultaneous exclusion engine and rose-tinting machine that did not exist in previous generations' relocations. Watch, do not confiscate.
4. **Adolescence collides with the move.** The bedtime channel may naturally start closing over the next 18 months. Risk: Julian misattributes normal teenage withdrawal to move trauma (smothering) or vice versa (missing it). Baseline rule: gradual closing = development; abrupt closing = signal.
5. **Julian's month-9-10 anxiety transmits directly.** She is hyper-attuned and suppresses her own distress to protect him. He needs an adult support channel in London that is not her and not only Joanne-on-video. He currently has neither.
6. **Counselling continuity.** She has a counselling history (Clodagh household). CAMHS waiting lists run a year-plus. Request the DBIS pastoral handover for the new school, and pre-identify a private adolescent counsellor near SW19 *before* she needs one.
7. **First London winter lands in months 2-3, in temp accommodation.** Dark at 4pm from November; she has never done a northern winter. Plan outdoor sport and structure deliberately.
8. **Body image meets UK state-school PE.** Communal changing rooms, physical appraisal by strangers, and direct banter culture. Counter-asset: get her onto a sports team in the first two weeks - her build reads as athlete, not outsider, in that context.
9. **The acting reframe is powerful and therefore dangerous.** If deployed as a sell and not delivered (drama clubs full, logistics chaos in the temp period), it becomes a broken promise attached to her load-bearing dream. Do not deploy until paired with a concrete, booked first step.

### Q3: Clodagh proximity - right to defer? [DEFER THE SELL; DON'T DEFER THE FACT]
Clodagh will mention the London property on her own timeline, in her own state - she has already shown she will (drunk 5 July disclosure). If Sophia learns Julian knew and said nothing, it spends the relationship currency everything else runs on.

Nothing happens with that unless you want it - and never, ever while she's drinking."* The last clause converts a threat into a boundary promise that speaks to Sophia's actual fear (drunk Clodagh destabilising the household).

### Q4: Rose-tinted retrospective - the intervention

Three-part, and timing is the point:

1. **Pre-name the pattern now, before departure.** She has already shown it with the previous school. Name it gently as a thing minds do, linked to her existing catastrophising-awareness: *"You know how the old school got shinier after you left it? HK will do that too. It's normal, and it means the missing is real - not that the place was perfect."* Pre-named, she and Julian can spot it together. Discovered mid-spiral, naming it looks like dismissal.

3. **Never rebut mid-spiral.** When "HK was perfect" arrives (expect months 2-6): validate the grief first, invoke the pre-named pattern second, revisit her own record third, and only if receptive.
