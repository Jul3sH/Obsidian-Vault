# N - Need time / Nothing

> The **N step** of [[why-brained|BRAINED]]. Three questions: (1) Is there a rational case for waiting? (2) What signal would change the analysis, and is it arriving? (3) What does "do nothing" actually cost? Feeds [[reconciliation|D]].

## The deadline

Julian's self-imposed commit date: **9 July 2026**, before the UK family trip departing 12 July. The framing: arrive in the UK WITH a decision, not arrive to make one under emotional conditions on the ground.

---

## Question 1: is there a rational case for waiting past 9 July?

A rational case for waiting requires: *there is a specific piece of information arriving within a definable window that has material probability of changing the conclusion.*

"I want more time to feel ready" is not that. It is the [[commitment-avoidance]] pattern dressed as prudence - and a pattern this analysis has already named.

**The only live signal that could change the analysis: TTI / Ty.**

The Stephan endorsement message has been sent (Stephan en route to Miami; possible Ty meeting). If that meeting happens and Ty makes a concrete offer, P(HK work via TTI) rises substantially - potentially pushing S1 above the ~45-50% breakeven into genuinely viable territory.

**Does this justify waiting past 9 July?** Work through it:

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Is a Ty offer imminent (days, not weeks)? | Uncertain. Meeting possible during Stephan's Miami trip - window is unclear. No offer is on the table. |
| If Ty offers a role, what changes? | S1's P rises; S1 financial case becomes real, not probability-discounted. HK corporate becomes viable. |
| Does a Ty offer change the UK scenarios (S3/S4)? | No. The relationships, personal, and career-direction arguments for S3/S4 are independent of TTI. |
| Does a Ty offer change the singlehood/optionality cost of moving? | No. That's a location factor, not a job factor. |
| Is waiting an effective way to receive this signal? | No. Waiting in HK at ~£2,100/week does not accelerate the TTI process. The UK trip happens regardless; Julian can receive a Ty offer from either country. |

**Verdict on waiting for TTI:** The signal is worth tracking but not worth waiting for. If Ty offers a role in the next 30 days, Julian can evaluate it regardless of where he is - the offer arrives by phone/email, not by staying in HK. A committed decision to move does not foreclose a conversation with Ty; it forecloses the *drift* that waiting produces.

**The correct response to TTI:** build it into D as a contingency trigger. *"If Ty makes a concrete offer within 60 days of this decision, I will evaluate it as a standalone opportunity - it does not reopen the location decision."*

## Question 3: what does "do nothing" cost?

"Do nothing" = stay in HK, decide nothing, continue the current state. It is not a neutral option. It has a price.

| Cost category | Size | Accumulation |
|---|---|---|
| **Financial burn** | ~£2,100/week (~£110k/yr) | Running now; each week of "wait" consumes runway |
| **Sophia's schooling window** | Mid-schooling moves have higher social cost; the window to move her at a natural break is finite | Narrows each term |
| **Mum-time** | Not recoverable | Lost each week |
| **MPF pension unlock** | ~1M HKD (~£100k) locked until Julian leaves HK | Locked each month he stays |
| **Joanne** | She has asked where things stand; she wants marriage; "waiting to decide" uses her time | Relational cost, non-zero |
| **Drift gravity** | The longer the no-decision state continues, the harder the move becomes (inertia, sunk-cost framing, life re-entangling with HK) | Compounds |

**The weekly cost of "do nothing": ~£2,100 + drift compounding.** There is no free option. The question is never "should I decide now or wait for free" - it is "what does waiting cost, and is the information I'm waiting for worth that price?"

On the analysis, no incoming signal is worth ~£2,100/week plus drift compounding.

## The drift scenario named plainly

"Do nothing" does not sit still. It has a terminal state: **Julian stays in HK indefinitely, burns through runway, drifts into semi-retirement, and the decision gets made by exhaustion** (when the money runs out) rather than by choice. This is the [[alternatives|"failure mode" scenario]] in the alternatives analysis - not a strategy, a consequence. It is the [[commitment-avoidance]] outcome. Every analysis of this decision produces the same finding: drift is not a path; it is what happens when no path is chosen.

---

## N verdict

**There is no rational case for waiting past 9 July.**

- The one live signal (TTI) is trackable from either country and does not require waiting in HK.
- All other signals are either already incorporated or not material enough to change the analysis.
- "Do nothing" is not free: it costs ~£2,100/week plus drift compounding, plus Sophia's window, plus Joanne's time.
- The UK trip (departing 12 July) is an excellent first field test of the decision, not a replacement for it.

**The correct response to the N question:**
> The decision is ready. The analysis is complete. The only thing waiting produces is a higher price tag on the same conclusion. Decide by 9 July.
