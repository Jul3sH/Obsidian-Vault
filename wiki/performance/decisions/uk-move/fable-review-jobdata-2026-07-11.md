# Fable adversarial review - the job data and the London-direct lean (11 Jul 2026)

> Hostile re-derivation of where London-direct vs Malvern-first stands now that the 11 Jul Adzuna pull is in. My 10 Jul review found the Malvern-first reopen procedurally tainted and one-sidedly argued, and nominated search-duration-by-location as the deciding input. The lean has now swung back to London on that data. Brief: hunt amplification in BOTH directions, with special suspicion of the NEW (London) direction, because amplifying the current lean is this system's documented failure mode ([[commitment-lock-protocol]] bias #4).

## 1. Verdict

**The London-direct lean is now substantially earned, but it is over-claimed.** The data legitimately kills both of Malvern's independent job rationales: the remote-equaliser hypothesis (a well-paid permanent remote role) and the local-pool hypothesis (a viable Malvern-commutable market). Those two questions were honestly asked before the answer was known, and the answers are robust in direction even after every caveat is applied. But the headline "THE HINGE ANSWERED" swaps the hinge: my 10 Jul hinge was search DURATION by location, and duration was never measured, even though the report's own source profile says Adzuna's Intelligence API exposes posting-duration and time-to-fill. What the data actually resolved is where the destination job will be (London hybrid, with high probability), which strengthens London-direct indirectly but leaves the runway question, the Sophia axis, and the TTI gate exactly where they were. **Current admissible position: the contested Malvern-first reopen has lost its best evidence and dies; the 7 Jul London-direct verdict resumes as the standing lean, still short of a LOCK because the 10 Jul conditions (pot bridge, symmetric burn, retyping, TTI seal) remain unmet.**

## 2. Does the evidence earn the London-direct lean?

**What is earned:**

- **The remote-mirage finding is directionally solid.** Remote-explicit postings are ~1.5% of the national pool (82 of 5,568 across families). Even granting the report's own 3-5x keyword undercount, remote-first stays a small minority, and the composition finding (well-paid remote = annualised contract day-rates in product-config stacks, or clearance-gated defence) rests on hard fields (salary_min/max, contract_type), not just inferred work-pattern. The specific plan the finding kills - "run the search from a cheap base and land a well-paid permanent remote role with benefits" - required remote-perm-senior-well-paid to be a real population. On this evidence it is not.
- **The pool ratio is fair, and its construction is generous to Malvern.** Malvern-base at ~15% of London-base (412 vs 2,662) includes Birmingham AND Bristol in the Malvern commute (both borderline at ~1h15-1h20 each way) and counts Remote in both bases, which narrows the ratio. A hostile construction would have made Malvern look worse. Credit where due: this is the one methodology choice in the whole 11 Jul set that leans against the new direction.
- **Pool size is a legitimate proxy for search duration, with a stated mechanism.** For a niche candidate the objection "he only needs ONE role" cuts less than it sounds: what he needs is the arrival RATE of roles clearing his bar, and if per-role match probability is roughly constant, a ~7x stock ratio implies a roughly ~7x arrival-rate ratio and a materially shorter expected search. The proxy inference is defensible.
- **The data feeds the non-job Sophia axis in London's favour, legitimately.** If the destination job is almost certainly London hybrid/on-site, then Malvern-first guarantees the second move (and its timing is job-driven, not school-calendar-driven). That raises the probability and worsens the timing of the double disruption my 10 Jul review said was un-rebutted. This is the strongest genuinely NEW pro-London inference the pull produces, and neither the report nor the status surface states it explicitly.

**What is over-claimed:**

- **The hinge was quietly swapped.** My 10 Jul framing: "if Malvern lengthens the search by even ~4 months, forfeited income exceeds a full year of burn savings, so the job-board remote-vs-hybrid data decides it." That is a claim about DURATION. The pull measured pool SIZE and composition. The two are linked by the arrival-rate argument above, but a proxy is a proxy, and the direct metric was available: [[adzuna-uk-job-market-data-source]] itself lists "posting duration, time-to-fill... by region/occupation" among the Intelligence API aggregations. The status surface should not say "THE HINGE ANSWERED" when the hinge's named quantity was never pulled and is pullable.
- **A subtlety the duration proxy ignores: applications are location-independent during the search phase.** A candidate in Malvern can apply to every London hybrid role on the board; the pool ratio constrains where he can ACCEPT, not where he can APPLY. What living in Malvern actually costs the search is presence (networking, the 7 Jul finding that presence shortens searches - reasoned, never evidenced) and final-round logistics (manageable at 2h45). So the pool data proves the destination is London; it does not prove that searching FROM Malvern for London jobs is 7x slower, or 4 months slower, or measurably slower at all. That specific gap is what the time-to-fill pull and/or the month-9 review should close.

> **CORRECTION (added 11 Jul, see §9): the clause "the pool ratio constrains where he can ACCEPT" is WRONG for hybrid work.** A hybrid London role (2-3 anchor days) is acceptable from a Malvern base via a Tue-Thu crash-pad, so the reachable-to-ACCEPT pool is not the ~15% Malvern-resident commute pool - it is Malvern's local pool PLUS the entire London HYBRID and REMOTE pool. Only London ON-SITE-only roles (~1/3 of the London pool per the new split) are genuinely lost to a Malvern base. The ~15% ratio therefore measures the wrong thing for the interim crash-pad strategy. I am striking the error on the record, not deleting it; full correction and its decision consequences in §9.
- **"Mirage" is graded harder than its own data grade.** The report's frontmatter rates work-pattern/remote share "low-to-medium" confidence; no remote flag exists; LinkedIn - which the source doc concedes is "the single biggest under-count" and plausibly where senior remote/hybrid roles live - is wholly absent; and the load-bearing "remote perm median £70k" is printed with no sample size (only on-site gets an n, and it is 7). The finding survives these caveats directionally, but a verdict word like "mirage," repeated in bold across four documents, carries more certainty than a keyword floor on a LinkedIn-less sample can honestly fund.
- **The whole-decision phrasing overreaches the jobs finding.** "Decision lean: the data points London-direct" is a jobs-pool conclusion promoted into a decision headline. The report does hedge lower down (the N-gate and Sophia hinge "still stand"), and the project file repeats the hedge, but the Bottom line section - the only part most readers will read - does not carry it. The runway asymmetry (London ~10-12mo liquid vs Malvern ~2.5yr), the pot derivation, Sophia, and TTI are all outside the data's reach and unmoved by it.

**Net: earned in direction, overstated in strength and scope.** London-direct is the right lean on this evidence. It is not yet a closed sub-decision, and the data does not make it one.

**Reconciled position:** the 10 Jul review said neither option survives as argued and the job data would decide between them. The job data has now decided the JOB question for London, and the burden of proof sits where it did on 7 Jul: London-direct stands; Malvern-first is dead as argued; the staged variant survives only as the structural Sept-Nov bridge or as the explicitly re-scoped cleared-perm re-targeting (§5), which nobody has actually proposed.

## 9. Addendum (11 Jul): the crash-pad interim strategy - and a correction to my own section 2

### 9.1 My section 2 error, corrected

Section 2 said: "the pool ratio constrains where he can ACCEPT, not where he can APPLY." **That is wrong for hybrid work, and hybrid is the majority pattern.** A hybrid London role with 2-3 anchor days is fully acceptable from a Malvern base by crash-padding in London Tue-Thu. So the crash-pad interim's reachable-to-ACCEPT pool is NOT the ~15% Malvern-resident commute pool the whole 11 Jul report is built around. It is: Malvern local + **all** London hybrid + **all** remote. The only London roles genuinely lost are on-site-only ones. If the new split is right (~40% London hybrid, ~33% on-site-only, ~20% remote), the crash-pad base reaches roughly two-thirds of the London pool, not 15% of it. For Solution Architect specifically the claim is ~513 London hybrid roles reachable vs the ~114 Malvern-cluster roles the report counted - a 4-5x expansion of the reachable set. **This dissolves the single strongest pro-London finding in my sections 1-3 (the pool-size / arrival-rate argument).** That argument only bites against a Malvern RESIDENT taking LOCAL jobs. It does not bite against a Malvern-based crash-padder taking London hybrid jobs. I over-weighted the pool ratio because I was adjudicating the wrong strategy.

What survives from section 2, unchanged: the remote-PERM-mirage finding (the crash-pad does not rely on remote-perm, so this is now moot rather than damaging), and the destination-is-London conclusion (the crash-pad AGREES the job is London hybrid - it just reaches it from a cheaper base during the search).

### 9.2 The strategy adjudicated on its merits

The logic is genuinely strong, and it is aimed at the correct target - the #1 risk this whole project exists to manage (large capital moved to the UK, then burning at £4,550/mo with no job). Its four moves each do real work:

1. **Search-phase burn stays at Malvern's ~£1,683/mo, not London's ~£4,550/mo.** This is the crux and it is sound. The crash-pad cost (~£1,083/mo) is a cost-of-EARNING incurred only once a job exists, paid from salary, not runway. On the numbers: ~18 months of search per £31k of runway vs ~7. Against the 10 Jul finding that London's liquid-only window is ~10-12 months (a genuine collision with the documented month-9 psychological ambush), a base that roughly triples search runway is not a minor optimisation - it directly defuses the one risk I rated most real in both prior reviews.
2. **The childcare crux is solved by Mum, and that is what unlocks the whole thing.** A sole parent cannot crash-pad midweek without overnight cover; Mum in Malvern provides it. Treat the crux as solved as specified. (Weigh the dependency in 9.3.)
3. **The London hybrid pool is large and reachable** (9.1). The strategy does not need the remote-perm unicorn the report spent its length killing; it targets exactly the hybrid roles the report itself says hold the perm money.
4. **Sophia's move is timed to a school-year boundary**, one clean Malvern-to-London move, cushioned by grandma in the interim.

On the duration question I flagged as the un-pulled hinge: the free proxy (age of live postings) reportedly shows London NOT filling faster than the West Midlands (~15-21 days median both). If that holds, it does not help London-direct - it weakly helps the interim, because it means searching is not obviously slower from a Malvern base, and applications are location-independent anyway. I could not verify this pull; graded asserted-directional. But note it cuts against my section 2 duration proxy too: I said pool size implies faster arrival; equal posting-age is mild evidence the arrival rate of reachable roles is not 7x worse. Both my proxy and this counter-proxy are weak; the honest read is duration-by-location is inconclusive, which removes a pro-London leg rather than adding a pro-Malvern one.

### 9.3 The honest residuals (hostile, both directions)

The strategy is strong but not free. Weighed against the de-risk:

- **It still adds one extra Sophia transition** (HK to Malvern to London = two moves vs London-direct's one), even timed to a year boundary. This is the residual I have consistently rated as London-direct's genuine advantage, and it survives. But its WEIGHT drops sharply here versus the 10 Jul Malvern-first: there, the second move was job-driven, unscheduled, and possibly mid-GCSE; here it is pre-scheduled to a year boundary and the FIRST move (to Malvern, near Grandma, a known place) is gentler than a cold-start into a London rental. It is one extra transition, but the least-bad version of one.
- **Julian is away Tue-Thu during the interim.** A teenager with a documented RSD pattern (per her stakeholder profile) spends three nights a week without her father, cared for by an elderly grandmother, in her first UK year. Grandma covers logistics; she does not replace a parent midweek for a child who processes rejection hard. This is the real human cost and it is under-priced in the finance doc's one-line treatment. It is also the same "Sophia parented midweek by Grandma" breach of the "as long as we're together" promise that the 7 Jul review flagged against the earlier crash-pad idea - it has not gone away, it is just now time-boxed to the interim rather than permanent.
- **Mum-dependency is a single point of failure.** The entire structure (childcare, the £0 board, the low burn) rests on one elderly widow's health and availability for the interim's duration. If her health turns, the interim collapses and forces an emergency London move under exactly the runway pressure the plan was built to avoid. Bias register H5 (silent stakeholders): Mum "generates no contact events," so her load and her fragility are the easiest thing in this plan to under-weight. Price it: what is the fallback if Mum is unavailable at month 4?
- **Duration-drift is the live trap.** "Stable job + end of education year" is two elastic conditions and no dated move-by. This is precisely the buffer-that-becomes-permanent risk the 10 Jul review built its school-calendar trigger to stop. Without a written, dated, tenancy-engineered move-by (Cecil let ending Jun-Jul so the London date is structural, not willpower), the interim's honest expected duration is "longer than planned," and every extra term compounds the Sophia-midweek cost and the Mum-dependency exposure. The interim is only admissible WITH the same hard trigger my 10 Jul review demanded.
- **Hybrid-anchor-day rigidity.** Most hybrid roles tolerate a 2h30 base at 2-3 flexible anchor days; some mandate FIXED days (e.g. "in office Mon and Thu") or ad-hoc client-site presence that a 3-night crash-pad block cannot flex to. This is a real but manageable filter, not a killer - it narrows the reachable hybrid pool below the headline ~40%, so do not bank the full figure.

### 9.4 Reconciliation with my section 6 admissible framing

Section 6 called the admissible form "London, dated Sept 2027, staged via Malvern," but I described that stage as, at best, a structural Sept-Nov bridge and otherwise "dead time" - because I could not see how a Malvern stage was PRODUCTIVE. The crash-pad is the missing mechanism. It converts the Malvern stage from dead runway-preservation into an EARNING period: Julian is in a London hybrid job, drawing salary, while the family holds the cheap base and Sophia stays settled until the year boundary. That is a materially better stage than the one I could imagine in section 6. **My section 6 framing is not overturned; it is completed.** "London, dated Sept 2027, staged via Malvern" was the right shape; the crash-pad is what makes the staging worth doing rather than merely tolerable. The non-negotiable from section 6 also survives intact: the stage is admissible ONLY with a written, dated, tenancy-engineered London move-by. A crash-pad interim without that trigger is the 10 Jul Malvern-first trap wearing a better suit.

### 9.5 Where this leaves the two options

I over-stated London-direct's lead in sections 1-8 because I adjudicated Malvern as a resident end-state, which the pool ratio genuinely sinks. Against the ACTUAL proposal - a crash-pad interim reaching the London hybrid pool from a cheap base, then a scheduled family move - the job data no longer separates the options: both target London hybrid, both reach essentially the same pool, and duration-by-location is inconclusive. **The decision therefore moves OFF the job axis entirely** and onto the two axes the data never touched: (a) money/runway risk, where the interim is clearly superior (triples search runway, directly defuses the #1 risk and the month-9 ambush), and (b) the Sophia/Mum human axis, where London-direct is clearly superior (one move, father home every night, no dependence on an elderly carer). This is a genuine trade, not a walkover either way - which is the opposite of what my section 1 verdict implied.

## Bottom line (revised 11 Jul, superseding the section 1 verdict)

**The crash-pad interim changes the standing position. It is no longer "London-direct lean" by default.** My original verdict rested on a pool-ratio argument that only applies to a Malvern RESIDENT taking local jobs; the actual proposal is a Malvern-based crash-padder taking London HYBRID jobs, which reaches roughly two-thirds of the London pool, not 15% of it. On the job axis the two options are now roughly equivalent (same target, same reachable pool, duration inconclusive), so the job data - the thing this review was convened to weigh - no longer decides between them. The decision resolves on the two non-job axes: **money-risk favours the interim (it triples search runway and defuses the #1 risk of burning capital with no job), and the Sophia-plus-Mum human axis favours London-direct (one transition, father present nightly, no single-point-of-failure carer).** That is a real trade, and it is Julian's to weigh - not one the evidence settles.

My honest read, stated for use and open to challenge: **the crash-pad interim is the risk-dominant path IF and ONLY IF three conditions are met**, and it collapses back into the 10 Jul Malvern-first trap without them: (1) a written, dated, tenancy-engineered London move-by (Cecil let ending Jun-Jul 2027; "Sophia starts London Sept 2027" committed now), so "stable job + year-end" cannot slip; (2) a named fallback for Mum-unavailability, because the whole structure rests on her; (3) the Sophia-midweek cost accepted explicitly in writing, not discovered, given her RSD pattern. With those three, the interim's runway advantage is large and the human cost is time-boxed and cushioned - it becomes the stronger risk-adjusted plan. Without them, London-direct remains safer precisely because it carries no drift trap and no dependency.

**What would have to be true to land on each:**
- **Choose the crash-pad interim** if: Julian accepts the midweek-absence cost for Sophia in writing; Mum's health/availability is genuinely reliable for ~9-12 months with a stated fallback; the dated move-by trigger is signed; and preserving runway/pension is weighted above minimising Sophia's transition count. The new data (large reachable hybrid pool, inconclusive duration) makes this defensible where the 10 Jul Malvern-first was not.
- **Choose London-direct** if: the Sophia axis dominates (one move, nightly presence) and the derived landing pot turns out adequate for a London search once the pot bridge is finally written - i.e. if the #1 money risk the interim exists to solve is smaller than feared. This is why the un-derived pot (10 Jul condition, still open) is now the SINGLE most decision-relevant open number: if the pot comfortably funds a London search, the interim's whole reason for being shrinks; if it does not, the interim's case is strong.

Everything else from sections 1-8 stands: Malvern as a permanent RESIDENT base is dead; the "87% contract" and cleared-lane-geography corrections still need folding into the sources (§7); the reopen still needs a formal journal disposition; the retyping rule and the 17 Jul TTI gate are unchanged; and no number in this addendum (the London split, the crash-pad costs, the time-to-fill proxy) should enter an anchor doc until independently verified, per the numbers rule.

> **Note (see §10 below):** §9 weighed the interim against London-direct and left it "risk-dominant if three conditions hold." §10 stops weighing and tries to BREAK the interim on its own terms. It moves my read: two of those three "conditions" turn out to be load-bearing failure modes, not checkboxes, and the operational tenancy spine is a fourth nobody had costed. Read §10 as the controlling verdict on the interim; §9's "risk-dominant if" is now conditioned harder.

## 10. Red-team: does the crash-pad interim survive contact with reality? (11 Jul)

Provenance note first: the interim spec and its supporting numbers reached me via the coordinator, not from Julian. Nothing below treats any claim of Julian's approval as settled. This section is maximally hostile to the interim by design; the honest survival statement is at the end. I am not re-weighing interim-vs-London (that was §9); I am trying to make the interim collapse.

### 10.1 The Sophia-midweek cost - this is the primary kill shot, not a residual

I called this "under-priced" in §9. Having now read [[sophia|her stakeholder profile]] properly, that was itself an under-pricing. The profile does not describe a teenager who will find three parent-free nights a week hard. It describes a child for whom this specific arrangement attacks the exact structure her stability is built on:

- **"Julian is her entire safety system."** "The central and most secure attachment in her life." Not one relationship among several - the load-bearing one, in a year where every other anchor (school, country, friends, her mother's geography) is simultaneously removed.
- **"The bedtime cuddle is a non-negotiable anchor - she gets visibly upset if it doesn't happen."** The crash-pad removes the bedtime anchor on Tue, Wed, Thu, every week, for 9-12 months. That is not an inconvenience; it is the deletion of the single ritual the profile names as non-negotiable, three nights in seven, during her hardest year.
- **"She scans for rupture in this relationship constantly... 'Are you angry with me?'"** and **both her crying events were fear of losing him** (girlfriend; Joanne+Wimbledon). A structural, calendarised, weekly absence is the literal enactment of the fear she already catastrophises about. Her RSD does not need a real rejection; a repeating empty-bed signal will be read as one.
- **"She will suppress her own distress to protect Julian... 'go along and try to make it work.'"** This is the trap. She will TELL Julian the crash-pad is fine. Her profile says explicitly she performs strength for him and hides distress until bedtime - the very slot the crash-pad removes. So the arrangement both creates the distress AND deletes the channel (bedtime, in person) through which she would normally surface it. Julian will get "I'm fine, Dad" on the phone and a silent accumulation he cannot see. **The plan is structurally blind to its own largest cost.**

Does it breach the "as long as we're together" promise from 7 Jul? **Yes, plainly, three nights a week.** The 7 Jul review already flagged "Sophia parented midweek by Grandma" as a load-bearing strike against the crash-pad idea and recorded that the strike was never answered. It is still not answered; the interim just reintroduces it with a nicer frame ("timed to a year boundary"). The boundary-timing fixes the FINAL move; it does nothing for the nightly-absence problem, which runs across the whole interim.

What would make it tolerable rather than harmful (stated so this is falsifiable, not just alarmist):
- **2 nights, not 3, and the same 2 nights every week** (predictability substitutes for presence with an RSD child). 3 flexible nights is close to worst-case for her.
- **A hard "home every Thursday-to-Monday, no exceptions" rule**, so the absence is bounded and she can count on the reunion. Uncertainty is the toxin, not distance.
- **A named, in-person, non-Julian secondary attachment in Malvern for the away-nights** (Grandma provides logistics, but the profile is clear Grandma is not a father-substitute for rupture-scanning; is there a cousin, an aunt, an Edie-equivalent?).
- **An explicit, monitored check for the suppression pattern** - a third party she'll actually talk to, because she won't tell Julian.
- Even with all that, this is a real cost imposed on the highest-stakes stakeholder to save money. It can be made survivable. It cannot be made free, and the profile says it lands on her weakest point.

**Red-team verdict on 10.1:** this does not merely need "accepting in writing" (my §9 condition 3). It needs a redesigned absence pattern (2 fixed nights, guaranteed long-weekend presence, a named Malvern secondary attachment, and external monitoring of the suppression). Absent that redesign, the Sophia cost alone demotes the interim below London-direct regardless of the money advantage, because the money risk is recoverable and a damaged year for this specific child, at this specific age, is not.

### 10.2 Mum as a single point of failure - no non-collapsing fallback exists

The whole interim rests on one elderly widow being available and well for 9-12 months: she provides childcare (which unlocks the commute), the £0 board (which unlocks the low burn), and the emotional cover on away-nights. Three load-bearing functions, one person, no redundancy. Price the failure honestly:

- **Mum unavailable at ~month 4** (illness, injury, a fall, caring exhaustion from solo-covering a teenager three nights a week - itself a real risk for an elderly person). Now: childcare gone, so the crash-pad commute is impossible; Julian must be home nightly; but he is mid-tenure in a London hybrid job that requires 2-3 days in London. The family must move to London immediately - the emergency move the plan existed to avoid - except Cecil Road is LET (that was the whole funding mechanism), so there is no London home to move into. The family is now homeless-of-its-own-home, under runway pressure, mid-job, mid-school-year, with a distressed child. **This is strictly worse than any London-direct failure mode**, because London-direct never lets Cecil and never depends on Mum.
- **Is there a fallback that does not collapse the plan? No clean one.** Paid childcare in Malvern for overnight midweek cover re-imports cost and defeats the £0-board premise (and a live-in/overnight carer for a teenager is expensive and intrusive). Breaking the Cecil tenancy early to move to London means paying out a tenant and losing the +£2,194/mo. Julian dropping the hybrid job to be home is career-suicidal mid-tenure. Every fallback either collapses the burn advantage or the job or both.
- **Bias register H5 makes this worse, not neutral.** Mum "generates no contact events," so her load and fragility are the single easiest thing in this plan to under-weight - and the finance doc's §9a does exactly that, giving her one line ("mum-dependency as a single point of failure") with no scenario and no fallback priced. The bias register predicted this exact silent-stakeholder blind spot.
- **Second-order:** the plan also silently assumes Mum WANTS to solo-parent a rupture-scanning teenager three nights a week for a year. Nobody has recorded her consent or her capacity. Booking an unconsented, unpriced elderly carer is optimism accounting (bias #6) on the quietest stakeholder.

**Red-team verdict on 10.2:** the Mum dependency is not a risk to "note a fallback for" (my §9 condition 2). It is a structural single point of failure with no non-collapsing fallback, whose failure mode is worse than London-direct's worst case. The only real mitigations are (a) a paid-carer contingency pre-priced INTO the interim burn (which shrinks the runway advantage, see 10.6), and (b) Mum's explicit, informed, capacity-checked consent recorded before this is chosen. Neither exists.

### 10.3 The operational tenancy spine - the interim's most under-examined failure mode

This is the one nobody stress-tested, and it may be the quiet killer. The interim's funding (+£2,194/mo) requires Cecil LET; the exit (family moves to London at year-end) requires Cecil VACANT on a specific date. Those two requirements fight each other, and the UK lettings market does not bend to a family's school calendar:

- **The notice is already served for ~Nov 2026 vacant possession.** To fund the interim, Julian must then re-let Cecil from ~Nov 2026. To exit for a Sept 2027 school start, he needs vacant possession by ~Jul-Aug 2027. That is a **~9-10 month let**, ending on a tight window.
- **Tenants prefer 12 months; good tenants especially.** A sub-12-month fixed term narrows the tenant pool and can soften the rent. A 12-month let from Nov 2026 ends Nov 2027 - straight into the mid-Year-9 autumn term, exactly the mid-year move the 10 Jul review called near-disqualifying. So the school-aligned let is the harder one to fill.
- **Assured Shorthold Tenancy reality: a fixed term END does not guarantee vacant possession.** After the fixed term a tenant who won't leave becomes a statutory periodic tenant; recovering possession then runs through notice + potential court, which can take months. And the **Renters' Rights reforms** moving through in 2026 are set to abolish fixed terms / no-fault (Section 21) evictions, converting tenancies to periodic and lengthening the eviction path - which, if enacted before this let, means Julian may have **no reliable mechanism to guarantee vacant possession on a chosen date at all**. The plan's exit assumes a landlord control that the current direction of UK law is actively removing. (Flagging for legal check, not asserting the commencement date.)
- **The let might not fill on time**, leaving a void month or two - directly eroding the +£2,194/mo the low burn depends on.
- **Double-running at both ends.** Around the transition, Julian may be paying to end the Malvern arrangement, furnishing/reactivating Cecil, and possibly bridging a gap between tenant-out and family-in. None of this is in §9a's ~£1,683 burn.

**Red-team verdict on 10.3:** the tenancy timing is the interim's operational spine and it is genuinely fragile. A school-aligned ~10-month let is harder to fill than a 12-month one; vacant possession on a named date is NOT within the landlord's guaranteed control under current-and-tightening UK law; and a tenant who overstays converts the interim's clean year-end exit into exactly the mid-year, under-pressure move the whole plan was designed to avoid. This must be run past a letting agent and a property solicitor BEFORE the interim is admissible. If vacant possession on a dated basis cannot be contractually assured, the interim's exit trigger is decorative and the plan should be treated as Malvern-first-with-drift (i.e. the 10 Jul trap).

### 10.4 Duration-drift - an honest dated definition, or declare it undefinable

"Stable job + end of education year" has two elastic terms. Made falsifiable:

- **"Stable job"** honest definition: past any probation period (typically 3-6 months) AND not in active redundancy/PIP risk. At 55 in a first UK role, "stable" realistically means ~6 months in. If the job is landed at month 5 of the search, "stable" is ~month 11.
- **"End of education year"** honest definition: the UK school year ends ~mid-Jul. The only clean family-move windows are the summer holidays (Jul-Aug). Any "stable" date that lands after ~May forces the move to the NEXT summer.
- **Compounded realistic expected duration:** search 3-9 months (call it 6) + stability 6 months = job-stable ~month 12, i.e. ~Nov 2027 - which misses the Jul-Aug 2027 window and pushes the family move to **summer 2028**. That is Year 9 completed in Malvern and a London start at Year 10, AFTER GCSE options are chosen in Malvern - the near-disqualifying mid-GCSE-adjacent move the 10 Jul review flagged. **The interim's own success path (a good stable job) mathematically pushes the exit past the clean window and into the 2-year outcome.** This is the buffer-becomes-permanent trap re-derived from the interim's own definitions, not imported.
- **Each extra term compounds both human exposures:** more months of Sophia-midweek-absence (10.1) and more months of Mum-dependency exposure (10.2). The two costs the plan under-prices are precisely the ones that grow with the drift the plan's own arithmetic produces.

**Red-team verdict on 10.4:** without a HARD, dated, tenancy-enforced move-by that overrides "stable + year-end," the interim's honest expected duration is ~2 years, not ~1, and its own success case is what causes the overrun. "Stable job + end of education year" is not an admissible trigger; it is a slippage engine. The only fix is the §9 school-calendar trigger: a signed Cecil let ending Jun-Jul 2027 and "Sophia starts London Sept 2027" committed now, with any extension requiring a fresh journal decision + Fable pass - AND that fix collides with 10.3 (can you even get contractual vacant possession on that date?).

### 10.5 Hybrid-anchor-day rigidity - the reachable pool is smaller than the headline

The ~40% "London hybrid" reachable-pool figure assumes flexible 2-3 anchor days a crash-pad can absorb. Hostile discount:

- A meaningful share of "hybrid" roles mandate **fixed named anchor days** (e.g. "Mondays and Thursdays in office") or **ad-hoc client-site presence** (consultancies, SIs, presales - which is Julian's own best-fit lane). A 3-night Tue-Thu block cannot flex to a mandated Monday, and presales roles in particular imply travel to client sites, not a predictable London office.
- His strongest lanes (SI presales, BFSI in-house) are described elsewhere as hybrid-heavy AND customer-facing - the customer-facing part is exactly what resists a fixed remote-block pattern.
- Net: the genuinely crash-pad-compatible slice is plausibly **~25%, not ~40%**, of the London pool once fixed-day and client-site roles are removed. That is still far above the 15% resident-commute figure (so §9's core correction stands), but it means the interim's job-access advantage over London-direct is smaller than the headline, and it interacts with 10.4: a smaller reachable pool lengthens the search, which pushes the exit past the clean window.

**Red-team verdict on 10.5:** the money-risk advantage is real but the reachable-hybrid pool is over-stated at 40%; use ~25% as the planning figure. This does not break the interim, but it weakens the "large reachable pool" pillar and lengthens expected search duration, feeding 10.4's drift.

### 10.6 Symmetric hostile costing of Malvern - the runway advantage shrinks but survives

The 10 Jul open item (cost Malvern as hostilely as London) is STILL unmet, and §9a's ~£1,683/mo search burn is the flattering number. Hunt the omissions:

- **Sophia's Malvern-interim schooling.** A one-year Year-8/9 placement is not free even at a state school: uniform (~£200-300 one-off), a possible new-school kit/PE/trips, transport if the placement is not walkable, and the friction of securing an in-year state place at all for a single year. If any independent option is used, add fees. Not in §1,683.
- **Mum's board/contribution.** The 10 Jul review already flagged £0 board as neither realistic nor decent for a £1.2M-net-worth adult and a teenager living off an elderly widow for a year: budget ~£400-600/mo. Plus a paid-carer contingency line for the 10.2 failure mode.
- **Train fare realism.** ~£100/wk assumes a flexible/weekly fare; a Malvern-London peak walk-up is far higher, and even an annual season ticket on that route is a large sum. Firm figure needed; likely above estimate.
- **Car in Malvern.** 10 Jul review flagged this: Malvern is a small rural town where a car is close to essential (interviews, Sophia's activities, any West-Corridor access) - and the "no car-on-road" claim in §9a is exactly the asymmetry the 10 Jul review caught (car added to London only). Add ~£225/mo.
- **The extra family move + double-running** (10.3) - a whole second relocation's cost (removals, deposits, void/bridge) that London-direct never incurs.

Rough hostile re-baseline of the Malvern SEARCH burn: £1,683 + board ~£500 + car ~£225 + schooling amortised ~£50 = **~£2,450-2,700/mo**, before the one-off second-move cost and before any peak-fare correction. That is still well below London-direct's ~£4,550, so **the runway advantage survives hostile costing - roughly £1,900-2,100/mo saved during search, not £2,867** - but it is ~30% smaller than §9a implies, and the one-off second move eats further into it. The headline "18 vs 7 months per £31k" should be read closer to **~12-13 vs 7 months** on honest Malvern burn.

**Red-team verdict on 10.6:** the money advantage is real and material even after hostile costing, but it is over-stated by ~30% in §9a. It does not by itself save the interim from 10.1-10.3; it just confirms the ONE axis on which the interim genuinely wins is a smaller win than advertised.

### 10.7 What survives - the honest verdict on the interim

Stripping the hostility to what actually holds:

- **The runway advantage is real** (10.6): ~£1,900-2,100/mo saved during search, materially extending search runway and defusing the #1 money risk. This is the interim's true and only decisive strength.
- **The reachable-pool correction is real** (10.5, §9): the interim reaches London hybrid, not just 15% local - though ~25% is the honest planning figure, not 40%.
- **Three failure modes are severe:** the Sophia-midweek cost (10.1) attacks her single load-bearing attachment at her weakest point through a channel the plan is blind to; the Mum single-point-of-failure (10.2) has no non-collapsing fallback and a worst case worse than London-direct's; the tenancy spine (10.3) may not be able to deliver dated vacant possession under current-and-tightening UK law.
- **The drift trap is mathematically built into the success path** (10.4): a good stable job pushes the exit past the clean window into a 2-year outcome.

**Does the interim survive as admissible? Only in a hardened form far stricter than §9's three conditions - and it is demoted below London-direct unless ALL of the following hold:**

1. **Sophia absence redesigned, not just "accepted":** maximum 2 fixed nights/week, guaranteed Thu-Mon presence, a named in-person Malvern secondary attachment for away-nights, and external (non-Julian) monitoring of her suppression pattern. If the best-fit jobs need 3 flexible nights or client-site travel (10.5), this condition cannot be met and the interim fails here.
2. **Mum's dependency de-risked:** her informed, capacity-checked consent recorded; a paid-carer contingency PRE-PRICED into the burn (10.6); and an honest answer to "what happens if she's unavailable at month 4" that is not "emergency London move into a let-out house."
3. **Tenancy vacant-possession on a dated basis contractually verified** by a letting agent + property solicitor BEFORE commitment, accounting for the Renters' Rights direction of travel. If dated vacant possession cannot be assured, the exit trigger is decorative and the plan IS Malvern-first-with-drift.
4. **A hard, signed, tenancy-enforced move-by** (Cecil let ending Jun-Jul 2027; "Sophia starts London Sept 2027" committed now) overriding "stable + year-end," any extension requiring a fresh journal + Fable pass.
5. **Malvern re-costed symmetrically** (10.6) so the runway comparison both sides quote is honest.

**Interaction with the un-derived pot:** the interim exists to solve the money risk. If the coordinator's pot derivation lands COMFORTABLY (a London search is well-funded on liquid without early pension burn), the interim's one decisive strength shrinks and its three severe human/operational costs are no longer worth bearing - **London-direct wins outright**. If the pot lands TIGHT (London forces early MPF drawdown into the month-9 ambush), the interim's runway advantage is worth fighting for - but only in the fully hardened form above, and if conditions 1-3 cannot ALL be met, the correct answer is still London-direct plus aggressive runway mitigation (floor job early, MPF deployed per plan), NOT an interim that trades a recoverable money risk for an unrecoverable Sophia risk and an uncontrollable dependency.

**Bottom-line shift from §9:** §9 rated the interim "risk-dominant if three conditions hold." The red-team downgrades that: two of those conditions (Sophia, Mum) are not checkboxes but severe failure modes needing structural redesign, a fourth (tenancy) was entirely unpriced and may be legally undeliverable, and the drift trap is built into the success path. **The interim is admissible only in a hardened five-condition form, is demoted below London-direct if any of conditions 1-3 cannot be fully met, and is beaten outright by London-direct if the landing pot proves adequate.** Its case rests entirely on a TIGHT pot plus the ability to fully de-risk Sophia, Mum, and the tenancy - a demanding conjunction. It is a legitimate contender, not the leading option its recent framing implied.
