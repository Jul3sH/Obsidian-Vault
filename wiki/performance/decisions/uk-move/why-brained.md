# Why BRAINED for This Decision

The framework chosen to run the UK relocation decision, and *why*, recorded here for future reference (and to calibrate the [[decision-maker-profile|Decision-Maker Profile]] for the next major decision).

## The framework

**BRAINED** = Benefits, Risks, Alternatives, Intuition, Need time / Nothing, Decision. The acronym is **B-R-A-I-N-D** (six elements, no E - "BRAINED" is only the readable spelling). It is the union of the two [[brain-brand-framework|BRAIN/BRAND]] variants: BRAIN contributes **Intuition**, BRAND contributes **Decision**. Julian's preferred variant because it keeps every element rather than dropping one.

## Why this model, not the alternatives

The framework picker ([[choosing-a-decision-framework]]) would route a clear, irreversible, research-available decision to the **7-step process**. We deliberately did *not* use it. Reasoning:

| Step | Why it fits this decision |
|------|---------------------------|
| **B** Benefits | Pros of staying HK + pros of moving UK, surfaced in the brain dump |
| **R** Risks | Cons reframed as risks - each gets a *probability*, so fear-driven beliefs ("I'll be single in the UK") are tested, not trusted. The engine for challenging the pros of staying and the cons of moving |
| **A** Alternatives | Already frozen: 3 scenarios + the 2 TTI branches. Pre-populated |
| **I** Intuition | The gut leans stay and is load-bearing here. BRAIN is the only common framework that builds the gut in *by design* - matches the gut-vs-analysis rule |
| **N** Need time / Nothing | "Is staying / doing nothing better?" - legitimate (staying is a real option). **The danger step** (see caveat) |
| **D** Decision | Forces an explicit, dated choice - the diagnosed failure mode is commitment, not analysis |

Three deciding reasons:
1. **Fits the failure mode.** The risk here is commitment, not insufficient analysis. The **D** step attacks that directly; a heavier 7-step would feed the avoidance.
2. **Names the gut.** The **I** step legitimises intuition as an input to be weighed and tested, rather than pretending the decision is purely analytical.
3. **Risk-framing challenges the stay-bias.** Treating every con as a probabilistic risk (and every "pro of staying" as a benefit with its own probability of materialising, e.g. P(well-paid HK work)) is what corrects the over-hopeful lean toward staying.

Plus: it is **already known and trusted**, so it carries low cognitive load under a 7-day deadline (ADHD-friendly - one less novel thing to learn).

## Not a loss of rigour

BRAINED is the *skeleton*, not a shortcut. The three analyses (financial, relationship, cross-workstream) and [[belief-assumption-testing|belief-testing]] run **inside** the B / R / A steps; the pre-mortem and worst-case acceptance sit at **N → D**; the [[decision-journal|Decision Journal]] Review-on date (31 July) wraps it. Research still feeds the steps - the Mind Tools source itself endorses feeding deeper analysis into the framework rather than abandoning it.

## The one caveat

The **N** step - "is doing nothing better?" - is the danger zone for a commitment-avoider: it is the crack where "push it to December" leaks in. Here it is legitimate (staying is a genuine option, not avoidance), but it must stay bounded by the **9 July anchor** and close into the **D** step, never float. Policed by the [[commitment-avoidance|Commitment Avoidance]] protocol.

## Key Takeaways

- **BRAINED = B-R-A-I-N-D**, six elements, no E. Union of BRAIN (Intuition) + BRAND (Decision).
- Chosen over the 7-step because the failure mode is **commitment, not analysis** - the D step is the point.
- The **I** step legitimises the gut; the **R** risk-framing tests fear-driven beliefs with probabilities; together they correct the stay-bias.
- No rigour lost: the three analyses and belief-testing fill B/R/A; research feeds the steps.
- Watch the **N** step - bound it to the 9 July deadline so it closes into D, never becomes a stall.
