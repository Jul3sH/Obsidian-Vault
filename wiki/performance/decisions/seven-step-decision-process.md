# Seven-Step Decision Process

The standard deliberate decision-making process. Best suited to high-stakes or complex decisions where time is available to research and reflect. Trades speed for thoroughness.

## The Steps

| Step | Label | What to do |
|------|-------|------------|
| 1 | **Identify the decision** | Define the problem or opportunity clearly. Misidentifying the issue leads to solving the wrong thing. |
| 2 | **Gather relevant information** | Collect data and insights - internal assessment and external research. Avoid information overload: only gather what is relevant and manageable. |
| 3 | **Identify alternatives** | Generate a list of possible options. Cast wide enough to avoid anchoring on the first option. |
| 4 | **Weigh the evidence** | Analyse pros and cons of each alternative. Assess outcomes, risks, and benefits. Tools: decision matrix, SWOT analysis. |
| 5 | **Choose among alternatives** | Select the option that best aligns with your goals, based on the analysis. Commit. |
| 6 | **Take action** | Implement the chosen solution. Develop a clear plan and assign tasks. |
| 7 | **Review your decision** | After implementation, evaluate outcomes against expectations. Learn and adjust if needed. |

## When to Use

- High-stakes or irreversible decisions
- Sufficient time available (hours to days)
- Problem is clear but options need research and evaluation
- Decision affects multiple stakeholders

## How It Compares

| Coverage | Present? |
|----------|----------|
| Problem definition | Yes (Step 1) |
| Information gathering | Yes (Step 2) |
| Alternatives | Yes (Step 3) |
| Risk analysis | Yes (Step 4, partial) |
| Explicit choice | Yes (Step 5) |
| Execution | Yes (Step 6) |
| Review/feedback loop | Yes (Step 7) |

The 7-step process is the most complete framework in this folder. Others are speed-optimised subsets of it.

## Key Takeaways

- Steps 1 and 2 are the most commonly skipped - people jump to alternatives before the problem is defined or the information is gathered.
- Step 7 (review) is the most commonly dropped - without it, no learning occurs and the same decision gets made poorly again.
- For faster decisions, [[sharran-simple-decision-framework|Sharran's framework]] covers steps 1, 2, and 6 in a real-time conversation.
- For pre-commitment evaluation of a clear binary decision, [[brain-brand-framework|BRAIN/BRAND]] covers steps 3, 4, and 5 efficiently.
