# Proactive Risk Management

## What It Is
- Identifying foreseeable risks early and proposing mitigation before escalation is forced
- Using formal channels (issue logs, risk registers, escalation paths) to surface problems above board
- Agreeing escalation protocols in advance so raising an issue is procedural, not confrontational
- Treating resource gaps, scope changes, and external blockers as logged risks, not verbal complaints

## What It Looks Like When It Goes Wrong
- Waiting until a problem explodes to mention it, then presenting it without any mitigation ("sob story")
- Relying on verbal mentions rather than logged, tracked issues
- Raising issues with management in ways that make others look bad or cause defensiveness
- Not caveating deliverables or adding items to the risk register when constraints are known

## Antidotes
- Agree the escalation path at the start of every project or role
- Log every constraint and resource gap in the issue register as it appears
- Only escalate to management when you can demonstrate you have done everything within your authority first
- Caveat deliverables explicitly when constraints are binding; get caveats signed off where possible
- Reference log numbers in meetings rather than raising issues live in front of adversaries

## My Pattern

I have a recurring tendency to either suffer in silence until a situation blows up, or raise issues verbally without a paper trail, giving others the opportunity to disseminate blame. The Marcel lesson is the clearest case: bringing a problem to Stuart without mitigation got shut down immediately. The DA resource issues are the flip side: they were not logged, so the RNMs could redirect blame. The lesson is the same: formal, early, documented risk management removes the political attack surface.

## Key Takeaways
- Bosses are not interested in problems without mitigation — they want to see you have done everything possible first
- Formal issue logging is protection, not bureaucracy
- Agreeing escalation paths before they are needed makes using them above reproach
- Caveating deliverables explicitly and getting sign-off is the only reliable way to manage unrealistic expectations
