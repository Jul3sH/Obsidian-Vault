# Email Warm-up & Infrastructure

**Job to be done:** Set up sending infrastructure (domains, mailboxes, SPF/DKIM/DMARC) and warm those domains so emails land in inbox, not spam.

## Inputs
- New domain(s) purchased for outreach
- Mailboxes configured on those domains
- Target daily send volume (determines warm-up duration)

## Outputs
- Warmed mailboxes with healthy sender reputation
- SPF / DKIM / DMARC configured
- Deliverability score tracking
- Inbox placement test results
- Blacklist monitoring alerts

## Key Capabilities to Evaluate
- **Warm-up pool size** — larger pools (4M+ accounts) simulate more realistic engagement
- **Pool quality** — real accounts vs. synthetic; aged Google/Microsoft accounts vs. generic
- **Premium / isolated pool** — option to exclude low-quality accounts (worth paying for)
- **Warm-up volume capacity** — daily limit per mailbox; unlimited vs. capped
- **AI-generated content** — warm-up emails that look human, not templated
- **Template warm-up** — warm your actual campaign templates, not just generic content
- **Inbox placement testing** — seed list tests before going live
- **Blacklist monitoring** — real-time alerts if domain/IP gets flagged
- **SPF / DKIM / DMARC validation** — pre-flight DNS check before warm-up starts
- **Dedicated IP** — needed for very high volume (500K+/month)

## Vendors in This Category
Snov.io, Apollo, Instantly, Lemlist (Lemwarm), Saleshandy, HubSpot (Dedicated IP only)

## Key Takeaways
- Instantly has the largest warm-up network (4.2M+ accounts) with a Premium pool of aged Google/Microsoft accounts
- Lemlist's Lemwarm uses 20,000+ real domains from 150+ countries — closed network (not synthetic)
- Saleshandy and Snov.io include unlimited warm-up on Pro plans
- HubSpot warm-up is only for Dedicated IP add-on — not relevant for cold email
- All platforms recommend 30 days minimum before sending cold campaigns
