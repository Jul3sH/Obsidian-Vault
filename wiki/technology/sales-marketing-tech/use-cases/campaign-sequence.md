# Campaign & Sequence Management

**Job to be done:** Build, send, and manage multi-step outreach campaigns — including follow-up logic, variant testing, and pacing controls.

## Inputs
- Verified prospect list
- Email copy / templates
- Sequence logic (delays, conditions, branching rules)

## Outputs
- Sent email sequences with automated follow-ups
- A/B or A/Z test results
- Campaign analytics (open rate, reply rate, click rate, bounce rate)
- Meeting bookings from campaign

## Key Capabilities to Evaluate
- **Multi-step sequence builder** — visual drag-and-drop vs. code-based
- **Conditional branching** — if/then logic based on opens, clicks, replies
- **A/B or A/Z testing** — how many variants supported (2 vs. 26)
- **Multi-channel** — email only, or email + LinkedIn + calls in one sequence
- **Sending limits & pacing** — max emails/day per mailbox; auto-randomisation
- **Inbox rotation** — spread sends across multiple mailboxes to protect domains
- **Smart send time** — send when individual prospect is most likely to open
- **Spintax** — randomise phrasing per send to avoid ESP spam detection
- **Sequence health check** — pre-flight score before launch
- **Campaign analytics** — drill-down metrics per step and per variant
- **Calendar / booking integration** — Calendly detection and scheduler links

## Vendors in This Category
Snov.io, Apollo, HubSpot, Instantly, Lemlist, Saleshandy, Clay, n8n, Make, Gumloop, OpenClaw

## Key Takeaways
- Instantly offers A/Z testing (26 variants) and SISR (dedicated IP sharding/rotation) — best for high-volume pure email
- Lemlist is the strongest for native multichannel (email + LinkedIn + calls) in a single sequence
- Apollo combines prospecting + sequencing + CRM in one platform — strongest all-in-one
- n8n and Make give full programmatic control but require technical setup
- Saleshandy's A/Z testing (26 variants) and subsequence branching are underrated at its price point
