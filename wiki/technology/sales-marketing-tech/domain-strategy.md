# Domain Strategy for Cold Email

Guidelines for determining how many domains and mailboxes you need, plus which TLDs to use for maximum inbox placement.

## How Many Domains Do I Need?

### Volume-Based Formula

**`Domains needed = Daily email volume ÷ 100`**

| Daily Volume | Formula Result | Recommended |
|--------------|---|---|
| 33–67 emails/day (1,000–2,000/mo) | 0.33–0.67 domains | **1 domain** (2 for rotation) |
| 100 emails/day (3,000/mo) | 1.0 domains | **1–2 domains** |
| 200 emails/day (6,000/mo) | 2.0 domains | **2–3 domains** |
| 500 emails/day (15,000/mo) | 5.0 domains | **5+ domains** |

### Mailbox-Per-Domain Ratio

**Per mailbox sending limit:** ≈ 20 emails/day (some sources allow 30–50, but 20 is conservative)

**Mailboxes per domain:** 3–5 mailboxes per domain

| Mailboxes/Domain | Daily Capacity | Example: 1,000/mo |
|-----------------|---|---|
| 3 mailboxes × 20/day | 60 emails/day | ✓ Handles 1,000–2,000/mo |
| 5 mailboxes × 20/day | 100 emails/day | ✓ Handles up to 3,000/mo |

### Best Practice: Multi-Domain Rotation

Even if one domain handles your volume, maintain **at least 2 domains** for:
- **Warm-up rotation** — new domains need reputation building before full volume
- **Sender rotation** — reduces spam flag risk by spreading sends
- **Risk isolation** — if one domain gets flagged, the other keeps your campaigns running
- **Mailbox rotation** — alternate which mailbox sends to each recipient

**Recommended strategy for 1,000–2,000 emails/month:**
- **Primary domain**: 3–5 mailboxes, handles full volume
- **Secondary domain**: 2–3 mailboxes, used for rotation and warm-up buffer

## TLD Selection & Inbox Placement

### Trust Tiers (2026 Data)

| TLD | Trust Tier | Inbox Placement | Recommendation |
|-----|-----------|-----------------|---|
| **.com** | Top | 90–95% | **Best choice** — highest universal trust |
| **.net** | Top | 88–93% | Strong alternative, nearly as trusted as .com |
| **.org** | Top | 87–92% | Excellent for B2B; appears less in spam filters despite popularity |
| **.co** | Top/Mid | 85–91% | Popular startup option, solid trust |
| **.io** | Middle | 85–90% | Reliable in tech niche; grouped with .net for trust |
| **.co.uk** (major ccTLDs) | Middle | 84–90% | OK for UK-focused outreach, not universally trusted |
| **.info** | Lower-tier | 70–80% (higher spam rates) | **Not recommended** — triggers spam filters, reduces trust |

### TLD Selection by Use Case

**Best for B2B cold outreach globally:**
- **.com** (highest trust)
- **.org** (excellent for non-profits, associations, B2B communities)
- **.net** (technical credibility)

**Acceptable for tech-focused outreach:**
- **.io** (expected in tech circles)
- **.co** (startup audience)

**Geographic targeting:**
- **.co.uk**, **.de**, **.fr**, etc. — Use for country-specific campaigns, but understand they have lower global trust than .com/.org

**Avoid:**
- **.info** — Associated with spam, phishing, and low-quality sites; emails from .info domains have higher filter rates

### Domain Registration Best Practices

- **Register multiple TLDs** (e.g., company.com AND company.org) — costs ~$15/year each, protects your brand and provides backup reputation
- **Age your domains** — New domains need 2–4 weeks of warm-up before full volume (this is why a secondary domain is valuable)
- **Use real company info** — Whois privacy is fine, but use legitimate business registration details
- **Avoid bulk registrations** — Registering 10+ domains from the same registrant account can trigger spam filters; stagger registrations over weeks

## Configuration Checklist for 1,000–2,000 Emails/Month

- [ ] **Primary domain**: .com, .net, .org, or .co TLD
- [ ] **Secondary domain** (optional but recommended): Same or different TLD from primary
- [ ] **Mailboxes per domain**: 3–5 mailboxes on primary domain
- [ ] **Sending per mailbox**: ≤ 20 emails/day (60–100/day per domain)
- [ ] **Warm-up**: Both domains run warm-up for 2–4 weeks before full volume
- [ ] **Sender rotation**: Alternate mailboxes across recipients to avoid pattern detection
- [ ] **Monitoring**: Track bounce rates, spam complaints, and inbox placement per domain

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **One domain is enough** for 1,000–2,000 emails/month if properly configured (3–5 mailboxes, ≤ 20 emails/mailbox/day)
2. **Two domains is safer** — enables warm-up rotation and risk isolation without increasing monthly costs
3. **.com, .org, .net, .io, .co** are trusted; **.info is not**
4. **TLD choice matters less than consistency** — pick one trusted TLD and maintain it (switching TLDs mid-campaign can hurt reputation)
5. **Age and warm-up** — new domains need 2–4 weeks of reputation building; plan accordingly
