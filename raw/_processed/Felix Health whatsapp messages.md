I'm providing a summary based on my interpretation from the conversation with the physician. Please research as you see fit before your decision. The link above gives a good overview and perplexity.ai is very good if you want to dive deeper (exact risk percentages are up for debate). 

Benefits 
In summary it's about improving quality of life rather than trying to extend life, and it doesn't have any bearing on the alzheimer's itself. The surgeon explained that Alzheimer’s reduces the brain’s ability to tolerate abnormal CSF dynamics, so CSF-flow problems can have a bigger functional impact.

Anticipated benefits are improved walking, balance, urinary continence, cognition and alertness. Evidence that it will be a success is based on the ‘better than expected’ results from the LB procedure. 

Risks
The more significant risks are post op, but the general impression from the physician is that they are nominal. He quotes the following risks and probabilities; 

1% risk of a fatal operating complication due to Dad's age and condition going under general anaesthetic. It’s a relatively short operation at 2 hours and it’s regarded as a relatively straightforward operation that is often carried out by interns.  

5% risk of blockage or infection once it's in which can lead to ventriculitis/meningitis and systemic illness. It is a major cause of shunt morbidity and can be life‑threatening if not treated promptly. 

5% VP shunt over‑drainage (overshunting) where the shunt removes CSF too fast or in excess. In more severe cases it can cause bleeding or neurologic deterioration. The argument for it being lower probability risk for Dad is that many young children and toddlers have a VP shunt and it survives their movements, which are significantly more intense than Dad’s. 

Summary
In summary I agree with Wendy that the anticipated benefits outweigh the risk. Whilst I was comparing the difference over a smaller baseline than Wendy (3 days prior to the LB and 2 days after),  perceptively I could see a significant difference to movement and cognition.
