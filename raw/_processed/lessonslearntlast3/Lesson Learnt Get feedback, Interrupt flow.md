**Situation:** I've noticed when I was preparing for presentations and even for an interview, that I get stuck in flow state doing something that is normally about avoiding emotional discomfort.
So I get onto flow state (perfectionist mode) ensuring i have a robust understanding something that I feel might come up, and I don't want to be embarrassed not knowing, or doing a really polished presentation for something that I haven't perhaps even clarified as the right content.  I'm reluctant to get feedback or clarify requirements because it might interrupt my flow. Then when I get feedback, or a clarifications. I realise that I I'm on the wrong track and should have changed course and I should have got the feedback earlier.
**Root cause:** I think this gets down to my ADHD. I like focusing on the things that that will make me look good, so knowledge acquisition so i will be knowledgeable if questioned. Or doing very polished presentations so that I look good.
**Lesson learnt:**
I need to have a checkpoint that is enforced that I do the due diligence I need before I get stuck in flow state, and I have checkpoints where I force myself to interrupt it.
