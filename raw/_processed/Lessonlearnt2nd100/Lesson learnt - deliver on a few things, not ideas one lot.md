Situation: I provided Paul with my “Brainstorm: account improvements plan, which was clearly too much to deliver. This then raised questions over my ability to prioritise, which I am sure helped him come to the conclusion I am not prioritising effectively.

Root cause: I wasn’t clear what I was trying to achieve with the deliverable, and it cause more good than bad. Paul wanted to see me deliver a few things, not brainstorm.

Lesson learnt – people are more interested in delivering on a few initiatives,  than having a load of ideas,  

Lesson learnt – be clear what the purpose of any interaction with management is. What is the message I am trying to deliver.
