**Situation:** Marcel came over to our area being a drama queen about the fact that Nigel had left BG. He started a spiel about how Nigel was irreplaceable, and how he had approached IBM to get e replacement and had been turned down. Stuart wasn’t impressed. Kept saying “so what, he’s the only person in the work who can do this is he” and “you are a PM, you should be managing the Risk. What are you going to do about it” He then said to us that they have known Nigel is the only DC guy for the last 6 months, and haven’t done anything about it. Basically they haven’t managed the risk, and hadn’t suitably considered contingencies.

**Root cause:** Stuart just saw this as a sob story, and was being defensive because BG are making life difficult. 
**Lesson Learn:**  Boss’ really aren’t interested in you bringing them problems, especially if you haven’t shown any initiative at getting them resolved first. Even what feels like a lightweight  "discussion in passing", it isn't if it's bringing potential problems to the attention of your boss. 
Also in Prince2 terms, really they should have both cost and time tolerance, and should be looking at these. He mentioned that Nigel would be coming back in Jan, so did we really not have a 2 week time tolerance built into the project. Don't create a drama, or appear not to be proactively addressing problems. Only mention what you have resolved, or what has to be escalated, and at that point make sure that you can demonstrate you have done everything you have authority to do, that you could have done, to resolve the problem. 

Proactively manage risk, and suggest mitigation rather than bring problems to the table
