Lesson learnt – I had got a good system working at BT for dealing with large volumes of email under a lot of demand. I then revamped the system when I was working in a totally different environment (at CC), and then radically changed it again based on a new system that looked good when I was just starting at BT. After six months of chaos, I revamped the system to one that worked, and it was basically like the one that had been working in similar conditions in BG.

Root cause – I made radical theoretical changes and didn’t without testing them properly first.

Lesson learnt – Don’t radically change systems in one go. Make small changes and test them in production, in an environment that is a good representation of where they will be used.
