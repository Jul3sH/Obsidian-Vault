**Situation** : - basically when we were first doing the pattern, we were trying to make sure that it worked, and spent time establishing what was wrong with the old system and how we could change that. Stuart didn't give a shit, and his expectations had been unrealistically set by Gio, who is never going to admit that, and will therefore just use his position to blame the DA. 
**Root Cause**: What should have happened is that pnce we established Gio had made these commitments, we should have established whether we can go back to Stuart and reset his expectations. If we then noticed a reluctance, then we should have worked strictly to these timescales. This means that we should have worked out what we could achieve, and then heavily caveat what we have done, working on the basis that we can have continual service improvement once the process in place. 
**Lesson learnt** : Realistically once the expectation was set, the damage was done. We should have asked the managers to confirm ( in writing) that this time constraint must be adhered to, and then we should have thought outside the box and delivered. 

We could have used use prototype to test the first iteration as they wanted immediate results.
