**Situation**: Stuart sent an email to the DA saying he was pissed off and we had failed. I responded by voicing my opinion about circumstances and bad communication with his management team. 

**Root cause**: It wasn't that we haven't delivered, it was that we haven't delivered anything he wants. Rather than a response blaming others, he wanted to see recognition that we will review and focus on delivering what he wants. 

He also didn't want me to blame his management team, even if it was a subtle insinuation through voicing my opinion. He is responsible for them so that reflects badly on him, and he works closely with them and likes them. 
 
**Lessons learned**:

This was my chance to show him what I am about directly, amd respond proactively, but I responded immaturely and defensively.  I should have stated the facts and then let Stuart do the conclusions the communication isn't working.

As Dad suggested in general keep things facts. 

The best position to be in would have been to be able to state facts that point to me following the correct processes and highlighting any constraints, issues, escalations.
