**Situation:** Noticed that I had forgotten the exact events that happened over the Standards production, which meant that when I sat in a meeting and they were stating contradicting requirements ( i.e that I should focus on Standards and nothing else) , I didn't refer then to their initial objectives ( Delegate standards, focus on local requirements). I therefore didn't point this contradiction out and defend myself. 

**Root Cause:** Whilst I was prepared for the discussion, I didn't go in with a clear understanding of the events surrounding the subject of largest contention - the production of the Standards. It was the first time this paradigm shift was made clear, and I wasn't prepared to ask for clarification in relation to the previous objectives. 
**Lesson Leaned** -  Always keep a high level log of events surrounding contentious deliverables. Make sure before I go into meetings that I have reviewed these. Also make sure that in such meetings I am very clear in addressing the most important issues. Pointing this contradiction in the meeting wouldn't have achieved anything unless handled correctly. What I should have said is "OK, so up to date the direction we had is to delegate standards and focus on regional activity, what you would now like me to focus on is creating standards and not regional QA activity". Also keep track of events so that I can remind myself I am not at fault when I am being subjected to office politics.Finally, follow up every meeting with a summary of events and actions, even it's adhoc, so that I can refer back to it if requirements change. 

Additional Note since reviewing this LL.  Keeping logs is time consuming, so alternatively make sure I have an audit trail in case I need it. The best way to manage this have been for us to get the RNMs to sign off our objectives with clearly stated CSFs, send out a follow up email confirming any changes to these objectives, and state the change in objectives in reports to Stuart.
