****Situation:**** Situation with Standards delivery being manipulated to look like a DA issue, when in fact we stuck to an agreed plan, which they failed to communicate to Stuart. 
**Root Cause.** Not taking enough control of the whole situation, giving the RNMs too much space to distort the facts, and being too soft. 
**Lesson learnt:** I need to not be embarrassed or scared to document meetings and make sure that managers are keeping to an agreed set or requirements. Have the mentality that If I fail, we all fail, and I am doing them a favour by keeping them on track, "I am genuinely concerned for us all "  or " concerned that we may not be on track"  ..... rather than " you aren't playing by the rules, that' not fair" etc. So Minute every meeting and ask in the meeting "should I take minutes" in an enthusiastic, efficient manner. Get into taking minutes at every meeting to make sure everything is caught and I sound efficient. If the meeting isn't minuted, then send confirmation by email afterwards of all actions and deliverables. 

**Recent note**: Make sure objectives are clear, and changes in objectives are tracked.
