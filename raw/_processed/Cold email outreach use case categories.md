Here is a proposed full category structure for your email cold outreach catalogue, built around the natural lifecycle of a cold email campaign. Each category maps to a distinct job your outreach stack needs to do, and you can either keep them as separate catalogues or group them into a higher-level umbrella.

**Here's what I'd recommend as the core categories:**

---

## Proposed Category Structure for Cold Email Outreach

---

## Category 1: Prospecting

**"I need to find leads and build a list of contacts to reach out to"**

This is the catalogue we've already built.

- Lead sourcing (LinkedIn, web, extensions, web scraping)
    
- Bulk lead lookup (spreadsheet → emails, domains → contacts)
    
- Account intelligence (ICP search, firmographic research, enrichment, signals)
    
- Reverse email lookup (email → LinkedIn URL / full profile)
    

---

## Category 2: Email Verification

**"I need to check that the emails I found are real and safe to send to"**

This is the catalogue we've already built.

- Core verification (single + bulk checks, accuracy, risk scoring, catch-all, greylisting, spam trap)
    
- Integration / delivery (extensions, APIs, CSV, CRM)
    
- Deliverability protection (bounce rate tracking, blacklist detection)
    

---

## Category 3: Email Infrastructure / Domain Warm-up

**"I need to set up my sending infrastructure so my domain doesn't get flagged as spam"**

This is the start of the warm-up catalogue we were building.

- Sending infrastructure setup (buy domains, set up mailboxes, configure SPF/DKIM/DMARC)
    
- Domain warm-up (automated warm-up, warm-up pool/network, capacity limits, AI-generated content)
    
- Dedicated IP warm-up (for high-volume senders on shared IP risks)
    

---

## Category 4: Campaign & Sequence Management

**"I need to build, send, and manage my cold email campaigns"**

This is the category you're describing with "cold email sending, follow-up sequences."

- Campaign / sequence creation (build multi-step email sequences with delays)
    
- Email follow-up logic (auto follow-ups on no-reply, conditional branching)
    
- Sending limits & pacing (volume controls per mailbox, per domain, per campaign)
    
- A/B testing & variant testing (test subject lines, copy, send times)
    
- Multi-channel sequences (email + LinkedIn + phone/dialer in one campaign flow)
    
- Calendar / booking integration (Calendly, scheduler links, meeting detection)
    

---

## Category 5: Email Personalization & Content

**"I need to create and personalize the actual email content so it doesn't look like spam"**

This is the "email creation for personalized email" category you mentioned.

- Dynamic personalization (merge fields, variables, if/then content logic)
    
- AI email generation (AI-written opening lines, full emails, reply suggestions)
    
- Spintax / text spinning (multiple variations of the same line to avoid detection)
    
- Template library management (save, reuse, and share email templates across campaigns)
    
- Multilingual personalization (auto-translate or generate emails in the prospect's language)
    

---

## Category 6: Unified Inbox & Reply Management

**"I need to manage replies across multiple sending accounts from one place"**

- Unified inbox (pull replies from all sending accounts into a single view)
    
- AI reply detection & triage (hot lead vs. out-of-office vs. spam)
    
- Reply macros & quick responses (one-click reply templates)
    
- CRM sync from replies (auto-create or update contacts/deals from replies)
    
- Booking detection (detect Calendly bookings or meeting intent in replies)
    

---

## Category 7: Deliverability & Reputation Management

**"I need to protect my sender reputation and keep emails landing in the inbox"**

This overlaps partially with Email Warm-up but is broader and post-campaign.

- Bounce rate monitoring & high-bounce safeguard (auto-pause on spike)
    
- Spam trigger detection (check copy before sending for spam words, formatting issues)
    
- Blacklist / DNS monitoring (track if your domain or IP gets blacklisted)
    
- Inbox placement testing (seed list tests before sending to see inbox vs. spam placement)
    
- Sender reputation scoring (real-time reputation dashboards)
    
- Recovery workflows (what to do when deliverability drops — pause, re-warm, rotate)
    

---

## Optional: Category 8: Sales Engagement / CRM Integration

**"I need to tie my outreach data back into my CRM and sales process"**

- CRM 2-way sync (push leads to CRM, pull data back into outreach)
    
- Pipeline tracking from outreach (link sequences to deals in CRM)
    
- Analytics & reporting (reply rates, open rates, meeting bookings, attribution)
    
- Team collaboration features (shared templates, shared lists, role-based access)
    

---

## How These Categories Fit Together

Your cold outreach job can be mapped as a lifecycle where each category serves a phase:

text

`Prospecting → Verification → Infrastructure/Warm-up → Personalization → Campaign/Sequence → Inbox/Reply Management → Deliverability Protection → CRM Sync`

If your goal is a clean, catalogue-usable structure, I'd recommend **6 core categories** (1–6, plus 7 merged into 3 or kept standalone), because Categories 7 and 8 tend to be features within the other categories rather than standalone tools.

**The 6 core categories I'd prioritise for your catalogue:**

|#|Category|Primary Job|
|---|---|---|
|1|Prospecting|Find and enrich leads|
|2|Email Verification|Check emails are valid before sending|
|3|Email Infrastructure & Domain Warm-up|Set up and warm your sending infrastructure|
|4|Email Personalization & Content|Create personalised, non-spammy email copy|
|5|Campaign & Sequence Management|Build, send, and manage multi-step outreach|
|6|Unified Inbox & Reply Management|Manage responses across all sending accounts|
