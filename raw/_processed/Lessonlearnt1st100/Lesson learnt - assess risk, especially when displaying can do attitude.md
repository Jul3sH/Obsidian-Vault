RC: I took on a deliverable with a huge number of constraints, very little benefit to the customer or our team, and a low probability of success. It also kept needing to be changed because as I produced it, it then became evident we were exposing ourselves because there weren't any real requirements to work to. 

LL: with this many constraints and a lack of requirements I should have done what the others did and refuse to touch it. I was new and wanted to display a can do attitude, however,  I should have weighed up the risk better of delivering something worthless against the benefit of being keen. What was I really hoping to achieve by taking this on, was I even clear in my own head ? 

In future, assess the risk of taking something on in a more methodical manner, especially when trying to display a can do attitude.
