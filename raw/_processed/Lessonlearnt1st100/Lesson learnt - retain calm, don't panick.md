****Situation:****
 Going in to see my Director, unprepared ( because I thought he had overheard me slating a regional manager ( even though a known snake ), and then ranting about my wishes, without really thinking through what feedback would get to my Manager. My director passed on the message, and all he heard was problems and whingeing, which also reflected badly on the manager. 

**Root Cause:**
 Knee jerk reaction. Speaking to leadership in a non controlled succinct manner. Under estimating bond between manager/director. Getting things off my chest. 

**Lessons Learned.**
 As Dad said, don't go into battle unless prepared to go fir the kill. Don't speak to Senior management unless prepared, and sounding very solution oriented. Don't whinge ! Vent frustrations elsewhere, and remain professional. Think tactically. My intention is to leave, so I should remain low until I have done so. There wasn't really much positive to  to achieve from this exercise other than whingeing. Cool off period. I rushed in to rectify one perceived problem, and caused another. In the same way that I don't respond to emails, I should have waiting before speaking to Stuart. 

Learn to control my emotion, and learn not to panick
