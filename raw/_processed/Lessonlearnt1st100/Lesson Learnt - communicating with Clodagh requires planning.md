Situation: Clodagh was feeling that I bullied her over not quitting DB because we need the money, and also feels like I am being a hypocrite because I have a few weeks off. 

**Root Causes:** 

1. They way that I communicated it was all wrong. 
	
2. She felt I took three months off to 'discover' what I wanted to do, and that I then chose to take time off from CC voluntarily. She may actually believe this, or is possible just using it as ammunition for poor me's because she wants time of work. 
	
3. She also feels that I am being dictatorial by telling her she can't resort to three days a week in Hong Kong or take extra time off for maternity leave.

**Lessons Learnt :** 

1. Think about the communication before engaging, and choose a suitable method more carefully. I need to practice the proper techniques more, and these were perfect opportunities. Look for the triggers, and apply the correct techniques. 
	
2. I should always have clodagh on side. Yes she may be jealous or feeling sorry for herself, but it's for the wrong reasons because I was working. I need her to understand why I am doing what I am doing. I also need to avoid whinging or communicating weakness that she will translate as me wimping out. 
	
3. I got her to agree, but she is claiming I bullied her. This is really important one. I needed her to agree before making a commitment because we can't afford her to do three days a week, but I knew deep down she was just appeasing me. It's not about just getting her to agree if she doesn't understand why, or feel she was part of the decision. I need to look at the techniques in crucial conversations and make her part of the decision. I also need to use the techniques in influencer - she needs to understand why it's important for herself, not have me telling her in a dictatorial tone. I need to her to be more aware of where we are financially, what we need to retire, and set realistic expectations for inheritanc
4. Use influencer techniques to help her understand finances and why I am over labouring the point. 
5. Empathise when she is feeling sorry for herself and being unnecessarily jealous.
