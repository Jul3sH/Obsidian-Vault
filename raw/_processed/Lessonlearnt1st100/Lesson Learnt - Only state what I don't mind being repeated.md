LL: Think of the consequences of what I am about to say when confiding in people. This is a new one, but also based on the same principle as bitching, and that is that people talk. So think of the consequences of what I am about the say when confiding in people or saying what I think they would like to hear where others are concerned. In the same way as with bitching I may feel like I have a strong relationship and my message will not be relayed, but that is nearly always not true. I particularly need to watch it when I think I am subtly trying to make a point, because often it isn't subtle, and the point will get relayed. 

I particularly need to be careful at work. Don't think that Paul, Anthony are suddenly my best buddies on the basis of a few days and also don't under estimate that the will have their own agendas. 

People always talk, so If I wouldn't like it repeated, don't say it. If it needs to be said, state facts not opinions, and always appear to be pragmatic in trying to solve a problem rather than having a bitch.
