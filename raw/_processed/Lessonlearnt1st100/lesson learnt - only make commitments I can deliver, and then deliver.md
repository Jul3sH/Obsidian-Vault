sit: Agreeing delivery dates, and then not making sure that I deliver on time
RC: for once reason or another I procrastinate, and justify it thinking "it won;t matter, i'll sort it a few days late". Whilst they may not make a big fuss, it gets noticed. Part of the problem is probably overcommitment. I think to myself," I am really busy, everyone knows I am, it won;t matter". But the most effective people don;t over commit, and always deliver. 

LL: If I am going to get into the habit of delivering when it does matter, I need to practice. Further more I need to get a reputation as someone who delivers. So the starting point is to always be aware of my current schedule, accurately forecast the effort, set  realistic expectations and then deliver.
