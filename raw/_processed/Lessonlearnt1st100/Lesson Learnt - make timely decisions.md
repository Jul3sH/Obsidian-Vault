Situation: Now that I have decided to take the chance with Computacenter, I am beginning to think positively about the opportunities it may offer rather than the negatives.
Root cause: I think I am very good at telling myself stories to take the easy option and avoid fear. I have also been endlessly speculating about what the next best move is, and over thinking what the next step should be, and I think this gets down to me panicking about getting the decision wrong. 
Lesson learnt: Feel the fear and do it anyway. Take next steps rather then endlessly speculate,  because I will then know. I also need to stop trying to establish the "perfect" answer, as it probaly doesn't exist, and things will change anyway. And finally I need to practice making uncomfortable decisions, as I clearly donlt like doing it. The impact will never be that bad if I get it wrong, Just use Brian's fear busting formula.
