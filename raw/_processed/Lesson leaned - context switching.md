Root cause: the pattern you noticed—initial resistance, forced engagement, hyperfocus, then intense irritation at a new intrusion—is not merely a universal dislike of being told what to do; it reflects a heightened vulnerability to context‑switching breakdowns that are frequently reported by adults with ADHD. 

Why ADHD makes context switching harder: 

- **Hyperfocus inertia**: When an ADHD brain enters hyperfocus, it becomes extremely difficult to disengage, as the task feels “all‑on” and shifting away can feel like “pulling your leg out of quicksand”.
- **Executive‑function deficits**: ADHD affects the brain’s ability to plan, start, stop, and switch tasks smoothly; what feels simple for others requires extra effort just to begin or end a transition.
- **Working‑memory challenges**: Holding multiple task‑sets in mind while switching is harder, leading to errors and frustration when attention is forced to shift.
- **Emotional reactivity**: Interruptions during hyperfocus can trigger intense irritation or anxiety because the brain perceives the disruption as a threat to a rewarding, absorbed state.
