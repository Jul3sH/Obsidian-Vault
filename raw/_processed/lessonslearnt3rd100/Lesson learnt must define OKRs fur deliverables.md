The situation was that, for my preparation for Cisco, I did a very good job of preparing the presentation. Admittedly, I lost some time, so I went down a rabbit hole. Then I clearly didn't have enough time for the learning, and it was a technical interview. If I hadn't found cunning ways to extend my time, I would never have done the technical preparation, and arguably that was more important than the presentation.
Root cause:
I spent ages perfecting a presentation, which is the wrong thing to spend ages on because they're there to test my technical understanding. Yes, the ability to present is some of it, but it's a technical interview.
I should have just been clear on the objectives and priorities, up front, grabbed any old presentation, and focused on the technical learning. I completely screwed up the prioritization, my preparation, prioritization, and scheduling, and I should have time boxed activities.
The lesson learned is that I must scope everything. I need to define the objectives and key results for everything. Once I've done that, then I can prioritize, and then I can schedule, and then I can time box.
