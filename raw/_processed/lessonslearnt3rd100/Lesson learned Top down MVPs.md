## Situation

On many occasions, when I don't have a deadline and I've got something to do, I start going from the ground up, going off on tangents about studying best practises, etc. I end up stressing myself out because I know I'm going on these tangents and I'm getting further away from just doing what I set out to do in the first place. So I can feel the stress, but then I'm stuck and I just can't get out of it. That's got to be the ADHD hyper-focus.

## Root cause

Perfectionism, procrastination, ADHD hyper focus

## Lesson learned

When I have to produce something in two hours, I have to produce it in A, and I just do the top-down approach, which I'm extremely effective at. As soon as I start to think "I need to do it the best way" or whatever, that's when I go off on tangents. So, do good enough, top-down, gut not looking stuff up, just what I think, get it done, and then iterate.
