Situation: watching loads of YouTube videos over-analyzing and researching things that I plan on doing, rather than getting started and actually learning through doing.
Root cause: typically, because it seems like a daunting amount to do, I build up all of these barriers in my head to stop me actually getting started. I'm sort of learning when watching, but it's nothing compared to doing.  Once I actually get started with things, I often find that it is very different to do it than watching it, the areas that I need to research are roadblocks that are not related to the ones that I anticipated.
A lot of the things that I thought were going to be really problematic are easy, and I let them be mental barriers to getting me started.
Lesson learnt:
I have to learn to just dive in. Do research after I've dived in. Do minimal research, get started, then do research that's based on practical roadblocks rather than tongues.
