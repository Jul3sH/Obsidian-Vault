Situation: After spending 7 months not wanting to commit to any development or long-term goals because of being scared of making the wrong decision and wasting my time, I've faced a huge amount of frustration. Now that I'm just getting on with a few things, they may or may not be wrong, but I feel so much better about myself, I suspect they will lead to something rather than nothing.
Root cause: I get into decision-making paralysis when I don't have clear evidence that a goal is going to achieve what I want, then I end up doing nothing.
Lesson Learnt: I'm better off doing something but limiting the scope than pontificating and doing nothing, so it's as Nate B said,
"To thrive, professionals must adopt a "skill issue" mentality, **maintain a high "say-do ratio,"** and focus on pushing maximum value into a "malleable" world.
