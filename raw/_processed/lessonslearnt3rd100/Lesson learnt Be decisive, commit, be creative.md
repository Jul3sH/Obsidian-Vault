**Situation**: The situation is that I deliberated all over Christmas over whether to apply for Equinix. I was doing a really good job of convincing myself out of it and spending ages doing decision trees. Even though I decided to do it, I then convinced myself out of it anyway.
Then I had a conversation with Rob Hans and he said he'd spoken to Max and Max said, "Great guy." And I realised I should take a punt anyway. The second I made the decision to actually get on with it, immediately my brain started removing all the blocks, thinking how to address the gaps and I moved into do mode.
**Root cause**:  So I think the root cause of the problem is that maybe it's an ADHD thing but I'm very good at convincing myself out of taking risks and just taking risks as some sort of protection against failure and rejection. It's all to do with fear of rejection.
**lesson learnt**. The lesson learned is that I need to be bold, I have to make decisions and put a stake in the ground and then my brain will start thinking about how to achieve the result. I perhaps need to have other people, other authorities who are going to push me over the line and tell me to take the risk, otherwise I'll self-sabotage.
I'm incredibly creative and quick at connecting the dots. Once I've got a clear goal, I'm very, very good. Once I've got a clear goal, it's why I was so good on big deals. I'm terrible at putting a stake in the ground and deciding what to do.
